What the ±\pm±plus minus sign means

Note that every positive number has two square roots: a positive square root and a negative square root. For example, both 6666 and −6-6−6minus, 6, when squared, equal 36363636. Therefore, this equation has two solutions.

The ±\pm±plus minus is just an efficient way of representing this concept mathematically. For example, ±6\pm 6±6plus minus, 6 means "either 6666 or −6-6−6minus, 6".

A note about inverse operations

When we solved linear equations, we isolated the variable by using inverse operations: If the variable had 3333 added to it, we subtracted 3333 from both sides. If the variable was multiplied by 4444, we divided both sides by 4444.

The inverse operation of taking the square is taking the square root. However, unlike the other operations, when we take the square root we must remember to take both the positive and the negative square roots.
