29. Which of the following fractions is closest to 0? A. -5/12 B. -2/3 C. 5/6


Answer:

A. -5/12

Step-by-step explanation:

First make sure they all have a common denominator (12)

Then find the number closest to 0

:)

If you don't understand the closest number to 0 in the number line os the correct answer...

-2/3 = -8/12   -5/12        0                 5/6 = 10/12

------|---------------|-----------|---------------------|

Answer:

A

Step-by-step explanation:

Compare the three by getting the LCM(?)

Looking at the denominators, you would know firsthand that the least common multiple would be 12.

12 = 12, 24, 36, 48

3 = 3, 6, 9, 12

6 = 6, 12, 18, 24

Use 12 as their common denominator, divide it with the given denominator multiple by the numerator.

(since A shares the same denominator, it would also yield the same, -5/12)

Let's try B.- 2/3

12÷3=4

4x2= 8

Therefore, -2/3 is now -8/12.

Whereas, C. 5/6

12÷6=2

2x5=10

Therefore, 5/6 is now 10/12.

To visual the results, use the x axis. 0 would be the middle point. Left for negative, and right for positive integers or numbers. You will notice, by moving from -5, you only have to move 5 points or step, whereas -8 or even 10 is farther from the middle point.
