15. An illustration board measuring 5 feet by 4 feet needs to be cut into small squares measuring 4 inches by 4 inches. What is the greatest number of squares which can be cut from the illustration board? need answer pooo


Answer:

To solve the problem, we need to convert the measurements to a common unit. We can convert the measurements to inches since we are given the length and width of the squares in inches.

The length of the illustration board in inches is:

5 feet x 12 inches/foot = 60 inches

The width of the illustration board in inches is:

4 feet x 12 inches/foot = 48 inches

Next, we need to determine how many rows and columns of 4-inch squares can fit into the length and width of the illustration board.

For the length, we divide the length of the illustration board by the length of each square:

60 inches / 4 inches = 15

So, we can fit 15 rows of 4-inch squares into the length of the illustration board.

For the width, we divide the width of the illustration board by the width of each square:

48 inches / 4 inches = 12

So, we can fit 12 columns of 4-inch squares into the width of the illustration board.

To determine the total number of squares, we multiply the number of rows and columns:

15 rows x 12 columns = 180 squares

Therefore, the greatest number of 4-inch squares that can be cut from the illustration board is 180.

AREA

Area is the amount of square units inside the boundary of a flat, 2-dimensional object such as triangle, rectangle, square and circle. Area is the size of the surface.

Let me show you to get the area of the given problems above.

Problem #1

Area of Rectangle

The first problem is about finding the area of a rectangle.

Rectangle is a 4-sided flat shape with straight sides where all interior angles are right angles (90°). It has two lengths and two widths. Opposite sides are parallel and of equal length.

To find the area, we have the formula:

Area █ = l × w

Multiply l (length) and w (width).

Going back to the first problem. Here is the solution.

Given:

length - 16 inches

height - 9 inches

Solution:

Area █ = l × w

= 16 in × 9 in

= 144 in²

The area of the rectangle is 144 in².

Problem #2

Area of Trapezoid

The second problem didn't directly state the kind of polygon but based on the given dimensions, it is clearly an isosceles trapezoid.

A trapezoid is a 4-sided flat shape with straight sides that has a pair of opposite sides parallel. A kind of trapezoid is an isosceles trapezoid which has two congruent sides and equal angles from a parallel side.

To find the area of trapezoid, we have the formula:

Area = ((a+b) / 2) h

Get the average of the two base lengths times the height.

Going back to the second problem. Here is the solution.

Given:

lengths of the bases - 31 in and 16 in

two sides - 15 in

We dont have the height of the trapezoid. So, before proceeding in finding its area, we need to find its height first.

We know that in isosceles trapezoid, we can form triangles to both of its side. And that is what we are going to use to find the height.

Triangles' dimension inside the trapezoid is: base - 7.5 in, hypotenuse - 15 in.

Pythagorean Theorem (a²+b²=c²)

a² + b²= c²

7.5 in² + h² = 15 in²

56.25 in² + h² = 225 in²

h² = 225 in² - 56.25 in²

h² = 168.75 in²

√h² = √168.75 in²

h = 12.99 in

Height is 12.99 in.

Now, that we have the height, we can now solve for the area.

Area = ((a+b) / 2) h

= ((31 in + 16 in) / 2) 12.99 in

= (47 in / 2) 12.99 in

= 23.5 in(12.99 in)

= 305.265 in²

The area of the frapezoid is 305.265 in².

Problem #3

Area of Triangle

The third problem is about the area of two right triangles.

Triangle is a plane figure with three straight sides and three angles. Right triangle is a kind of triangle with one right angle.

To find the area of triangle, we have the formula:

Area △= ½ bh

We need to get the one-half of the base times the height.

Going back to the third problem. Here is the solution.

Given:

base - 33 in

(We have two triangles, each with a base of 16½ inches. 16½in + 16½in)

Solution:

= ½(33 in)(20 in)
