7. given side of a triangle 10 m 15 m 12 m what is the smallest angle​


Solution:

This problem can be solved by cosine law. The formula we will use to find the smallest angle is

A = Arccos [(b² + c² - a²) / 2bc]

where:

A = angle opposite to side a (smallest)

b = side of a triangle

c = side of a triangle (longest)

a = side opposite to angle A (shortest)

Note: Arccos is also the inverse cosine. To input the Arccos function in scientific calculator, first, press shift button, and then press cos button.

Listing the given values

a = 10 m

b = 12 m

c = 15 m

Solving for A

A = Arccos [(b² + c² - a²) / 2bc]

A = Arccos [(12² + 15² - 10²) / 2(12)(15)]

A = Arccos (269 / 360)

A = Arccos (0.7472)

A = 41.65°

Therefore, the smallest angle is 41.65°

19. in a room that measures 10 m by 12 m by 8 m what is the capacity of the room??​


➭ Length of the room = 10 m

➭ Width of the room = 12 m

➭ Height of the room = 8 m

➭ Capacity of the room = Volume of the room

➭ Volume = length x width x height

➭ Volume = 10 x 12 x 8

= 120 x 8

= 960 m³

Step-by-step explanation:

V=L×W×H

V=10×12×8

V=120×8
