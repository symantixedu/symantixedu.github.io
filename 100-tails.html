1. A coin is tossed 100 times. The tails appeared 25 times. What is the experimental probability of getting tails? ​


Step-by-step explanation:

If we take this question literally, then it would mean what is the probability of getting at least 1 head and at least 1 tail.

The only way that cannot happen is if the result is 100 heads (no tails) or 100 tails (no heads).

The chance of getting 100 heads, assuming it is a fair coin is [math]1/2^{100}[/math]

We can approximate that by recognisin that [math]2^{10}=1024[/math]. So let's approximate that as 1000 which is [math]10^{3}[/math].

Then the chance of getting 100 heads would be approximately [math]10^{-30}[/math].

The probability of getting 100 tails is the same, so the probability of getting neither outcome is about [math]1-2*10^{-30}[/math].
