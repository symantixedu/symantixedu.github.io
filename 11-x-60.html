To solve for the unknown values of x and y, we will use the properties of a 30°60°-90° right triangle:

In a 30°60°-90° triangle, the hypotenuse is twice the length of the shorter leg, and the longer leg is the square root of 3 times the length of the shorter leg.

y = 4 * Cos 30°

y = 4 * (sqrt(3)/2)

3. Tan 36° = Opposite / Adjacent = x / y = 2 / (2 * sqrt(3)) = 1 / sqrt(3)

5. Sec 38° = Hypotenuse / Adjacent = 4 / (2 * sqrt(3)) = 2 / sqrt(3)
