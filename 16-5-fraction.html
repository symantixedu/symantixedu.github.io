To add 5/18 and 16/18, we need to find a common denominator. The smallest common multiple of 18 and 18 is 18 itself, so we can write:

5/18 + 16/18 = (5 + 16)/18 = 21/18

However, 21/18 is an improper fraction, meaning the numerator is greater than the denominator. To express it as a mixed number, we can divide the numerator by the denominator:

21 ÷ 18 = 1 with a remainder of 3

28. 5.) adding fraction 4/8 and 4/16​


Answer:

Find the least common denominator or LCM of the two denominators:

LCM of 8 and 16 is 16

Next, find the equivalent fraction of both fractional numbers with denominator 16

For the 1st fraction, since 8 × 2 = 16,

4 /8  =  

4 × 2 /8 × 2  =  8 /16

Likewise, for the 2nd fraction, since 16 × 1 = 16,

4 /16  =  

4 × 1 /16 × 1  =  4 /16

Add the two like fractions:

8 /16  +  4/ 16  =  

8 + 4/ 16  =  12/ 16

12/16 simplified gives  3/ 4

So, 4/8 + 4/16 = 3/4
