Q: What is the atomic number of chlorine?

A: The atomic number of chlorine is 17.

Q: How many protons and electrons does chlorine have?

A: Chlorine has 17 protons and 17 electrons.

Q: How many neutrons does chlorine have?

A: The mass number of chlorine is 35.452, and the atomic number is 17. Therefore, the number of neutrons can be calculated as:

Number of neutrons = Mass number - Atomic number

= 35.452 - 17

= 18.452

So, chlorine has 18 neutrons.

Q: What is the atomic mass of neon?

A: The atomic mass of neon is 20.197.

Q: How many neutrons does calcium have?

A: The mass number of calcium is 40.078, and the atomic number is 20. Therefore, the number of neutrons can be calculated as:

Number of neutrons = Mass number - Atomic number

= 40.078 - 20

= 20.078

So, calcium has 20 neutrons.
