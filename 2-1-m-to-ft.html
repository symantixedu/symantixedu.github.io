To determine if the three given numbers can be the measures of the sides of a triangle, we need to check if the sum of the lengths of any two sides is greater than the length of the remaining side.

2 in, 1 in, 8 in - This set of numbers does not satisfy the condition because 2 + 1 < 8. Therefore, these numbers cannot be the measures of the sides of a triangle.

6 ft, 3 ft, 9 ft - This set of numbers does not satisfy the condition because 6 + 3 < 9. Therefore, these numbers cannot be the measures of the sides of a triangle.

7 m, 2 m, 7 m - This set of numbers satisfies the condition because 7 + 2 > 7, 2 + 7 > 7, and 7 + 7 > 2. Therefore, these numbers can be the measures of the sides of a triangle.

4 mm, 1 mm, 6 mm - This set of numbers satisfies the condition because 4 + 1 > 6, 1 + 6 > 4, and 4 + 6 > 1. Therefore, these numbers can be the measures of the sides of a triangle.

V = 8 m x 5 m x 6 m = 240 cubic m
