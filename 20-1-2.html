Step-by-step explanation:

1. Convert the mixed numbers into improper fractions.

  a. Multiply the denominator by the whole number and add the result to the numerator.

  b. Use this result as your numerator and copy the denominator.

       =  4 [tex]\frac{3}{20}[/tex] - 2[tex]\frac{1}{10}[/tex]

        =   [tex]\frac{83}{20}[/tex] - [tex]\frac{21}{10}[/tex]

2. Find the common multiple of both the denominators.

The common multiple of both the denominators of [tex]\frac{83}{20}[/tex] and [tex]\frac{21}{10}[/tex] is 20.

3. Convert the fractions as common denominators.

= [tex]\frac{83}{20}[/tex] - [tex]\frac{21}{10}[/tex]

= [tex]\frac{83}{20}[/tex] - [tex]\frac{42}{20}[/tex]

4. Solve.

= [tex]\frac{83}{20}[/tex] - [tex]\frac{42}{20}[/tex]

= [tex]\frac{41}{20}[/tex]

5. Convert the fraction as a mixed number.

   a. Divide the numerator by the denominator.

   b. Find the whole number.

   c. Make the remainder the new numerator and copy the denominator.
