8. the volume of an aquarium is 24 cubic meters. If it is 4 meters long and 2 meters wide, how high is it?​


Step-by-step explanation:

To find the height of the aquarium, we can use the formula for volume:

Volume = length x width x height

We know that the volume of the aquarium is 24 cubic meters, the length is 4 meters, and the width is 2 meters. Substituting these values into the formula, we get:

24 = 4 x 2 x height

Simplifying the right side of the equation, we get:

24 = 8 x height

Dividing both sides by 8, we get:

height = 3

Therefore, the height of the aquarium is 3 meters.

We can start by using the formula for the volume of a rectangular prism:

V = lwh

where V is the volume, l is the length, w is the width, and h is the height.

We are given that the volume of the aquarium is 24 cubic meters, the length is 4 meters, and the width is 2 meters. Substituting these values into the formula, we get:

24 = 4(2)h

Simplifying the right-hand side, we get:

24 = 8h

Dividing both sides by 8, we get:

h = 3

Therefore, the height of the aquarium is 3 meters.
