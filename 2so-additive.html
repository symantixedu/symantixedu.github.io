1. Calculate the molarity of a solution that contains 70.0 g of H 2SO 4 in 280. mL of solution.


Given:

mass of solute = 70.0 g

volume of solution = 280. mL = 0.280 L

solute: H₂SO₄

Required:

molarity

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of solute.

molar mass of solute = (1.008 g/mol × 2) + (32.07 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 4)

molar mass of solute = 98.086 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \dfrac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \dfrac{\text{70.0 g}}{\text{98.086 g/mol}}[/tex]

moles of solute = 0.714 mol

Step 3: Calculate the molarity of solution.

[tex]\text{molarity} = \dfrac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molarity} = \dfrac{\text{0.714 mol}}{\text{0.280 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity = 2.55 M}}[/tex]

Therefore, the molarity of the solution is 2.55 M.

The reversible reaction: 2so₂ + o₂ ⇄ 2so₃ has come to equilibrium in a vessel of specific volume at a given temperature. before the reaction began, the concentrations of the reactants were 0.060 mol/l of so₂ and 0.050 mol/l of o₂. after equilibrium is reached, the concentration of so₃ is 0.040 mol/l. what is the equilibrium concentration of o₂?


SOLUTION:

Summarize the concentrations of each species by using ICE table.

[tex]\begin{array}{lccccc} & 2\text{SO}_2 & + & \text{O}_2 & \rightleftharpoons & 2\text{SO}_3 \\ \text{Initial} \: (M): & \: \: \: \: \: \: 0.060 & & \: \: \: 0.050 & & 0 \\ \text{Change} \: (M): & -2(0.020) & & -0.020 & & \: +2(0.020) \\ \hline \text{Equilibrium} \: (M): & \: \: \: \: \: \: 0.020 & & \: \: \: 0.030 & & 0.040 \\ \end{array}[/tex]

Based on the ICE table, the equilibrium concentration of O₂ obtained is

[tex]\boxed{\text{0.030 mol/L}}[/tex]
