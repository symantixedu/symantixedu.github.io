28. Chin canned a number of quart jars and a number of pint jars of tomatoes from his garden. A pint is 16 ounces and a quart is 32 ounces. The number of pints he canned is represented by p, and the number of quarts he canned is represented by q. Which equation models the scenario if Chin canned a total of 1,280 ounces?


For this problem, you need to find your variables. These variables when changed, will also change the output 

(in this case, the total ounces of canned tomatoes).

We have 2 variables, which are the different size of the cans:

1. pint (16 ounces), total # of pint is represented as p.

2. quart (32 ounces), total # of quart is represented as q.


We already defined the variables, given the total ounces which is 1280, we can make the equation:


16p + 32q = 1280   >>> this is the answer :) 


*Why we cannot make a straight-forward equation of p + q = 1280 ? It's because your equation must be stated and consistent in units which is ounces. p and q are still not in ounces, so we need to multiply 16 and 32 respectively.

30. The chef has 32 ounces of red peppers and 8 ounces of green peppers how many pounds of peppers does he have altogether?


Answer:

To convert ounces to pounds, we need to divide the number of ounces by 16, since there are 16 ounces in 1 pound.

So, the chef has a total of (32 + 8) = 40 ounces of peppers.

To convert this to pounds, we divide by 16:

40 ounces ÷ 16 ounces/pound = 2.5 pounds

Therefore, the chef has a total of 2.5 pounds of peppers.
