13. A mixture of Barako coffee and Arabica coffee cost for Php 360 a kilogram. The barako coffee cost Php 320 a kilogram. The arabica coffee cost Php 420 a kilogram. How many kilograms of each are used in 12 kilograms of mixture?


Answer:

Barako = 7.2kg

Arabica = 4.8kg

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

x = barako coffee

y = arabica coffee

Php 320 = cost per kilogram of barako coffee

Php 420 = cost per kilogram of arabica coffee

12kg = total kilos of mixture

Php 360 = cost per mixture of coffee

Equation:

1st total mixture: [tex]x+y=12[/tex]

2nd total cost of mxture: [tex]320x+420y=360x12[/tex]

Solve by substitution:

1st equation

[tex]x+y=12[/tex]

[tex]y=12-x[/tex]

2nd equation: substitute the value of y using the first equation

[tex]320x+420y=360x12[/tex]

[tex]320x+420(12-x)=360x12[/tex]

[tex]320x+5040-420x=4320[/tex]

[tex]-100x=-720\\x=7.2[/tex] kg of Barako coffee

[tex]y=12-7.2\\y=4.8[/tex] kg of Arabica coffee

Checking:

[tex]320(7.2)+420(4.8)=360x12[/tex]

[tex]2304+2016=4320\\4320=4320[/tex]

[tex]\frac{7.2}{12} = .6\\.6 x 320 = 192\\\\\frac{4.8}{12} =.4\\.4 x 420= 168\\\\168+192=360[/tex]
