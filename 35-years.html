3. Jack is 35 years younger than Karen. frank is half of Jack's age. Jennifer is 17 years older than Frank. Jennifer is 35 years old, how old is Karen?​


Answer:

Let's use algebra to solve this problem.

Let's start by assigning variables to the ages of Karen and Jack. We'll let K be Karen's age, and since Jack is 35 years younger than Karen, we can say that J = K - 35.

Next, we're told that Frank is half of Jack's age, so F = 0.5J.

Finally, we're told that Jennifer is 17 years older than Frank and that Jennifer is 35 years old, so J = F + 17 and J = 35.

We can use the equation J = F + 17 to substitute for J in the equation J = K - 35:

35 = K - 35 + 17

Simplifying this equation, we get:

K = 53

Therefore, Karen is 53 years old

14. A=1,320,000 r=10% per year t=35 yearsFormula For exponential growth.​


EXPONENTIAL GROWTH

Answer: The initial population is 39, 861.

Explanation: This problem involves exponential growth. The problem goes this way: Find the initial population if the total population after 35 years at 10% growth rate is 1,320,000. We need to use the exponential growth formula which is [tex]A=A_{0}e^{rt}[/tex] where:

A is equal to the total population after time t [tex]A_{0}[/tex] is equal to the initial population e is equal to Euler's constant which is approximately equivalent to 2.72 r is equal to the rate of growth t is equal to the time

In the problem, here are the following given values that we need to list down:

A is equal to 1,320,000 r is equal to 10% t is equal to 35 years [tex]A_{0}[/tex] is the required value in the problem

We will substitute the values that we listed down above to solve for the initial population.

[tex]A=A_{0}e^{rt}\\1,320,000=A_{0}e^{(0.10)(35)}\\\frac{1,320,000}{e^{(0.10)(35)}} = A_{0}\\A_{0}=39,860.54612[/tex]

In the first line, we substitute all the values in the exponential growth formula. The rate which is 10% should be converted to its decimal equivalent which is 0.10. In the second line, we will divide both sides by  in order to isolate [tex]A_{0}[/tex].

Therefore, the result would be 39,860.54612 but we should round this up to consider the decimal part so this will become 39,861 as the total population.

Checking: To verify if our answer is correct, let us assume this time that we are looking for the value of the total population. Let us check if we can get a total population of 1,320,000 when the initial population is 39,861 at a growth rate of 10 percent for 35 years.

[tex]A=A_{0}e^{rt}\\A=39,861e^{(0.10)(35)}\\A=1,320,015.031[/tex]

So we indeed calculated for a total population of approximately 1,320,000. Hence, our answer is correct.

HOW DO WE SOLVE EXPONENTIAL GROWTH PROBLEMS

In order to master solving problems involving exponential growth, we need to put in minds the following tricks and tips that I will be enumerating in order to avoid solving for wrong results that can make all of your efforts wasted:

Memorize and familiarize the exponential growth formula.If the total population is greater than the initial population, the problem involves exponential growth but if the total population is less than the initial population, the problem involves exponential decay.Recall about converting percent to decimals and vice versa. The final answer for the initial population or total population should be always rounded up to whole numbers. Imagine if your computed answer showed 39,860.54612. You need to reconsider the 0.54612 as a one. So, 39,860 plus 1 is 39,861.
