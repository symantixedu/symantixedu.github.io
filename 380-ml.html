16. saline solution is an aqueous solution of 0.9% by mass of NaCl. a laboratory technician prepared a solution containing 3.6g of NaCl in 20mL of water, what approximate volume of water will need to be added to the concentration corresponds to that of saline solution?A.) 20 ml B.) 180 ml C.) 380 ml D.) 400 ml ​


SALINE SOLUTION

380 mL of water must be added so that the concentration is equal to that of the saline solution.

Therefore, the correct answer is C.) 380 ml

Dilution

This is an exercise that involves the concept of dilution, that is, reducing the concentration of a solution by adding water.

In this case, we have an initial solution that has 3.6 g of NaCl in 20 mL of water. We want to dilute it in such a way that its concentration is that of a saline solution that has 0.9% NaCl by mass, that is, 0.9 g in 100 g of water.

As the density of water is 1 g/mL, we have that 100 g corresponds to 100 mL, so we have that the initial solution has:

3.6 g of NaCl ------- 20 mL of water

x ------- 100 ml of water

x = 18.0%

So, we have to:

ci . Vi = Cf. vf

18.0%. 20 = 0.9%. vf

Vf = 400 mL

Thus, we must add the following volume of water:

400 - 20 = 380 mL

23. · Bliss has to drink 380 mL of milk every day. How many Liters of milk will she drink in a week?​


Bliss has to drink 380 mL of milk every day. So she will drink 2,66 L of milk every week.

How much milk Bliss drinks can be found by multiplying the amount of milk Bliss drinks per day by 7. The number 7 here means the time of 7 days in a week.

So 380 ml x 7 = 2660 ml.

2660 ML can be converted into liters by dividing 1000 into 2,66 liters. So the amount of milk that Bliss drinks for 1 week is 2,66 liters.

How many milliliters of 1 liter?

The volume of an object is expressed in various volume units such as liters, cubic meters, cubic feet, and so on.

In the international system, the unit of volume is the cubic meter (m3) .

Although the liter is not International system, this unit is very often used in everyday life. The liter unit is equivalent to one of the SI units, the cubic decimeter, where 1 liter = 1 cubic decimeter.

The liter unit is usually used to indicate the volume of liquids, solids, and gases.

The liter unit is very often used to express the volume of water.

Because water is a basic need for living things, it is not surprising that the unit used to express the volume of water is very well known in everyday life.
