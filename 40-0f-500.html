1. The F to C formula is (F − 32) × 5/9 = C. When we enter 130 for F in the formula, we get (130 − 32) × 5/9 = C.

To solve (130 − 32) × 5/9, we first subtract 32 from 130, then we multiply the difference by 5, and then finally we divide the product by 9 to get the answer. Here is the math to illustrate:

130 - 32 = 98

98 x 5 = 490

490 / 9 = 54.44444

Therefore, the answer to 130 F to C is 54.44444 which can be written as follows:

130 °F = 54.44444 °C

2. 75 C to F

C to F

When you are asking to convert 75 C to F, you are asking to convert 75 degrees Celsius to degrees Fahrenheit.

Here we will show you how to convert 75 C to F so you know how hot or cold 75 degrees Celsius is in Fahrenheit.

The C to F formula is (C × 9/5) + 32 = F. When we enter 75 for C in the formula, we get (75 × 9/5) + 32 = F.

To solve (75 × 9/5) + 32 = F, we first multiply 9 by 75, then we divide the product by 5, and then finally we add 32 to the quotient to get the answer. Here is the math to illustrate:

75 x 9 = 675

675 / 5 = 135

135 + 32 = 167

Therefore, the answer to 75 C to F is 167 which can be written as follows:

75 °C = 167 °F
