27. The area of rectangle is 40cm². What are the dimensions if the perimeter is 36cm?​


Problem:

The area of rectangle is 40cm². What are the dimensions if the perimeter is 36cm?

Given:Area = 40cm²Perimeter = 36cm

Formula:A = lwP = 2l + 2w

Solution:

The area of rectangular is length times width to solve the problem, should you have to remember the formula of area of rectangular.

A = lw

Diagram is particularly useful in a problem of this kind. A rectangle is four-sided figure in which the opposite sides are equal and the sides meet at right angles. Thus, for a rectangle we need to write the formula of perimeter.

P = 2l + 2w

In representation,

Let l be the length

Then, let w be the width

Equation 1:

lw = 40

Equation 2:

2l + 2w = 36

Solve the unknown in the equation.

Solve w in equation 2.

2l + 2w = 36l + w = 18w = 18 - l

Substitute the value of the width in the first equation and then solve for l.

lw = 40l(18 - l) = 4018l - l² = 400 = l² - 18l + 40(l - 20)(l + 2) = 0l - 20 = 0 or l + 2 = 0l = 20 or l = -2

20 cm is the length of the rectangle, -2 is called the extraneous solution since negative value for length is not permissible.

Use the computed value to the compute the other unknown or to compute the final answer.

Now, let us compute the width, you can choose either the first equation or the second equation.

lw = 40(20)w = 4020w/20 = 40/20w = 2

Answer:

∴ Therefore, the dimensions of the rectangle are length is 20cm and width is 2cm.
