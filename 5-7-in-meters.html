A triangle has sides with lengths of 5 meters, 5 meters, and 7 meters. Is it a right triangle?

[tex]\large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:}[/tex]

Not a right triangle.

[tex]\large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:}[/tex]

Use Pythagorean Theorem to check if it is a right triangle:

[tex]\boxed{\red{ \tt \: Formula:}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf \: a^{2} +b^{2} =c^{2}[/tex] ⇒ Pythagorean Theorem

[tex]\boxed{\green{ \tt \: Solve:}}[/tex]

Substitute a, b, and c into 5, 5, and 7

[tex]\sf \: a^{2}+b^{2} \overset{?}{=}c^{2}[/tex]

[tex]\sf \: 5^{2}+5^{2} \overset{?}{=}7^{2}[/tex]

[tex]\sf \: 25+25 \overset{?}{=}49[/tex]

        [tex]\sf \: 50\neq49[/tex]

The result is false so it's not a right triangle.

27. find the volume of rectangular solid 8 meters long,5 meters wide.and 7 meters high.,show the solution​


Answer:

.The solution is to

multiply 8×5×7

. That's the solution

to the question

Step-by-step explanation:

8×5×7=280

    Finding the volume of a rectangle is pretty simple, it's the same as finding the area of a square but instead of a 2-dimensional figure we're now talking about a 3-dimensional figure, the rectangle has 3 dimensions which also requires us to get all 3 measurements to get its volume.

    In the 3D figure we have length, width, and height in finding the volume of a triangle, To get the volume of the figure we just need to multiply the 3 sides of it.

    lenght = 8

    width = 5

    height = 7

    In this question, it states that we have 8 meters long, 5 meters wide, and 7 meters high.

    Now that we have our measurements let's multiply the three of them to get their volume. The equation to get the volume is V = L * W * H.

    Substitute the length, width, and height. And then multiply the following.

    V = (8)(5)(7)

    V = 280

    Our final answer is 280 meters³.
