11. Which is more concentrated, a solution containing 5 grams of salt in 10 grams of water or a solution containing 18 grams of salt in 90 grams of water? Show your calculations.


The solution containing 5g of salt in 10g of water has a higher concentration.

In computing percentage concentrations, the formula [weight of solute/ weight of solution]*100% may be used since the given values are expressed in grams.

Solution:
Problem no.1
Given: 5g of salt
           10g of water
using the formula:   [5g of salt/10g of water]*100%= 50%

Problem no.2
Given: 18g of salt
           90g of water
using the formula:  [18g of salt/90g of water]*100%= 20%m salt 1 = 5 gr
m water 1 = 10 gr
m solution 1 = m salt 1 + m water 1
m solution 1 = 5 gr + 10 gr = 15 gr

% salt 1 = (m salt 1 / m solution 1) × 100%
% salt 1 = (5 gr / 15 gr) × 100%
% salt 1 = 33,34%


m salt 2 = 18 gr
m water 2 = 90 gr
m solution 2 = m salt 2 + m water 2
m solution 2 = 18 gr + 90 gr = 108 gr

% salt 2 = (m salt 2 / m solution 2) × 100%
% salt 2 = (18 gr / 108 gr) × 100%
% salt 2 = 16,67%


Salt 1 is more concentrated
