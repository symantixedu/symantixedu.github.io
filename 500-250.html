28. Find the 10th term of the sequence 1000, -500, 250, -125,… ​


Answer:[tex]a_{10}= \boxed{-1.95312}[/tex]Step-by-step explanation:

The sequence is a geometric sequence.

To find the 10th term, we need to find its common ratio first.

Step 1: Finding the Ratio.

Use the formula: [tex]r= a_{2}\div a_{1}[/tex]

[tex]r= -500\div 1000\\r = -0.5[/tex]

Step 2: Finding the 10th Term.

Use the formula: [tex]a_{n}= a_{1}r^{n-1} \\[/tex]

[tex]a_{10}= 1000(-0.5^{10-1} )\\a_{10}= 1000(-0.5^{9} )\\a_{10}= 1000(-0.00195312 )\\a_{10}= -1.95312[/tex]

30. what is the 10th term of the sequence 1000, - 500, 250, - 125​


Answer:

aₙ=[tex]\frac{125}{64}[/tex]     or     aₙ=1.953125

Step-by-step explanation:

Problem:

What is the 10th term of the sequence?

Given:

1000, - 500, 250, - 125​

a₁=1000

n=10

n is equal to the position of the term which is 10.

r=a₂÷a₁

=-500/1000

=-1/2

a₁=1000

Formula:

[tex]a_{n} =a_{1} r^{n-1}[/tex]

Sol'n:

[tex]a_{n} =a_{1} r^{n-1}[/tex]

Substitute everything with the correct value and do P.E.M.D.A.S. properly.

[tex]a_{n} =1,000(1/2)^{10-1}[/tex]

[tex]a_{n} =1,000(1/2)^{9}[/tex]

[tex]a_{n} =1,000(1/512)[/tex]

[tex]a_{n} =125(1/64)[/tex]

[tex]a_{n} =\frac{125}{64}[/tex] or

[tex]a_{n} =1.953125[/tex]
