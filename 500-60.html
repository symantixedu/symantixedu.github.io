27. will there be more or fewer zeros in the product when you multiply 90 × 500 than when you multiply 60 × 500? explain​


Answer:

There are more zeros in 60 x 500

Step-by-step explanation:

Since you are finding the number of zeroes in each term, cancel out zeros as many as you can.

60 x 500 = 6 x 5

90 x 500 = 9 x 5

Now that we have canceled 3 zeros from both terms, multiply and get the result.

6 x 5 = 30

9 x 5 = 45

then, look if there is still a zero in the product, if there is, that number has the most number of zeros.

Rechecking:

60 x 500 = 30000 = 4 zeros

90 x 500 = 45000 = 3 zeros
