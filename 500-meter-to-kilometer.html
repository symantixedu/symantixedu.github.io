9. If alexcan run 10 kilometers in one hour, how many minutes would it take him to run 500 meters?


The problem asks for the time needed for Alex to cover a distance of 500 meters. The formula needed to compute for time is:
 [tex]time= \frac{distance}{speed} [/tex]
Since, we already have the distance covered which is 500 meters or 0.5 km, we only need to know how fast he can run or his speed.

Since he can run 10 kilometers in one hour, this tells us that his speed is 10 kph.

Applying these in the formula:
[tex]time= \frac{distance}{speed} [/tex]
[tex]= \frac{0.5km}{10 kph} = 0.05 hours [/tex]

0.05 of an hour can be converted to minutes by multiplying it by 60 minutes (since 1 hour equals 60 minutes)

0.05 hr × 60 minutes = 3 minutes.

Therefore, Alex can cover 500 meters in 3 minutes.

17. Math Problem A. Simplify the following Ratio 1. 75 centavos: 2 pesos 2. 500 meters: 2 kilometers ​


Answer:

1. 75 centavos : 2 pesos

First, convert 2 pesos to centavos. (1 Peso is 100 Centavos)

2 x 100 = 200 centavos

So now the ratio is 75 : 200.

Now we can simplify by dividing them by their GCF, 25.

75 ÷ 25 = 3

200 ÷ 25 = 8

So our simplified ratio is 3 : 8.

3 and 8 cannot be simplified, therefore this is our lowest term and final answer.

2. 500 meters : 2 kilometers

Again, convert. (1 km = 1000 meters)

2 km = 2000 km

So our ratio is now, 500 : 2000

Find the GCF, which is 500.

Divide the ratio by the GCF.

500 ÷ 500 = 1

2000 ÷ 500 = 4

So our simplified ratio is, 1 : 4.

Again, you can't simplify this even more, so this is our final answer.

Hope it helps. ^^
