19. for a solution containing 0.251 mol of naphthalene (a nonvolatile, nonionizing compound) in 250. g of liquid benzene? (Kbp = 2.53°C/m for benzene)


Answer:

The molality of a solution is the no. of moles of the solute dissolved in 1000g of the solvent.

No. of moles of napthalene dissolved in 250g of benzene = 0.251

No. of moles of napthalene dissolved in 1000g of benzene = (0.251 × 1000)/250 = 1.004

Therefore, molality (m) = 1.004

Ebullioscopic constant for benzene

(Kb) =2.53° C/molal

Experimentally, it is found that

ΔTb= Kb × m

Therefore, elevation of boiling point

(ΔTb) = Kb × m

= 2.53 × 1.004

= 2.54012

= 2.54°C (approximately)

The absolute difference between the mark and space frequencies is expressed as the peak frequency deviation divided by two.

Peak frequency deviation Δ f =  1 KHz

Minimum bandwidth = 2 (Δ f + Frec b)Minimum bandwidth = 2 (1 + 4)
