1. Original weight:60 kg,new weight:55 kg Decrease in weight:_______, percent decrease______​


DECREASE IN WEIGHT: 60 - 55 = 5kg

PERCENT DECREASE: to get the percent decrease you need to divide the decrease in weight to your original weight;

[tex] \frac{5}{60} = \frac{1}{12} [/tex]

CONVERT TO PERCENTAGE

[tex] \frac{1}{12} = 0.083333 \\ 0.083333 \times \frac{100}{100} = \frac{8.3333}{100} \\ = 8.3333\%[/tex]

14. a drum full of rice weights 24 1/6 kg. if the empty drum weights 55/4 kg. find the weight of the rice in the drum​


Problem:

A drum full of rice weights 24 1/6 kg. If the empty drum weights 55/4 kg. Find the weight of the rice in the drum.

Given:

Drum full of rice - 24 1/6 kilograms

Empty Drum - 55/4 kilograms

Rice - ?

Solution:

✓You need to subtract the weight of the drum with full of rice by the weight of the drum when it's empty to get the weight of the rice.

[tex]24 \frac{1}{6} - \frac{55}{4} \\ \frac{6(24) + 1}{6} - \frac{55}{4} \\ \frac{144 + 1}{6} - \frac{55}{4} \\ \frac{145}{6} - \frac{55}{4} \\ \frac{4(145) - 6(55)}{24} \\ \frac{580 - 330}{24} \\ \frac{250}{4} = 62.5[/tex]

✓Tranform the mixed fraction into improper fraction.

✓Proceed to subtraction after finding the least common denominator.

✓Simplify the result.

Answer:

The weight of the rice in the drum is 62.5 or 125/2 kilograms.
