[tex] {1}^{2} \times 100 \div 1 \times (4 + 6) - 56 + 100 = n \\ {1}^{2} \times 100 \div 1 \times 10 - 56 + 100 = n \\ 1 \times 100 \div 1 \times 10 - 56 + 100 = n \\ 100 \div 1 \times 10 - 56 + 100 = n \\ 100 \times 10 - 56 + 100 = n \\ 1000 - 56 + 100 = n \\ 944 + 100 = n \\ 1044 = n [/tex]

Answer:

1044

Step-by-step explanation:

Use the rule of PEMDAS

PEMDAS stands for

P= Parenthesis

E= Exponent

M= Multiplication

D= Division

A= Addition

S= Subtraction

The order of the PEMDAS gives you a hint which operation you will calculate first. (In Left to Right)

By the given expression,

1^2 × 100 ÷ 1 × (4+6) - 56 + 100= ?

By the rule of PEMDAS, calculate the expression in sequence starting from P= Parenthesis and so on.

1^2 × 100 ÷ 1 × (10) - 56 + 100  ( Parenthesis )

1 × 100 ÷ 1 × (10) - 56 + 100      ( Exponent )

1000 - 56 + 100                                                                                                (Division/ Multiplication depending on which operation                      comes first)

1000 - 56 + 100 = 1044 ( Same rule apply to Division/Multiplication )
