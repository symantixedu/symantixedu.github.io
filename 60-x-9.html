Answer/Step-by-step explanation:

To find the GCF of 20x² and 40x^9, we need to find the greatest common factor of the coefficients and the variables.

The prime factorization of 20 is 2² × 5, and the prime factorization of 40 is 2³ × 5. So the GCF of the coefficients is 2² × 5 = 20.

The variables have a common factor of x², so the GCF of the variables is x².

Therefore, the GCF of 20x² and 40x^9 is 20x².

To simplify (2x + x), we can combine like terms:

2x + x = 3x

Therefore, the simplified form of (2x + x) is 3x.
