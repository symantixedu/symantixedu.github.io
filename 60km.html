10. M/B Rosario travels 60km downstream and 60km back in 8 hours and 20 minutes. what is the rate of the current if the boat travels 15 kph?


Given:

Upstream Distance=60 km
Downstream Distance=60 km
Boat's Velocity=15 kph
Time(Upstream + Downstream)= 8 hrs. and 20 mins. or [tex] \frac{25}{3}[/tex] hrs.
River's Current= ?

Let (x) be the river's current.

Solution:

[tex] \frac{Upstream(d)}{Velocity+Current} + \frac{Downstream(d)}{Velocity-Current} =Time[/tex]
[tex] \frac{60}{15+x} + \frac{60}{15-x} = \frac{25}{3} [/tex]
[tex]60(3)(15-x)+60(3)(15+x)=25(15-x)(15+x)[/tex]
[tex]2700-180x+2700+180x=25(225- x^{2} )[/tex]
[tex]5400=5625-25 x^{2} [/tex]
[tex]-225=-25 x^{2} [/tex]
[tex]9= x^{2} [/tex]
[tex] \sqrt{9}= \sqrt{ x^{2} } [/tex]
x={3, -3}

Therefore, the rate of the current is 3 kph.

Displacement

The displacement of an object is the change in the position of the object. The displacement of an object gives the shortest distance between two locations.

Δx = 30 km west

Thus, the displacement of the car is 30 km west.

27. M/B Rosario travels 60km downstream and 60km back in 8 hours and 20 minutes. what is the rate of the current if the boat travels 15 kph? Please explain the solution.


Speed of the boat = 15 kph


Define x:

Let the speed of the current be x

Speed of the current = x kph


Upstream:

Distance = 60 km

Speed = (15 - x) kph

Time = Distance ÷ Speed

Time = 60 / (15 - x)


Downstream:

Distance = 60 km

Speed = (15 + x) kph

Time = Distance ÷ Speed

Time = 60 / (15 + x)


Solve x:

Given total time taken is 8 hours 20 mins

8 hours 20 min = 8 1/3 hour = 25/3 hour


Solve x:

60 / (15 - x) + 60 / (15 + x) = 25/3


Single fraction:

[ 60( 15 + x) + 60(15 - x) ] / (15 - x) (15 + x) = 25/3


Cross multiply:

3[ 60( 15 + x) + 60(15 - x)]   = 25 (15 - x) (15 + x)

3[ 900 + 60x + 900 - 60x ] = 25 {15² - x²)

3[ 1800] = 25 {225 - x²)

5400 = 5625 - 25x²

25x² = 225

x² = 9

x = √9

x = 3 kph


Answer: The rate of the current is 3 kph
