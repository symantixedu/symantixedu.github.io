29. What is the mass of a 50 kg person in pounds?​


Answer:

The answer is 110.231 pounds

Thanks

Answer:

First, it's important to distinguish between weight an mass. Kilograms are a measure of mass, or how much “stuff” there is in an object. If you are looking for the weight of an object, it's equal how much force an object is pulled down with.

50kg is a measure of mass, which means it is 50kg whether or not it is on the moon or on Earth (it has the same amount of “stuff” in it). What changes is how much it weighs/how much force it is being pulled down with.

On the surface of the Earth, the acceleration due to gravity is 9.81m/s^2. With Newton's Second Law, you can derive the equation F=ma where “F” is the force the object is pulled with/the weight of the object, “m” is the mass of an object and “a” is the acceleration of the object. So for a object with mass 50kg on the surface of the Earth, its weight is given by:

F=ma

F=(50kg)(9.81m/s^2)

F=490.5 N

The object weighs 490.5 N on Earth. If you were to take it to the moon, the mass would stay the same, but the weight of the object would change. You can use the same formula:

F=ma

F=(50kg)(1.62m/s^2)

F=80N

So, the object WEIGHS 1/6th of what it would on Earth but still has the same MASS. hope that helped!
