7. what is the volume of a pyramid that has a height of 12 m. its triangular base is 3m and with an atitude of 6m A.36cm³ B.63cm³ C.108cm³ D.180cm³​


Step-by-step explanation:

The volume of a pyramid is given by the formula: V = (1/3) * B * h, where B is the area of the base and h is the height of the pyramid. In this case, the base is a triangle with a base of 3m and an altitude of 6m. The area of this triangle is (1/2) * 3m * 6m = 9m². So, the volume of the pyramid is V = (1/3) * 9m² * 12m = 36m³. The correct answer is A. 36cm³.

18. a rectangle has a perimeter of 32 cm and an area of 63cm^2. Find its dimensions.


Perimeter: 2L + 2W = 32


Simplify this equation by dividing everything by 2: L+W = 16


Area: LW = 63


Solve the area equation for one of the variables: L = 63/W


Substitute this into the perimeter equation:


63/W +W =16


W is in the denominator so we want to multiply everything by W:


63 + W^2 = 16W


Rearrange into standard form, then solve by factoring or by using the quadratic formula:


W^2 - 16W + 63 = 0


(W-9)(W-7) = 0


W = 7 or 9


Since we generally think of length as being the longer side, we will use 7 for W, which makes L = 9.


This makes the dimensions 7 x 9
