8. 5.) If the length of the two sides of an isosceles triangle are 3 cm and 7 cm, then what must be the length of the third side? A. 3 cm B. 10 cm C. 14 cm D. 7 cm


Answer:

Let us first understand what is isoceles triangle :-

Isoceles triangle :- A triangle in which two sides are equal is called an isoceles triangle.

Now, here we are given that two sides are 3 cm and 7 cm and it is an isoceles triangle.

So, the third side should be either 3 or 7.

If the third side is 3, then the sides of the triangle will be 3 , 3 and 7.

Here, if we observe that sum of two sides i.e. 3 and 3 is less than the third side 7.

So, this triangle is not possible.

Now, if we take the third side as 7, then the sides of triangle will be 3 , 7 , 7.

Here, if we take sum of any two sides, it will be greater than the third side.

So, this triangle is possible.

Length of third side is 7 cm.

y = 10

AD = 13 cm

Perimeter = 52 cm

Step-by-step explanation:

One of the properties of a parallelogram is that it has congruent opposite sides. In parallelogram ABCD, AB≅CD and BC≅AD.

We can substitute the values and equate the opposite sides.

We have x = 6.

AB is 13 cm. Because AB = CD, CD is also 13 cm.

Now for the opposite sides...

We have y = 10.

AD is 13 cm. Because AD = BC, BC is also 13 cm.

The perimeter of a polygon is the sum of the measures of all its sides.

The perimeter of Parallelogram ABCD is 52 cm.
