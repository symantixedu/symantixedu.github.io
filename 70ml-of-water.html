9. 70ml of water was allowed to freeze in a 100ml container. What will the container look like after the water has frozen?


it has only 80ml because the water is cold if the water is cold the water magaamay

Answer:

When water freezes, it expands by about 9%. This means that the volume of the frozen water will be greater than the volume of the liquid water.

In this case, 70ml of water was allowed to freeze in a 100ml container. After the water has frozen, the volume of the ice will be approximately 0.09 x 70ml = 6.3ml greater than the volume of the liquid water.

Therefore, the container will be completely filled with ice and there will be no room left for any liquid water. The ice will take up the entire volume of the container, which is 100ml.
