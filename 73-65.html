8. What is the missing interior angle of a quadrilateral whose three angles are 73˚, 65˚, and 98˚?


Answer:

124°

Step-by-step explanation:

The sum of all interior angles of a quadrilateral is equal to 360°.

∠1 + ∠2 + ∠3  + ∠4 = 360°

where,

∠1 = 73°

∠2 = 65°

∠3 = 98°

∠4 = ?

solve for the 4th one, ∠4

∠1 + ∠2 + ∠3  + ∠4 = 360°

73°+ 65° + 98° + ∠4 = 360°

236° + ∠4 = 360°

∠4 = 360° - 236°

∠4 = 124°

So the missing angle is 124°

28. (kg) of 9 workers are 45, 65, 50, 73, 60, 70, 65, 75 and 62. Determine the 60th percentile, 70th percentile and 30th percentile.


To find the percentiles, we first need to arrange the data in order from smallest to largest value:

45, 50, 60, 62, 65, 65, 70, 73, 75

To find the values of the percentiles, we can use the following formula:

%ile = (P/100) x N

where %ile is the percentile we want to find, P is the percentage of the percentile, and N is the total number of data points.

For example, to find the 60th percentile:

%ile = (60/100) x 9

%ile = 5.4 or approximately 5.5 (rounding up to the nearest whole number)

So, the 60th percentile is the value that is fifth in the ordered list: 65.

Similarly, to find the 70th percentile:

%ile = (70/100) x 9

%ile = 6.3 or approximately 6.5 (rounding up to the nearest whole number)

So, the 70th percentile is the value that is sixth in the ordered list: 70.

Finally, to find the 30th percentile:

%ile = (30/100) x 9

%ile = 2.7 or approximately 2.5 (rounding down to the nearest whole number)

So, the 30th percentile is the value that is second in the ordered list: 50.
