1. A bond paper 11 inches has 8. 5 inches is to be used to make an open box. Squares of equal sides X are cut out of each corner then the sides are folded to make a box. Show how the volume (in terms of X) of the box must be computed.


Answer:

To compute the volume of the open box made from a bond paper 11 inches long and 8.5 inches wide, we need to consider the following:

The length and width of the bond paper after the squares of equal sides X are cut out of each corner.

The height of the box, which is equal to the length of the bond paper.

To determine the length and width of the bond paper after the squares are cut out, we need to subtract twice the size of the squares (2X) from the length and width of the bond paper. This gives us a length of (11 - 2X) inches and a width of (8.5 - 2X) inches.

The volume of the box can then be computed as follows:

Volume = (length - 2X) * (width - 2X) * height

= (11 - 2X) * (8.5 - 2X) * 11

= (11 - 2X) * (8.5 - 2X) * 11

= X^3 - 3X^2 + 22X

Therefore, the volume of the box can be computed in terms of X by using the formula above.
