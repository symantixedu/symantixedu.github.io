6. Two seconds after a stone was thrown upward, the speed was measured as 25m/s. What was the velocity of projection? How far was the stone away from the maximum attainable height? ​


The velocity of projection: 44.6 m/s

Stone distance from the maximum attainable height: 31.89 m

Further explanation  

The motion of the stone being thrown is included in uniformly accelerated motion with constant acceleration. Its acceleration is influenced by Earth's gravity of g (g = 9.8 m / s²)  

Equation used  

[tex]\rm \boxed{\bold{v=u+gt}}[/tex]

v = final velocity

u = initial velocity

g is negative if the motion goes up (vertical upward motion)  

g is positive if the motion goes down (free-fall motion)  

Two seconds after a stone was thrown upward, the speed was measured as 25m/s, so :

t = 2 s

v = 25 m/s

The velocity of projection (u) :

[tex]\rm 25=u-9.8\times 2\\\\25=u-19.6\\\\u=\boxed{\bold{44.6~m/s}}[/tex]

The height of the stone after 2 second :

[tex]\rm d=u.t-\dfrac{1}{2}gt^2\\\\d=44.6\times 2-\dfrac{1}{2}\times 9.8\times 2^2\\\\d=69.6~m[/tex]

The time taken to reach the highest point (maximum height) ⇒v=0

[tex]\rm v=u-gt\\\\0=44.6-9.8\times t\\\\44.6=9.8\times t\\\\t=4.55~s[/tex]

The maximum height :

[tex]\rm d=u t-\dfrac{1}{2}gt^2\\\\d=44.6\times 4.55-\dfrac{1}{2}\times 9.8\times 4.55^2\\\\d=101.488`m[/tex]

Stone distance from the highest point :

[tex]\rm =101.488-69.6\\\\=\boxed{\bold{31.89~m}}[/tex]

17. 41. A stone is thrown straightly upward with a speed of 60 ft/s from a tower 200ft high. Find the speed with which it strikes the ground.


Given:

v₀ = +60 ft/s

∆y = –200 ft

g = –32 ft/s²

Solution:

v² = v₀² + 2g∆y

v² = (+60 ft/s)² + 2(–32 ft/s²)(–200 ft)

v² = 3600 ft²/s² + 12800 ft²/s²

v² = 16400 ft²/s²

v = √(16400 ft²/s²)

v = 128.06 ft/s

Therefore, the speed with which the stone strikes the ground is at 128.06 ft/s.

To solve this problem, we can use the equations of motion for a freely falling object.

First, we can find the time it takes for the stone to reach its maximum height by using the equation:

v_f = v_i + at

where v_f = 0 (since the stone reaches its maximum height and stops momentarily), v_i = 60 ft/s, a = -32 ft/s^2 (acceleration due to gravity), and t is the time taken to reach maximum height.

Rearranging this equation to solve for t, we get:

t = (v_f - v_i) / a = (0 - 60) / -32 = 1.875 seconds

Next, we can find the maximum height reached by the stone using the equation:

y = v_i t + 0.5at^2

where y is the maximum height.

Substituting the values we know, we get:

y = 60 x 1.875 + 0.5 x -32 x (1.875)^2 = 225 ft

This means that the stone reaches a maximum height of 225 ft before falling back down to the ground.

To find the speed with which it strikes the ground, we can use the equation:

v_f = v_i + at

where v_i = 0 (since the stone is dropped from rest at the maximum height), a = -32 ft/s^2, and t is the time taken to fall from the maximum height to the ground.

Using the same equation, we can find t:

t = sqrt(2y / a) = sqrt(2 x 225 / 32) = 3.354 seconds

Finally, we can find the final velocity of the stone using the same equation:

v_f = v_i + at = 0 + (-32 x 3.354) = -107.328 ft/s

The negative sign indicates that the stone is moving downward, and the magnitude of the velocity is 107.328 ft/s. Therefore, the speed with which it strikes the ground is 107.328 ft/s.

24. a ball is thrown vertically upward with an initial speed of 80 ft/s. how long will it take for the ball to return to the thrower?​


Answer:

The time for the ball to return to the thrower's hand is 5 seconds.

Explanation:

In this problem, we will be using the concept of free-falling bodies. The formula that we are going to use is:

Vf = Vi - gt

where

Vf     is the final velocity, the given unit is in ft/s

Vi     is the initial velocity, the given unit is also in ft/s

g      is the acceleration due to gravity, the unit to use is 32 ft/s²

        since the given initial speed is in ft/s

t       is the time traveled by the ball

Solving the problem

Let us solve for the time for the ball to go up by using the given formula above.

Vf = Vi - gt

Then substitute the given data

0 = 80 ft/s - (32ft/s²)t

Take note that the final velocity (vertical velocity) at maximum height reached by the ball is zero.

Transpose (32ft/s²)t to the left side of the equation

(32ft/s²)t = 80 ft/s

Divide both sides by 32ft/s²

t = (80 ft/s) / (32ft/s²)

t = 2.5 seconds

Now, to compute the time for the ball to return to the thrower's hand, we will multiply 2.5  seconds by 2 because the time going up is the same time going down.

Therefore,

t = 2.5 seconds x 2

t = 5 seconds

Therefore, the time for the ball to return to the thrower's hand is 5 seconds.

28. A stone is thrown vertically upward with a speed of 30 m/s. a) What is the maximum height reached by the stone? b) How many seconds will it take for the stone to reach the ground? show complete solution.


Good Day

Problem:

A stone is thrown vertically upward with a speed of 30 m/s.

a) What is the maximum height reached by the stone?

                            Answer: height = 45.915 m

Given data:

Vi (initial velocity) = 30 m/s

Vf (final velocity) = 0 m/s (maximum height the stone stop)

a (acceleration) = -9.8 m/s²

d (distance) = ? unknown

Solution:

We need to solve for time first:

t = (Vf-Vi) / a

 = 0 - 30 m/s / - 9.8 m/s²

  = - 30 m/s / - 9.8 m/s²

t  = 3.061 seconds

Solving for distance:

d = (Vf+Vi)/2 × t

  = (0 m/s + 30 m/s)/2 × 3.061 s

  = 15 m/s × 3.061 s

d = 45.915 m

b) How many seconds will it take for the stone to reach the ground?

                         Answer:  6.122 seconds

The time it take the ball to reach the maximum height is 3.061 seconds and it would also take 3.061 seconds for the ball to return to its initial elevation. Which means it would take 6.122 seconds for the ball to reach the ground.

time going up = 3.061 s

time going down = 3.061 s

total time = unknown?

                 =  6.122 seconds
