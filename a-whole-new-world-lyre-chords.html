13. new values are describe as -- that have been shown to strike a chord with the media audience


New values are describe as -- that have been shown to strike a chord with the media audience.

News values are broad rules or criteria that influence how much weight a news article receives in a certain media organization.

They explain why a story piques the interest of its target audience, as well as how editors and other journalists judge which information is news and which isn't.

Proximity, Controversy, Personal Influence, Suitability, Impact, Bizarre, Human-Interest, Timeliness, Progress, Genuineness, Completeness, and Negativeness are the 12 news values in journalism that improve newsworthiness.

Aside from them, romance, criminality, and technology are other news values in journalism.

"Criteria that govern the selection and presentation of events as published news," according to news values.

These characteristics help to understand what qualifies something as "newsworthy."

News values are usually credited to Johan Galtung and Mari Holmboe Ruge, who coined the term "news factors."

Understanding this set of news values is the key to gaining those news placements: impact, timeliness, prominence, proximity, the weird, conflict, currency, and human interest.

These eight guiding elements determine the newsworthiness of a story.

19. it is a new method of extended chords,harmonies,whole tone,chromatic scale and pentatonic scale​


Answer:

The whole-tone scale has no leading tone and because all tones are the same distance apart, "no single tone stands out, [and] the scale creates a blurred, indistinct effect".[2] This effect is especially emphasised by the fact that triads built on such scale tones are all augmented triads. Indeed, all six tones of a whole-tone scale can be played simply with two augmented triads whose roots are a major second apart. Since they are symmetrical, whole-tone scales do not give a strong impression of the tonic or tonality.
