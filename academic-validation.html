5. recommendation about the effect of academic validation on students​


Answer:

Studies have shown that academic validation has a significant positive effect on students' psychological sense of well-being and their academic performance. Academic validation can help students feel valued and respected for their contributions, which can be especially important in environments where they may face discrimination or hostility.

Research has also suggested that academic validation can help to mediate the negative effects of a hostile climate on students' sense of belonging, self-esteem, and motivation to learn. Therefore, it is recommended that educators and institutions prioritize validating students' academic contributions and creating inclusive and welcoming learning environments to support students' academic success and well-being.

10. What are other academic disciplines which helped history becomes opened for possibility of valid historical sources which were not limited to written documents only? Provide example of their contributions. ​


Answer:

There are many academic disciplines that have expanded history by offering valid historical sources. History is not limited to text, after all. The following are good examples of such fields:

1. Geography

Geography is a field concerned with the physical features of the planet and the atmosphere, as well as the way they affect and are affected by human activity. Cartography falls under this study. Maps, globes, and charts serve as amazing historical references because they show territories, delineations, and boundaries even without the use of written documents. These tools also serve as a way to gauge the scientific prowess of a group of people in a specific time.

2. Theater studies or performance studies

It is interesting to see the part played by ritualistic performances and in history. These traditions, which have been passed on from one generation to the next, continue to reveal more things about the ancestors. Folk, indigenous, and ethnic theater performances depict the beliefs and social norms of a time long past. It is also fascinating to see how performances changed and developed under colonial rule. From the pag-anito of the Zambals to the Moro-Moro, the Philippines has a rich history of theater and performance.  

3. Literature

To be specific, I am talking about oral literature. This refers to literary works that do not have a singular written source. It is instead passed on from one person to the next. While this is a precarious source that might die out unless eventually documented in text, it is useful as a historical source. What makes it so special is that ordinary people can serve as the reference themselves. This is not the case for written history, which needs a lot of funding to be printed and published. The narration of the story or historical event is also effected by subsequent events, which gives us even more information. Biag ni Lam-ang, for example, is a folk epic from the Ilocos region. While it has already been recorded in text, it used to be narrated by mouth. It offered more insight into local customs and believes from several centuries ago.

11. local literature po about sa impact ng academic validation on students development, research lang po. ​


Answer:

One local study on the impact of academic validation on student development is "The Role of Academic Validation in Promoting Academic Engagement and Well-Being Among Filipino College Students" by C. R. Orbeta and L. J. Miranda.

The study, published in the Philippine Journal of Psychology in 2019, aimed to explore the relationship between academic validation and academic engagement, as well as its indirect effects on student well-being. Academic validation was defined as "the degree to which students perceive that their academic efforts, achievements, and identity are recognized and appreciated by significant others, such as parents, teachers, and peers."

The researchers conducted a survey among 410 Filipino college students and found that academic validation was positively associated with academic engagement, as well as with various aspects of well-being such as life satisfaction, positive affect, and self-esteem. Moreover, academic engagement partially mediated the relationship between academic validation and well-being.

The study highlights the importance of academic validation in promoting positive student outcomes and suggests that educators and parents should provide feedback and recognition to students' academic efforts and achievements.
