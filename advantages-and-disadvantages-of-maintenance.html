4. Advantages for disk maintenance


Disk maintenance allows you to perform tasks that would help you clean, utilize and monitors your disk performance and condition. If you are using Windows 10, you can perform disk maintenance using Disk Management Tool. Other tools available can be downloaded in the internet.

Listed below are advantages of disk maintenance:

1. Making you free more disk space - By applying disk maintenance in your system you can clean up disk space which makes room for more files to store. Compressing and deletion of old files can improve your disk performance and make your system perform better.

2. Monitors and detects bad sectors of your disk - Bad sectors are part of the hard disk that cannot obtain new data as it is defective. Bad sectors can either be repaired or not.  If you perform disk maintenance, you can be informed of how much space is included in the bad sector.

3. Improves security of your disk and system - Deletion of old files can make you delete malwares that are embedded with these files, thus, making your system perform more effectively.

15. What is the advantages of malware maintenance?


“Malware” is abbreviated from the word  “malicious software”. It is a general term covering all the different types of threats to your computer safety. Malware is designed to disable and damage computers without the user's knowledge or consent. Malware can impact your computer performance by disrupting the whole operations of your system.  If your computer is infiltrated by malware, you are at high risk of stealing sensitive information thus making you an easy target for fraud.

Advantages of Malware Maintenance

Most types of malware often go unnoticed so it is imperative to do malware maintenance from time to time. Some of the advantages of doing malware maintenance are as follows:

Detect issues early, before they become problemsPrevent against viruses and malwareSpeed up Your ComputerMaximize Software Efficiency

Types of Malware

Malware covers all types of threats in your system. To get the best understanding of their difference,  malware can be group into 3 types as follows:

A Contagious threat like viruses and worms where it is designed to spread without the user’s knowledgeMasked threats like trojans and rootkits conceal attacks on computers.  It could go unnoticed by antivirus or removal programs.Financial threats like spyware and keyloggers are used in malicious attacks like identity theft, phishing to steal money unknowingly.
