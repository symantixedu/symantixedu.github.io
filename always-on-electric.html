Answer:

SAVING ENERGY: WHY IS IT IMPORTANT?

By admin Posted March 24, 2017 In solar panels philippines  1 0

We are always told to turn off the television when we need to do something else; or the lights on other parts of the house when we’re not around; or simply turn everything off whenever we leave home. Not just it puts us into safety but turning them off means saving. Saving electricity and in turn – money. But there is a deeper sense in saving electricity; because we get the chance to CONSERVE.

What does it mean to conserve?

Conserve in its simplest term means “to preserve” or “to keep” something for it to last even longer. Electric shortage is rampant especially on warm seasons because people use more electricity to run air conditioners, and e-fans. Not all energy sources are renewable like solar, hydro, and wind energy does. Most energy come from coals, thus, the energy from this source cannot be recycled anymore. Moreover, coals can harm people through air pollution. Thus, saving energy does not just save the environment from coal-mining, but the people as well, from getting sick.

Less Use of Fossil Fuels (coal)

Electricity is not solely obtained from fossil fuels. Renewable energy sources like solar, wind, or hydro can also be used in homes and businesses. Fossil fuels as energy source involve the process of burning to produce electricity. The more electricity is consumed, the more fossil fuel burns, the quicker they will be depleted.

Less Pollution and Fewer Greenhouse Gases

Acid rains, soot, carbon dioxide, and toxic gases are just few of the poisons in the environment. The mere mining of coals can ruin marine and terrestrial ecosystems; Oil spill being the usual threat experienced by our environment.

Conserving Electricity at Home

There are many ways to conserve energy. Turning off appliances not in use is the simplest. Homeowners could also use energy saving bulbs and appliances, and insulate the home properly to lower the heating and cooling costs. Changing your energy source is also a big help. If your home has an ample amount of wind, you can convert it into a wind energy source, or hydro-electric source if you’re near a body of water. But if these aren’t available in your area, solar energy will be the perfect fit. You just need the sun’s light, some solar panels installed in your roofing, and voila! You can conserve energy and lessen your electric cost.

Conserving Electricity at the Office

(1) Use fluorescent lighting to replace any remaining incandescent lighting that you may have in your office. Compact florescent lamps (CFLs) cost less and last 10 times as long as incandescent lights. (2) For your light switches, add sensors to them as well as to the outdoor lighting to save electricity. These are helpful for restrooms and conference rooms that do not need much lighting. (3) Also, make it a habit to turn off office equipments each evening to reduce electrical consumption. (4) And since we are on the “techy” generation, you can try using an energy management app to monitor and check your energy provider and your office energy at the same time. But if you’re looking for a long-term energy saving solution, you can use (5) Solar panels to lower your electric bill. The installation may cost you a bit but the effect lasts until 30 years (depending on the quality of your panels). So, better check the manufacturer of the panels and its reputation to safeguard your business.
