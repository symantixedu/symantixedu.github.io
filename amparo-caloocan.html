17. ano Ang writ of Amparo ​


Answer:

The petition for a Writ of Amparo is a remedy available to any person whose right to life, liberty and security is violated or threatened with violation by an unlawful act or omission of a public official or employee, or of a private individual or entity, and covers extralegal killings and enforced disappearances

Ang petisyon para sa isang Writ of Amparo ay isang remedyo na magagamit ng sinumang tao na ang karapatan sa buhay, kalayaan at seguridad ay nilabag o binantaan ng paglabag ng isang labag sa batas na gawa o pagtanggal ng isang pampublikong opisyal o empleyado, o ng isang pribadong indibidwal o entity, at sumasaklaw sa mga extralegal na pagpatay at sapilitang pagkawala o pagbabanta nito

20. symbol of caloocan logo​


Answer:

The symbol refers to the famous monument of Andres Bonifacio and the revolutionaries who bravely fought for the freedom and rights of Filipinos during the Spanish colonization.

Explanation:

One of the more popular places in Caloocan is called Monumento. The name itself is an ode to the famous monument that is located right in the middle of the roundabout of the highway where vehicles coming from the northern provinces, from Valenzuela and Bulacan, and from the southern part of the Metro meet and intersect.

Behind the monument, we can also see the familiar colors and symbols of the Philippine flag. Blue for peace and justice, red for bravery and patriotism, and the division between the two colors as the equality of each tribe, region, and native of the country.

Also seen is the sun with its eight rays that stand for the eight provinces that stood against the Spaniards during the revolution. Instead of three yellow stars, the seal included 16 white stars.

30. I live in Caloocan City. Which is a proper noun in the sentence? A.I B.Live Caloocan City D.In​


A proper noun is something that SPECIFICALLY exists. The answer is Caloocan City.

C. Caloocan City

Explanation:

A proper noun is a specific (i.e., not generic) name for a particular person, place, or thing. Proper nouns are always capitalized in English, no matter where they fall in a sentence. Because they endow nouns with a specific name, they are also sometimes called proper names.
