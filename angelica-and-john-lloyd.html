Using a Control Chart to Monitor Forecasts

Control charts are crucial for system optimization and process improvement since they are used to track the results of a certain process. These statistical tools do have significant drawbacks, despite the fact that they are widely used in service and production situations.

MEMORANDUM

TO: John Lloyd Cruz

FROM: Alfon Alonzo

DATE: December 2, 2022

SUBJECT: Using a Control Chart to Monitor Forecasts

I am writing this letter to inform you on the qualities of using a control chart monitoring forecast.

A graph used to examine how a process evolves over time is the control chart. Data are plotted according to time. An average line in the middle, an upper line for the upper control limit, and a lower line for the lower control limit are always present on a control chart.

These curves were created using historical data. One of numerous graphical tools commonly used in quality control analysis to comprehend how a process evolves over time is the control chart.

A control chart's essential components are as follows:

a time series graph that displays data points gathered at particular intervals.a horizontal control line that makes it simpler to see trends and variations.horizontal lines that are positioned at equivalent heights above and below the control line to symbolize the upper and lower control boundaries. These upper and lower bounds are computed using the data plotted on the time series graph for a predetermined amount of time.

Control charts are beneficial for:

Recognize the variances that are present in processes at all times. Variations that fall within your control parameters show that the technique is effective. When a variation spikes outside of your control parameters, there are issues that need to be fixed.Watch for signs that something is wrong or could be wrong. These trouble indications alert you to the necessity for corrective action.

Signed by

Alfon Alonzo

21. John Lloyd borrowed Php 88,800 at an annual simple interest rate of 10 ¼ % for 18 months. How much interest should he pay?


Answer:

John Lloyd should pay Php 15,486.40 in interest.

Explain:

This can be calculated by multiplying the principal amount of Php 88,800 by the interest rate of 10.25%, and then multiplying that result by the number of months (18).

Answer:

Php 13,320

Step-by-step explanation:

To calculate the interest that John Lloyd should pay, we need to use the formula for simple interest:

I = Prt

where:

I = interest

P = principal (the amount borrowed)

r = interest rate (expressed as a decimal)

t = time (in years)

In this case:

P = 88,800

r = 10.25% (expressed as a decimal) = 0.1025

t = 18 months (converted to years) = 18/12 = 1.5 years

So, the interest is:

I = 88,800 x 0.1025 x 1.5 = 13,320

Therefore, John Lloyd should pay a total of Php 13,320 in interest.
