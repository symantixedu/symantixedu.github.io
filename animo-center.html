Which of these is the correct sequence of producing protein?

A. DNA strand - mRNA - tRNA - animo acid - protein.

The right sequence of producing protein is DNA strand - mRNA - tRNA - animo acid - protein. First transcription takes place in which DNA is copied to mRNA. The mRNA molecule can be translated to build protein molecule encoded by original gene. This flow of information is called Central dogma. So, the right option is A.

8. write the animo acid sequence that can be derived from this DNA sequence.​


Answer:

To determine the amino acid sequence that can be derived from a DNA sequence, the DNA sequence must first be transcribed into mRNA, which is then translated into a sequence of amino acids.

Here is an example of how to determine the amino acid sequence that can be derived from a DNA sequence:

DNA sequence: ATG TTT GTC GTT GTC CTT CTT GAC

Transcribe the DNA sequence into mRNA: AUG UUU GUC GUU GUC CUU CUU GAC

Translate the mRNA sequence into an amino acid sequence using the genetic code:

AUG = methionine

UUU = phenylalanine

GUC = valine

GUU = valine

GUC = valine

CUU = leucine

CUU = leucine

GAC = aspartic acid

Therefore, the amino acid sequence that can be derived from this DNA sequence is: Methionine-Phenylalanine-Valine-Valine-Valine-Leucine-Leucine-Aspartic acid.
