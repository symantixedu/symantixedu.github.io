3. normal lang ba ang irregular menstruation


Sagot: hindi

Explanasyon:

Ang isang normal na panregla ay tumatagal ng 28 araw, kasama o bawas ng pitong araw. Ang pagdurugo ng panregla ay itinuturing na irregular kung nangyayari ito nang mas madalas kaysa sa bawat 21 araw o tumatagal ng mas mahaba kaysa sa 8 araw. Ang mga hindi nakuha, maaga, o huli na panregla ay itinuturing din na mga palatandaan ng iregular na panregla.

Answer:

Hindi normal ang irregular menstruation

Explanation:

Maraming dahilan kung bakit nagiging irregular ang mestruation ng isang babae. Pero kadalasan sa mga dahilan ay stress at hormonal imbalance.

Kapag ang isang babae ay stressed sa pag-aaral o pagtratrabaho maaring maging irregular ang kanyang menstruation dahil sa pagkakaroon ng hormonal imbalance.

Ang hormonal imbalance ay delikado dahil kahit kaunting imbalance lamang ay malaki na ang epekto sa katawan ng isang tao.

Ang PCOS o polycystic ovarian syndrome, isang sakit na dulot ng hormonal imbalance ay nagreresulta sa pagkakaroon ng mas madaming male hormones. Dahil dito, nagkakaroon ng mga cyst ang isang babae sa kanyang ovary. Ito ay nakakaapekto sa menstruation at kadalasang dahilankaya hindi nagkakaanak ang isang babae. 8. Help pls lang about menstruation cycle​


Answer:

Menstrual phase (day 1-5)

The uterus sheds its inner lining of soft tissue and blood vessels which exits the body from the vagina in the form of menstrual fluid.

The follicular phase: The time between the first day of the period and ovulation. Estrogen rises as an egg prepares to be released. The proliferative phase: After the period, the uterine lining builds back up again. Ovulation: The release of the egg from the ovary, mid-cycle.

The average length of the follicular phase is 16 days . But it can last anywhere from 11 to 27 days depending on your cycle. The length of your follicular phase depends in part on the amount of time it takes one dominant follicle to emerge. When the follicle is slow to mature, this phase will last longer.

Ovulation is the release of a mature egg from the surface of the ovary. This usually occurs mid-cycle, around two weeks or so before menstruation starts. During the follicular phase, the developing follicle causes a rise in the level of oestrogen.

Ovulation happens at around day 14 if you have a 28-day cycle — right in the middle of your menstrual cycle. It lasts about 24 hours. After a day, the egg will die or dissolve if it isn't fertilized.

The luteal phase is one stage of your menstrual cycle. It occurs after ovulation (when your ovaries release an egg) and before your period starts. During this time, the lining of your uterus normally gets thicker to prepare for a possible pregnancy.

The luteal phase begins after ovulation. It lasts about 14 days (unless fertilization occurs) and ends just before a menstrual period. In this phase, the ruptured follicle closes after releasing the egg and forms a structure called a corpus luteum, which produces increasing quantities of progesterone.
