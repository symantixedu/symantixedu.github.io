18. A xylophone with 17 keys made of bamboo, separated by metal nails​


KULINTANG

We can read here the descriptions of Kulintang.

1) According to Maguindanao, kulintang refers to an entire set of gong kettles that are arranged horizontally on a rack to make a full set of kulintang called apasangan. - It's a bamboo xylophone with 17 keys that are separated by metal nails and are constructed of bamboo. Flourish-themed floral patterns are used to embellish the resonant case.

2) Kulintang is a phrase used to describe a musical style that consists of a row of small, horizontally arranged gongs that play melodically and are backed by bigger, hung gongs and drums.

It's the kulintang that's the most well-known, with seven or eight gongs in a row as melodic instruments, accompanied by three other gong kinds (one broad rimmed pair; one narrow rimmed pair; one with a turned-in rim) and a cylindrical drum.
