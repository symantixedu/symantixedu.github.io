22. "IMPERIALISM IN MALAYSIA"What are the conclusions or give some conclusions in imperializing malaysia​


Answer:Economic exploitation: One of the primary motivations for European imperialism was to gain access to the natural resources of colonized countries. In the case of Malaysia, European powers extracted valuable resources such as rubber and tin, which helped fuel their own economic growth. However, this economic exploitation often came at the expense of the local population, who were forced to work in mines and plantations for little pay.Cultural imperialism: Another consequence of imperialism is the imposition of European culture and values on the colonized population. In the case of Malaysia, European powers sought to undermine traditional Malay culture and replace it with European norms. This included promoting Christianity and European-style education, which often led to the erasure of local languages and traditions.Political domination: Imperialism also involved the subjugation of colonized populations under the rule of foreign powers. In the case of Malaysia, European powers established colonial governments and appointed local rulers who were subservient to their interests. This led to a loss of political autonomy and self-determination for Malaysians, who were forced to accept foreign rule.Resistance and struggle: Despite the challenges imposed by imperialism, Malaysians also resisted and struggled against European domination. This included armed uprisings against colonial rule, as well as social and cultural movements aimed at preserving traditional Malay culture and resisting European influence

Overall, the imperialization of Malaysia had profound effects on its people and society, both positive and negative. While European powers were able to extract valuable resources and establish political control over the country, they also imposed their culture and values on the local population, leading to a loss of cultural autonomy and self-determination. Nevertheless, Malaysians also resisted and struggled against imperialism, contributing to a rich history of resistance and struggle against foreign domination.
