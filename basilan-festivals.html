19. province of basilan reflection essay​


Answer:

Basilan is a province in the southern part of the Philippines that has long been marred by conflicts and violence. This image of instability and insecurity has put Basilan on the national spotlight, which has in turn led to negative stereotypes and heightened fear among the general public.

However, after visiting Basilan, I realized that there is more to this province than its notorious reputation. Basilan is a melting pot of different cultures, each with its unique way of life and traditions. I was amazed at how the locals practiced their customs with pride and joy, despite the odds that they face daily.

The province's natural beauty is also something to behold. From the unspoiled beaches to the towering mountains, Basilan has an abundance of pristine natural resources that are worthy of preservation and protection. It is evident that the locals have a deep sense of respect and love for their environment, which manifests in their sustainable practices of farming and fishing.

I was also struck by how the locals were genuinely welcoming and friendly. The hospitality and warmth that I experienced during my stay were unparalleled, and it made me realize that the negative reputation of Basilan did not reflect the character and values of its people.

However, I cannot overlook the fact that Basilan is still struggling with issues such as poverty, underdevelopment, and armed conflicts. These problems have hindered the province's progress and potential for growth, posing significant challenges for both the government and the people living there.

In conclusion, my experience in Basilan has been eye-opening, and it has taught me to look beyond the surface and understand the complexities of a place and its people. Despite its issues, Basilan has a rich cultural heritage, breathtaking natural scenery, and hospitable locals who deserve to have their stories heard and appreciated. As a visitor, I pledge to promote a better understanding of Basilan and contribute to initiatives that can empower its people and promote its development.

27. Yakans live in Basilan.TRUE OR FALSE​


Answer:

I think it's true

Explanation:

The Yakan people are among the major indigenous Filipino ethnolinguistic groups in the Sulu Archipelago. Also known as dream weaver having a significant number of followers of Islam, it is considered as one of the 13 Moro groups in the Philippines. The Yakans mainly reside in Basilan but are also in Zamboanga City. They speak a language known as Bissa Yakan, which has characteristics of both Sama-Bajau Sinama and Tausug (Jundam 1983: 7-8). It is written in the Malayan Arabic script, with adaptations to sounds not present in Arabic (Sherfan 1976).
