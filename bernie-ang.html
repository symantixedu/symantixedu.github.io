6. mrs bernie is preparing dinner plate she has 12 pieces of fried chicken and 16 pieces meatballs if she wants to make all the plates and denticle without any leftover what is the greatest number of played mrs bernie can prepare 83 b4c 28d 48​


Answer:

D. 48

Step-by-step explanation:

If Mrs. Bernie wants to make all the plates and denticle without leftover, You must find the LCM of it in which we skip count both numbers to find their least common multiple. Example is 2 and 3. we skip count both numbers like this:

2 - 2, 4, 6, 8, 10,...

3 - 3, 6, 9, 12,...

As you can see their LCM is 6. According to this question, the LCD of 12 and 16 must be 48.

12 - 12, 24, 36, 48, 60,...

16 - 16, 32, 48, 64,...

Answer:

Arnold: $7,400

Step-by-step explanation:

My bad! I didn't see that Cedric gets $18,500.

You can think of a ratio of 2:3:5 as 2/10, 3/10, and 5/10 respectively.

Since Cedric gets $18,500 from the equation 5/10 x n = 18,500, you can change the formula to get the total amount.

Therefore, n = 37,000.

Once you get the total, you can substitute the other formulas.

Arnold: 2/10 x 37,000 =  $7,400

Bernie: 3/10 x 37,000 = $11,100

$7,400 + $11,100 + $18,500 = $37,000

Therefore Arnold, Bernie, and Cedric earn $7,400, $11,100, and $18,500 respectively.
