3. 6. Something you buy based on your needs and wants is called. a. educationb. goodsc. bills d. service​


Something you buy based on your needs and wants is called: B. GOODS

Option A is incorrect. Because education is the learning of knowledge, skills, and habits of a group of people that are passed down from one generation to the next through teaching, training, or research.

Option C is incorrect. Because bill or invoice is a document containing details of the delivery of goods that records a list of goods, prices and other matters usually associated with billing for payments issued by the seller to the buyer.

Option D is incorrect. Because service is any activity that is intended or intended to provide satisfaction to customers.

EXPLANATIONS:

Goods refer to tangible products to fulfill our needs and wants. We can physically see it, touch it, and store it for future use. They can be consumer goods for final use or industrial goods such as raw materials, semi-finished goods and capital goods. Your various items such as clothes, food, drinks, computers, and smartphones are examples.

Another product category is services, which represent intangible products. They have no physical substance, we can only feel the benefits without being able to touch or see them.

Importance of goods

Goods are essential for our daily life. Without them, we cannot live. On the other hand, businesses produce them to make us more comfortable in meeting our needs and wants. By doing so, they make a profit. And, without business, we have to manufacture them ourselves.

Why are items important?

It can be seen from two sides: consumers and producers. Goods have an important function in our life. By consuming them, we can satisfy our needs and wants. Without them, we would return to primitive life, where we relied on nature to live. Imagine no clothes, canned food, soft drinks, computers or smartphones? What will happen to our life?

On the other hand, by producing goods, businesses make money. Their main motive is profit. They earn money when they succeed in satisfying consumer needs and wants. With that money, businesses can pay employee salaries, pay off debt, and pay taxes. The rest they distribute as dividends to shareholders and as retained earnings for internal capital.
