8. What happens if we mix Vinegar with Soy sauce. What happens if we mix Vinegar with Oil. 20 points


Answer:

Q1 .) An amount of vinegar mixed with soy sauce is called as a homogeneous mixture because the the two substances are completely miscible and get mixed up and particle level .

Q2.) The mixing of oil and vinegar produces a temporary mixture that will eventually separate into two layers. This occurs because these two liquids are immiscible, meaning it is impossible for these two substances to be mixed into a permanent emulsion. Oil is less dense than vinegar.

Step-by-step explanation:

Answer:

What happens if we mix vinegar and soy sauce?

Both substances will mix evenly and you can no longer identify each of them. It also has a uniform solution. This is an example of a homogeneous mixture.

What happens if we mix vinegar and oil?

The oil will eventually float on top of the vinegar since this substance don't mix with vinegar. It has a non-uniform solution. This is an example of a heterogeneous mixture.
