1. type of bond in ammonia


Ammonia, NH. Hydrogen atoms can each formone covalent bond, while and nitrogen atoms can each form three covalent bonds. Three pairs of electrons are shared in an ammonia molecule (NH3).

Formation of Ammonia ion (NH4)

In ammonia, there are three single N-H bonds and a lone pair on the nitrogen atom. When it reacts with H+ ion, the lone pair is used to form a coordinate bond between the nitrogen atom and hydrogen ion. Thus ammonium ion is formed.

The electrons of nitrogen have been denoted by dot and those of hydrogen atoms by the cross. So by definition, there are three covalent N-H bonds and one N → H coordinate bond in NH4+ ion.

14. Task I : Show how the sharing of elections form covalent bond in the following compounds! A. Ammonia ( NH3) B. Water (H2O)


Answer:

A. Ammonia (NH3)

In ammonia (NH3), the bond is formed by the sharing of electrons between the nitrogen atom (N) and the hydrogen atoms (H). Nitrogen has five valence electrons and requires three more electrons to achieve a stable electron configuration. The hydrogen atoms, on the other hand, have only one valence electron each and require one more electron to achieve a stable electron configuration.

To form the covalent bond in ammonia, the nitrogen atom shares its three unpaired valence electrons with the hydrogen atoms. The resulting compound has a total of eight valence electrons, with two coming from the nitrogen atom and one coming from each hydrogen atom.

B. Water (H2O)

In water (H2O), the bond is formed by the sharing of electrons between the oxygen atom (O) and the hydrogen atoms (H). Oxygen has six valence electrons and requires two more electrons to achieve a stable electron configuration. The hydrogen atoms, on the other hand, have only one valence electron each and require one more electron to achieve a stable electron configuration.

To form the covalent bond in water, the oxygen atom shares its two unpaired valence electrons with the hydrogen atoms. The resulting compound has a total of eight valence electrons, with four coming from the oxygen atom and one coming from each hydrogen atom.
