SITUATIONS

1. Situation: Car travelling on a steep road

Gravity: When a car goes uphill, gravity pulls on different direction. Therefore, the absence of gravity will make the car. Cars still work even without gravity, but it will affect the speed. Example if a car is driving uphill, with gravity, it is slower, but without gravity it will speed up.

Friction: The car tends to go to a straight line. Which means that curbing or zigzag roads are difficult without friction.

2. Situation: Boy riding a skateboard

Gravity: Gravity affects the scattered weight of the skate boarder. Without gravity, it will be difficult to balance it.

Friction: The same as the car. The boy can only skate in straight lanes.

Gravity: Biking will be lighter when moving uphill. When bicycles go uphill, the gravity pulls in an opposite direction. It will be lighter when cycling up.

Friction: Considering this if there may be no friction, no air resistance or another pressure then the frame might be stay in motion.

4. Situation: Washing dishes

Gravity: Gravity pulls denser air and water downward, forcing much less dense air and water to transport upward. So when you are turning on your faucet, the water will go up instead of down the basin.

Friction: The slippery movement of the cleaning soap decreases friction an excessive amount of so the pad with its very difficult edges enables upload returned a few friction to the system. Without friction, it will be difficult to remove hard greases.

5. Situation: Painting the wall

Gravity: The paint in the can will float. If you try to paint a wall in  a world with no gravity, It will not stick properly in the brush.

Friction: Friction stops matters from sliding apart. If there has been no friction the whole thing could slide to the bottom point. It could be not possible to climb up anything.
