4. why do we need the career assistance in Students with personal, social and career problems


Career assistance is important for students who are facing personal, social, and career problems because it can provide them with guidance and support in making informed decisions about their future careers. Students who are struggling with personal and social issues may not have a clear idea of what they want to do with their lives, and may not have access to the resources they need to make informed decisions. Career assistance programs can help students identify their strengths and interests, explore different career options, and develop a plan to achieve their goals. Additionally, these programs can provide students with access to job search resources, such as resume writing and interview skills workshops, which can be critical in today's competitive job market. Ultimately, career assistance can help students overcome personal and social challenges, build confidence and self-esteem, and create a path to a fulfilling and successful career.

30. 2.How do gender stereotypes influence the career choices of the students? 3.What are the factors that shape the gender stereotypes of the students in relation to future career choices?4.What strategies can be developed to mitigate the influence of gender stereotypes on the career choices of the students?5.How do gender stereotypes influence the future career choices of students as perceived by the students?​


2. Gender stereotypes can influence the career choices of students by shaping their perceptions of what is considered appropriate or desirable for their gender.

3. The factors that shape the gender stereotypes of students in relation to future career choices are diverse and complex. Some of these factors include cultural norms, media representation, family expectations, peer influence, and educational environment. For example, if a student grows up in a family or community where traditional gender roles are emphasized, they may be more likely to adopt gender stereotypes in their own career choices. Similarly, if the media portrays certain careers as gender-specific, this can reinforce gender stereotypes among students.

4. Strategies can be developed to mitigate the influence of gender stereotypes on the career choices of students. One approach is to provide students with exposure to a wide range of career options and role models that challenge gender stereotypes. This can be achieved through career counseling, mentoring programs, and educational initiatives that promote gender equality. Additionally, teachers and parents can help to create an inclusive and supportive environment that encourages students to pursue their interests and passions regardless of gender stereotypes.

5. Gender stereotypes can influence the future career choices of students as perceived by the students themselves.
