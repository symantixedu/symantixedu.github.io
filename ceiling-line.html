Price Ceiling

The maximum price at which products and services may be offered is known as a price ceiling, sometimes known as a price cap. The maximum price that can be charged for something is a form of price control. When it appears that prices are overly high or rising out of control, it is frequently set by government authorities to assist consumers.

Represents the orange juice market. the horizontal line at $2 shows a price ceiling imposed by the government, so the price ceiling the shortage equals 400 units. (TRUE STATEMENT)

Despite being referred to as the highest price, it is actually set below equilibrium in order to be practical.Price tends to rise because the quantity required by the market is greater than the quantity supplied (a shortage) and because the price is below equilibrium.Prices cannot rise above the price ceiling, even if just to reach equilibrium, because the government fixes prices below equilibrium.In contrast, the pricing strategy is ineffective if the price is higher than the equilibrium.Because the quantity sought is lower than the quantity provided when the market is above equilibrium, there is an excess supply.As a result, the price will be driven toward equilibrium by the market mechanism. Price would therefore be a waste strategy.

Additional information:

To safeguard consumers from inflated costs, particularly in times of severe inflation or due to monopolies, price caps are created. Basic electricity rates, fuel prices, and other price ceilings are some examples.

11. Exhibit 4-2 represents the orange juice market. the horizontal line represents a price ceiling imposed by the government. the quantity demanded at the price ceiling will equal the quantity supplied.


Exhibit 4-2 represents the orange juice market. the horizontal line at $2 shows a price ceiling imposed by the government :

The quantity supplied at the price ceiling will equal the quantity exchanged.

Description of the price ceiling

Even though it is called the highest price, in order to be effective, the price is set below equilibrium. Because the price is below equilibrium, the quantity demanded by the market is higher than the quantity supplied (shortage), so there is a tendency for prices to rise. However, because the government sets prices below equilibrium, prices cannot rise beyond the price ceiling, even if only to reach equilibrium.

Conversely, if the price is above the equilibrium, the pricing policy is not effective. The reason is, when above equilibrium, the market experiences excess supply because the quantity demanded is less than the quantity supplied. Thus, the market mechanism will push the price down towards equilibrium. So, pricing would be a waste policy.

Pricing objective

The purpose of setting price ceilings is to protect consumers from unreasonable prices, especially during high inflation or because of monopolies. Examples of setting ceiling prices include setting basic electricity rates, fuel prices, and so on.
