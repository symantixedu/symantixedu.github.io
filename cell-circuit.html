20. which circuit draws the smallest current from the cell, assuming both cells are identical?​


Assuming that both circuits are identical and have the same components, the circuit with the highest resistance will draw the smallest current from the cell. This is because according to Ohm's Law, the current (I) through a circuit is inversely proportional to its resistance (R), given a constant voltage (V).

I = V/R

So, if the resistance of the circuit is high, the current drawn from the cell will be low. On the other hand, if the resistance of the circuit is low, the current drawn from the cell will be high. Therefore, the circuit with the highest resistance will draw the smallest current from the cell, assuming both cells are identical.

Answer:

Circuit with a resistor, or it could be an open circuit.

Explanation:

The circuit that draws the smallest current from the cell is the one with the highest resistance. This could be a circuit with a resistor, or it could be an open circuit.
