Answer:

1.Female priests

There is no conceivable reason why the church has resisted the ordination of female priests. Most other religions have already begun to accept women. It is shameful that women are not treated equally.

2.Celibacy

Frank Bruni, op-ed writer for the New York Times, wrote a story about celibacy recently, suggesting that it may be too much to ask a man to abstain his entire life, and so the rules requiring celibacy among priests should be reconsidered. By mandating celibacy, the church may be, in effect, encouraging aberrant behavior such as pedophilia and physical contact of priests with men and women.

Catholics are not permitted to use birth control under any circumstances. This is one of the most backward and harmful restrictions in religious canon. Why wouldn’t the church allow safe and effective methods to control the size of families (and population)? Even worse, why would the church object to birth control in the prevention of disease including AIDS and STDs.

21. why does the evolution of technology become a challenge in the church today?​


Answer:

The evolution of technology presents several challenges for the church today. Here are a few reasons why:

Change in communication: Technology has changed the way people communicate with each other. It has made communication faster, more accessible, and more convenient. This has led to a shift in the way people interact with each other, which can be challenging for the church to adapt to. For example, people may prefer to attend online church services instead of in-person services, and this can be a challenge for churches that are not equipped to offer this option.

Online distractions: With the rise of technology, people are now more distracted than ever. Social media and other online platforms can be a distraction from attending church, and it can be challenging for churches to keep people engaged.

Theological challenges: As technology evolves, it raises new ethical and theological questions that the church needs to address. For example, the use of artificial intelligence raises questions about the nature of humanity and our relationship with technology. The church needs to address these questions to stay relevant and provide guidance to its followers.

Cultural shifts: Technology has brought about significant cultural shifts that the church needs to navigate. For example, the rise of individualism and consumerism can be at odds with traditional Christian values. Churches need to find ways to communicate their message in a way that resonates with modern culture.

Overall, the evolution of technology presents several challenges for the church, but it also presents opportunities. Churches can use technology to reach a wider audience and engage with people in new ways. To do this, they need to be willing to adapt and embrace new technologies while staying true to their core values and message.
