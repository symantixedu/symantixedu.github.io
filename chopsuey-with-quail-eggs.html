11. Compute the amount of feeds consumed by 10,000 quail layers for the month of February, 2021 with an egg production of 85%. Assume feed consumption of 22 grams/quail/day.


To compute the amount of feeds consumed by 10,000 quail layers for the month of February 2021, we need to know the number of days in February.

Assuming that February 2021 has 28 days, the total number of feeds consumed by 10,000 quail layers is:

Feed consumption per day per quail = 22 grams

Number of quails = 10,000

Total feed consumption per day = 22 grams/quail x 10,000 quails = 220,000 grams or 220 kilograms

Total feed consumption for February = 220 kg/day x 28 days = 6,160 kg

Since the egg production rate is 85%, we can assume that each quail layer produced 0.85 eggs per day. Therefore, the total number of eggs produced by 10,000 quail layers for February 2021 is:

Total number of quail eggs per day = 10,000 quails x 0.85 eggs/quail/day = 8,500 eggs per day

Total number of quail eggs for February = 8,500 eggs/day x 28 days = 238,000 eggs

Therefore, 10,000 quail layers consumed 6,160 kg of feeds and produced 238,000 eggs in February 2021.

26. identify tools and equipment needed in preparing orange quail egg​


Answer:

Ingredients

15 pieces quail eggs

4 cups water to boil eggs

1½ cup flour

¼ cup cornstarch

1 cup annatto water

½ teaspoon salt

¼ teaspoon ground black pepper

1 teaspoon garlic powder

2 cups cooking oil

Explanation:

Instructions

1 Pour 4 cups water on a pot. Let boil.

2 Add the quail eggs and boil for 3 to 4 minutes. Discard water and let the eggs cool down. Peel the shell off and then set aside.

3 Meanwhile, make the batter by combining flour, cornstarch, garlic powder, ground black pepper, and salt. Mix well using a wire whisk.

4 Pour annatto water in the mixture. Stir until all the ingredients are well blended.

5 Submerge the boiled eggs to the mixture one-by-one.

6 Heat the oil in a cooking pot. Once the oil gets hot, scoop a piece of egg from the bowl along with some orange mixture using a spoon. Gently slide the egg and mixture into the hot oil. Let the excess mixture drip on the egg. Deep fry in medium heat until texture of the orange coating gets a bit crispy (around 1 to 2 minutes). Remove from the pot using a kitchen strainer and place in a plate lined with paper towel. Perform this step until all the eggs are cooked.

7 Serve with a spicy vinegar dip.

28. quail egg description


Answer:

Quail eggs are rapidly gaining traction in cooking as a tiny and cute alternative to chicken eggs. They taste remarkably like chicken eggs but are small — typically just one-third the size of a standard chicken egg. They have cream-colored shells with brown splotches and deep-yellow yolks.
