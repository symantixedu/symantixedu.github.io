2. Christ is the supreme norm of Christian moral life. Question 3 options: True False​


Answer:

True.

The Catholic Church teaches that Christ is the supreme norm of Christian moral life, as He is the perfect example of how to live a life in accordance with God's will. The Catechism of the Catholic Church states that "Jesus Christ, the Son of God made man, is the Father's one, perfect and unsurpassable Word. In him he has said everything; there will be no other word than this one" (CCC 65). Therefore, Christians are called to model their lives after Christ and His teachings.
