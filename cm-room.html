1. 1. The scale of the plan of a house is 2 cm to 3 m. If the room is 7 cm by 6 cm in the plan, what is the actual area of the room?​


Step-by-step explanation:

The scale of the plan is 2 cm to 3 m, which means that for every 2 cm on the plan, there is 3 m in real life. To find the actual size of the room, we need to convert the size of the room on the plan to the corresponding size in real life.

The room on the plan is 7 cm by 6 cm, so the actual size of the room is:

7 cm * 3 m/2 cm = 10.5 m * 3 m/2 cm = 15.75 m * 3 m/2 cm = 23.625 m

So the actual area of the room is 23.625 m * 23.625 m = 557.15625 m^2.

18. How many 30cm by 30cm square tiles are needed to cover the floor a room that is 183 cm long and 152 cm wide​


Answer:

31 tiles

Step-by-step explanation:

Since the room is rectangular, we can determine the quantity of tiles needed by dividing the width and height of the room with the width and height of the tiles. From this, we can multiply the two values and get the number of tiles required to fill the room.

# of tiles needed for length: [tex]183/30=6.1[/tex]

# of tiles needed for width: [tex]152/30=5.066...[/tex]

# of tiles for entire room: [tex]6.1*5.066... =30.9066...[/tex]

Approximately, we would need 30.9066... tiles but that would be quite impractical to consider, so we get the next highest integer. Therefore, we would need 31 tiles. However, whoever has to assemble it in the room must adjust to cut the last tile in order to ensure proper coverage.
