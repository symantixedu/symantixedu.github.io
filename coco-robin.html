Hello!

Robin can clean 72 rooms in 6 days. How many rooms can Robin clean in 9 days?

We have a proportional magnitude (directly proportional), if we increase the number of days for cleaning, obviously we will have more rooms cleaned, then:

clean rooms __________ days

72 ----------------------------------- 6

y ------------------------------------- 9

[tex]\dfrac{72}{y} = \dfrac{6}{9}[/tex]

multiply the means by the extremes

[tex]6*y = 72*9[/tex]

[tex]6\:y = 648[/tex]

[tex]y = \dfrac{648}{6}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\boxed{y = 108}}\Longleftarrow(clean\:rooms)\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]
