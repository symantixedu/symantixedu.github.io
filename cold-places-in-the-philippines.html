4. 1. Why are some places on Earth cold while other places are warm? 2. What are the factors that affect the seasons in the Philippines? 3. How would you compare the two seasons in the Philippines?


Answer:

1. Why are some places on Earth cold while other places are warm?

The tropics consistently get more sun than either of the poles. They have a consistent amount of light and warmth around the equator, while the poles receive little light in winter, and long days in summer, giving them distinct warm and cold seasons.

2. What are the factors that affect the seasons in the Philippines?

Pressure zones. There are two major factors affecting the seasonal change globally: the High Pressure Area (HPA), which  originates from colder regions, and the Low Pressure Area (LPA), which propagates mainly in warmer regions.  Those gigantic pressure zones in the Philippines  are crucial to determining our tropical climate.  We have just recently witnessed the development of several tropical Pacific  LPAs, and the advance of the Intertropical Convergence Zone ( ITCZ). This pushes local thunderstorm build-up, bringing  rain showers and occasional flooding in areas.

Monsoon. The monsoon is a seasonal cycle of rain and  wind that occurs, among other areas, across South Asia.  The wind system carries heat from more tropical areas, driving the distribution of rainfall over  soil, which in turn controls temperature and returns the greenery to large tracts of land.

Tropical cyclone. The annual cycle of tropical cyclone formation drives moonsoon rains stronger  along the western shores of the Philippines, based on recent studies.  With the advance of the Southwest Monsoon (Habagat) during mid-June through late August, the prevalence of tropical cyclones we call "bagyo" will certainly guarantee a steady stream of  warm and most air masses — thus the incessant build-up of rain-bearing clouds toward the West Philippine Sea, and the all-too-familiar heavy flooding we have come to dread.

3. How would you compare the two seasons in the Philippines?

The two seasons in the Philippines are the rainy/wet season and the dry season. The rainy season happens from May to October, and the dry season can be subdivided based on temperature - cold dry from November to February and hot dry from March to April.
