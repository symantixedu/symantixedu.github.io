27. Retell the story through a comic strips of "The Parable Of The Talents".​


Answer:

I can provide you with a brief summary of "The Parable of the Talents" that could potentially be used as a basis for a comic strip:

The parable tells the story of a man who goes on a journey and entrusts his property to his servants. He gives one servant five talents, another servant two talents, and a third servant one talent, each according to their ability. The first servant invests his five talents and earns five more talents, the second servant invests his two talents and earns two more talents, but the third servant is afraid and buries his one talent in the ground.

When the man returns, he asks his servants to report on what they have done with the talents he entrusted to them. The first two servants proudly present the additional talents they have earned, and the man praises them and entrusts them with even greater responsibilities. The third servant, however, admits that he buried his talent and returns it to the man. The man is angry with the third servant for not using the talent and even calls him a wicked and lazy servant.

The parable teaches that God has given each of us talents and abilities, and we are expected to use them to serve Him and others. It also teaches that those who use their talents wisely will be rewarded, while those who waste their talents will be punished.
