2. 2. How you differentiate Community Political Structure from community Economic Structure?​


Answer:

Community political structure refers to the system of governance or decision-making processes within a community, while community economic structure refers to the organization and distribution of economic resources and activities within a community.

In other words, community political structure is concerned with how power is distributed and decisions are made within a community, such as through a democratic process, a hierarchical system, or some other means. Community economic structure, on the other hand, is concerned with how goods and services are produced, exchanged, and consumed within a community, such as through a market economy, a planned economy, or some other system.

While community political structure and community economic structure are related and can impact each other, they are distinct concepts that refer to different aspects of how a community operates.
