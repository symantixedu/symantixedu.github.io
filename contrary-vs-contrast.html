One key difference between man vs. man conflict and man vs. self conflict is the source of the conflict. Man vs. man conflict is external, arising from interactions with others, while man vs. self conflict is internal, arising from an individual's own thoughts, feelings, and desires.

Another difference is the way in which the conflict is resolved. Man vs. man conflict may be resolved through negotiation, compromise, or some other form of resolution, while man vs. self conflict is typically resolved through personal growth, self-reflection, and making changes within oneself.

15. Compare and contrast: Job vs. Career​


Answer:

A job and a career are usually used in the same context, but they actually mean pretty different things. While both are focused on how you earn your money, there are more differences than you may realize.

A job is more short-term oriented and tends to focus purely on earning money. On the other hand, a career is a series of related employment in one field that provides experience for your future and helps you earn a better paycheck and living status.

A common misconception is that you need many years of education to earn a decent career, but there are many careers that only require an associate degree that will greatly improve your paycheck and satisfaction with your working life.

Let’s take a deeper look at the difference between these two commonly associated terms.

What is a job ?

A job is something you simply do for the money. Usually, jobs have a small impact on future resumes because they aren’t typically related to what your career is or will be. Also, jobs usually offer less networking opportunities because your coworkers often won’t be continuing on to the same field as you in your future career.

Most jobs consist of hourly wages, are more short-term, and focus on getting a task done.

That’s not to say that jobs aren’t valuable. Jobs show your work ethic, which is important to future employers, and money pays the bills! Jobs can help prepare you for a career by providing you with valuable skills like time management and communication.

what is a career ?

A career is all about building up skills through various employment opportunities, giving you the ability to move on to higher paying and more prestigious ones. Careers provide a foundation of experiences that help fuel your professional life for many years.

Careers are more long-term and are about learning, gaining experience, building connections, and putting yourself in the right position for promotions and raises. Also, careers tend to be more salary based, as opposed to hourly based like jobs, and often include benefits such as paid time off and healthcare.

19. compare and contrast Xylem Vs Phloem

Xylem and phloem are two types of plant tissues that are responsible for transporting water, nutrients, and other substances throughout the plant. Here are some similarities and differences between xylem and phloem:

Similarities:

1. Both xylem and phloem are vascular tissues that are found in all parts of the plant, including the roots, stems, and leaves.

2. Both tissues are responsible for transporting substances throughout the plant. Xylem transports water and minerals from the roots to the leaves, while phloem transports organic compounds (such as sugars) from the leaves to the rest of the plant.

3. Both tissues are made up of specialized cells that are arranged in specific patterns to facilitate transport.

Differences:

1. Xylem is made up of dead cells, while phloem is made up of living cells. The cells in xylem are elongated and hollow, and they are arranged end-to-end to form long tubes. The cells in phloem are alive and have cytoplasm, and they are arranged in a pattern that resembles a sieve.

2. Xylem transports water and minerals in a unidirectional flow, moving from the roots to the leaves. Phloem transports organic compounds in a bidirectional flow, moving both up and down the plant.

3. Xylem is responsible for maintaining the turgor pressure of the plant, while phloem plays a role in the storage and distribution of nutrients.

4. Xylem is composed of four types of cells: tracheids, vessel elements, fibers, and parenchyma cells. Phloem is composed of four types of cells: sieve tubes, companion cells, fibers, and parenchyma cells.

In summary, xylem and phloem are both important vascular tissues in plants, responsible for the transport of different substances throughout the plant. While they share some similarities, such as their function and distribution throughout the plant, they also have differences in their composition, structure, and transport mechanisms.

Answer:

Xylem and phloem are the two types of specialized vascular tissues found in plants. Both of these tissues are responsible for the transportation of essential substances within the plant, but they differ in terms of their structure and function.

Here are the main differences between xylem and phloem:

1. Structure:

- Xylem is made up of tracheids, vessel elements, fibers, and parenchyma cells, and forms a continuous network of interconnected tubes throughout the plant.

- Phloem is composed of sieve elements, companion cells, fibers, and parenchyma cells, and is found adjacent to the xylem.

2. Function:

- Xylem is responsible for the transportation of water and minerals from the roots to the leaves of the plant, in a process known as transpiration.

- Phloem is responsible for the transportation of organic nutrients, such as sugars and amino acids, from the leaves to the rest of the plant, in a process known as translocation.

3. Flow direction:

- In xylem, the flow of water and minerals is always unidirectional, from roots to leaves.

- In phloem, the flow of organic nutrients can be bidirectional, moving from source (where they are produced, typically leaves) to sink (where they are utilized, typically roots), depending on the needs of the plant.

4. Movement mechanism:

- The movement of water and minerals in xylem is driven by transpiration pull, which is created by the evaporation of water from the leaves.

- The movement of organic nutrients in phloem is driven by a pressure gradient, with the source (high pressure) pushing the nutrients towards the sink (low pressure).

In summary, xylem and phloem are two different types of vascular tissues that are specialized for the transport of different substances within the plant. Xylem transports water and minerals from roots to leaves via transpiration pull, while phloem transports organic nutrients from leaves to other parts of the plant via a pressure gradient.
