5. a square lot has a side 30 meters long how long is a straight line from one corner to the corner diagonally opposite?​


Answer:

The length of the straight line is 30√2 meters.

Step-by-step explanation:

The straight line from one corner to the corner diagonally opposite is the diagonal of the square lot. So basically, we are asked to solve for the length of the diagonal of the square.

To solve for the length of the diagonal, use the Pythagorean Theorem. The square and the diagonal form two congruent right triangles in which the two legs and hypotenuse are the sides of the square and diagonal thereof, respectively.

Hence, employing Pythagorean Theorem,

[tex]\begin{aligned} s^2+s^2&=d^2\\ 30^2+30^2&=d^2 \quad \quad\quad \text{substitution} \\ 900+900&=d^2 \quad \quad \quad \text{evaluate} \\ 1800&=d^2 \quad \quad \quad \text{simplify} \\ d&=\sqrt{1800} \quad \text{square root property} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Since √1800 = √2 · 900, we can further simplify √1800 meters to 30√2 meters.

15. a grass lot is 45 m long and 32 m wide at each corner,there is a square flower plot of 6 m.what is the area of the lot that contain grass?


Answer:

[tex]1,296m^{2}[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

Total area of the grass lot

[tex]45m(32m)=1,440m^{2}[/tex]

Area of a square flower pot

[tex](6m)^{2}=36m^{2}[/tex]

Since there is a flower pot on each corner of the lot, there are 4 flower pots in all. Multiply the area of a flower pot by 4 to determine the total area occupied by the flower pots -- or also, the area unoccupied by grass.

[tex]36m^{2}(4)=144m^{2}[/tex]

Subtract the area unoccupied by grass from the total area of the grass lot to determine the area occupied by grass.

[tex]1,440m^{2}-144m^{2}=1,296m^{2}[/tex]

29. A triangular corner lot has perpendicular sides of lengths 120 m and 160 m. Find the dimensions of the largest rectangular building that can be constructed on the lot with sides parallel to the streets.


In solving the problem we must know the given,
     - The lengths of the triangle are 160 m and 120 m.

What is ask in the problem?
     - Find the dimension of the largest rectangle building that can be constructed on the lot with sides parallel to the streets.

To solve:
 
     - Draw the triangle for representation.

Let x be the length of a rectangle parallel to the 160 m side
      y be the height

As we noticed, If the rectangular building was placed in a triangular lot, we can have 3 similar triangles. The given triangle and the 2 triangles in both side. The similar triangle has the following ratio:

            [tex] \frac{y}{160m - x} = \frac{120m}{160m} = \frac{3m}{4m} [/tex]
            [tex] \frac{y}{160m -x} = \frac{3m}{4m} [/tex]
            [tex]4y = 3(160m -x)[/tex]
            [tex]4y = 480m -3x[/tex]
            [tex]y = 120m - \frac{3mx}{4m} [/tex]
 
Since A = xy 
Then,    [tex]A = x (120 - \frac{3x}{4}) [/tex]
             [tex]=120x - \frac{3 x^{2} }{4} [/tex]

Get the maximum value of A
             [tex]dA/dx=120 - \frac{3x}{2} = 0[/tex]

So,      [tex]120 - \frac{3x}{2} =0[/tex]
           [tex] \frac{3x}{2} = 120 [/tex]
           [tex]x = 80[/tex]
 
From   [tex]y = 120 - \frac{3x}{4} [/tex], substitute the value of x.
           [tex]y = 120 - \frac{3(80)}{4} [/tex]
           [tex]y = 120 - 60[/tex]
           [tex]y = 60[/tex]
 Since x = 80 and y = 60,
Therefore the dimension of a largest rectangular building that can be constructed on the triangular lot with the with side parallel to the street is 80m by 60m.

Diffraction refers to various phenomena that occur when a wave encounters an obstacle or opening. It is defined as the bending of waves around the corners of an obstacle or through an aperture into the region of geometrical shadow of the obstacle /aperture.
