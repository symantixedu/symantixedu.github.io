14. "A life without dancing is possible but pointless" what do you mean by this quote?​


Answer:

The quote "A life without dancing is possible but pointless" means that although it is possible to live without dancing, it would be a life without joy or meaning. Dancing is an activity that brings people together, allows them to express themselves creatively, and brings happiness and excitement to their lives. Without dancing, life may feel dull and lacking in excitement. This quote emphasizes the importance of finding activities that bring joy and meaning to our lives, and how these activities can contribute to a fulfilling life.

24. "it takes two to tango." what does this quote mean?A. a tango requires two individuals to danceB. one cannot exist without the otherC. it takes two to make any endeavor workD. there should always be two individuals dancing the tango


WHAT IS ASKED?

Choose from the given choices the statement that best defines the phrase or expression "It takes two to tango".

ANSWER

The correct answer is Option C - It takes two to make any endeavor work.

The phrase "It takes two to tango" is a prominent idiomatic expression which emphasizes the importance of collaboration or working together

"It takes two to tango" is applied to everything that required two parties or a situation or argument that involves two people and they are both therefore responsible for it.

25. Task 8.2 A QUOTE ON QUOTE


The phrase “quote unquote” means speaking a quotation on a word for word basis. You end the quotation with the word “unquote” to indicate that it’s finished. On the one hand, the phrase “quote on quote” is a mispronunciation of “unquote.” The correct phrase is “quote unquote.” This indicates that what you’re saying is a repetition of someone else’s statement.

30. According to howard ruff, “it wasn’t raining when noah built the ark.” How do you expound the given quote in relation to our topic. (Topic: Exposure and Vulnerability)


Explanation:

Howard Ruff, author of numerous financial planning books has been quoted as saying "It wasn't raining when Noah built the ark”. His reference was to financial planning, but the same notion applies perfectly when it comes to planning for disasters. Many business spend a great deal of time planning on how to grow their business while ignoring what might happen if they ever experience a major disaster such as a fire or hurricane.
