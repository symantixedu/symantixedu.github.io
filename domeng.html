Severe Tropical Storm Maliksi, known in the Philippines as Severe Tropical Storm Domeng, was a tropical cyclone in June 2018 that brought rainfall to the Philippines and Japan. It caused 2 deaths and prompted the PAGASA to declare the beginning of the rainy season in the Philippines. The fifth named storm and 4th tropical cyclone in the Philippine Area of Responsibility (PAR),[1] it was first noted as an area of convection on May 31.

3. what are the cause about typhoon domeng?​


Answer:

In general, typhoons can be caused by a combination of factors, including warm ocean waters, high humidity, low wind shear, and the Coriolis effect. Warm ocean waters provide the energy source for the typhoon, while high humidity provides the moisture necessary for the typhoon to form and grow. Low wind shear allows the storm to remain intact and maintain its organization, while the Coriolis effect helps to spin the storm and give it its characteristic circular shape.

It's important to note that weather patterns and events, including typhoons, can be influenced by a range of complex and interconnected factors, and it can be difficult to pinpoint specific causes or factors for any given event.
