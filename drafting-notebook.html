ARGUMENTATIVE ESSAY

Theme: Pandemic Covid-19 and Home Learning

Main Argument:

During a pandemic, there's a possibility that students may get sick with Covid-19. Many school-aged children have also witnessed family members afraid they or a loved one may be at risk for a terrible illness or death. Students often experience prolonged physical isolation from their peers, teachers, and families due to school closures.

Pros:

Teachers and students have their interactions with the family at home. Students become more motivated and appreciate education when they perceive parental support. When parents are actively involved, instructors observe significant changes in their classes, including increases in the student's motivation, performance, and character.

Cons:

The student challenges included reduced communication and socialization, a greater challenge for pupils with special education requirements, and increased screen time. Parents perceived the issue to be more closely associated with a lack of learning discipline at home, more time spent assisting their children's learning at home—especially for children in Primary School below Grade 4, lacking technology skills, and increased internet costs.

Teachers identified additional challenges and limitations, such as some restrictions in the selection of teaching methods normally applicable in a traditional face-to-face class, less coverage of curriculum content, and a lack of technology skills that impede the potential of online learning.

Rеf-u-ta-tion:

Social isolation is 0ne of the greatest mental health difficulties in this crisis. Numerous people live alone or away from their support networks, which are typically important in their hard times of distress.

Today, there are so many external activities that it is not surprising that many kids feel distracted. Social media, friends, phones, television, video games, and other activities contribute to students' inability to concentrate on their studies.

The social and interaction-based teaching profession has been transformed into an isolating endeavor. Educators are overcoming obstacles, discovering methods to adapt their virtual classrooms to our new reality, and redefining the purpose of education.

Conclusion:

Online instruction boosts learning by encouraging students to adopt a variety of learning methodologies and by increasing their commitment to their majors. Faculty and students concurred in the study that online education is beneficial during the present pandemic. However, its effectiveness is inferior to face-to-face learning and instruction.
