2. Draw and create a slogan which is related to drugs education.


Drugs End All Dreams, say no to drugs!

Drug use suppresses dreaming in most drug users.

Drugs stimulate feel-good brain regions. After a while, your brain's pleasure centers get acclimated to a drug. Then you need more medicine to feel happy. Soon, you'll need medicine to feel normal. Without the medicine, you're unwell, worried, and irritable. You no longer get high. This applies to illegal and prescription drug use.

Misuse includes taking a drug differently than your doctor instructs (taking more or smashing pills to "shot up" or snort), using someone else's prescription, or taking it to get "high." Chronic drug addiction. It's long-lasting, even if you quit usage. It's not contagious. Addicts can obtain help, but quitting is difficult.

Learn to live without drugs. Drug use produces problems with your family, employment, friends, and money. It would be best if you avoided drug-using friends and venues. You must learn what triggers your drug use so you can avoid or change it. Depression, anxiety, or other mental health issues may have led to your drug usage.
