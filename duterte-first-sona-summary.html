1. summary of duterte's sona 2018


Drug war - Duterte's banner program gets first mention. He says it will be "relentless" and "as chilling as the day it began." Slams human rights groups for criticizing the anti-drug campaign.

Corruption - Thanks Congress for passing the Ease of Doing Business Act. Says all agencies must simplify processes and cut red tape. Says he's had to fire "friends" due to corruption.

Mindanao promise - Vows "significant increases" in Mindanao's budget

Bangsamoro law - Promises to sign the Bangsamoro Organic Law within "48 hours" after Malacañang receives Congress' approved version.

Marawi siege - Recognizes soldiers and police who died fighting in Marawi City. Says government welcomes with open arms terrorists who surrender to authorities.

Foreign policy - Vows to pursue an "independent foreign policy." Mentions ties with Southeast Asian countries but spends bulk of this portion on benefits of "reenergized" ties with China and commitment to "defend our interests in the West Philippine Sea."

Overseas Filipino workers - Condemns deaths and abuses suffered by Filipino migrant workers at the hands of foreign employers. Says OFW rights to be his administration's "foremost foreign policy concern."

Contractualization - Says government efforts have led to regularization of 300,000 workers. Calls on Congress to pass a law prohibiting all forms of contractualization after admitting his executive order won't suffice.

Coconut farmers - Asks Congress to pass bills creating a Coconut Farmers' Trust Fund, one of his campaign promises.

Telecommunications, internet - Reiterates promise to ensure reliable, affordable, and secure telecommunications services. Says terms of reference for 3rd telco player is almost ready. Promises the lowering of interconnection rates between all players to ensure improvements in the entire sector.

Bong Go - Mentions his aide's name in relation to "consultations" with the public and establishing "malasakit centers." Go is said to have plans to run for senator in 2019. (READ: Do Bong Go's donations, billboards violate government ethics code?)

Boracay closure - Justifies the island's closure as an environmental protection measure. Warns local government units to enforce laws and not wait for the national government to "swoop in."

Land use act - Calls on Congress to pass a law creating a national land use policy, legislation that has been pending for two decades.

Disaster management - Calls on Congress to pass a law creating a disaster management department with more powers and resources than existing disaster response agencies. these are some examples. marami pa pong iba.

Coronavirus pandemic – After the usual introductions of top officials, Duterte opens his speech stressing the toll the pandemic has taken on the Philippines as the virus “snuffed” out dreams of prosperity and had the world living in a “troubled time.” He mentions hopes for a vaccine and thanks frontliners who risk their lives to respond to those most affected by the disease

Economy – Duterte highlights the country’s positive credit ratings and says with a strong economic position, the Philippines is in a “better position to weather the crisis caused by the COVID-19 global pandemic."

Infrastructure – He says significant infrastructure projects under the Build Build Build Program were completed and that a “comprehensive written report” detailing these will be released. He also pushes for more infrastructure projects to uplift the livelihoods of Filipinos.

Drug war – As expected, Duterte dedicates time in his speech to his drug war. It was among the first topics he mentioned as he proposed to fight the pandemic with the “same fervor” seen in the government’s anti-illegal drug campaign.

Human rights – He took the language of human rights to justify his war on drugs and abuses seen under his administration, falsely suggesting some rights should be upheld at the expense of others. Duterte vowed to uphold human rights and later threatened to kill drug users.

Overseas Filipino workers – Duterte orders various agencies to help OFWs who have lost their jobs and returned home due to the COVID-19 pandemic. He again calls for the creation of a Department of Overseas Filipinos. Death penalty – For the third time, he asks Congress to bring back the death penalty by lethal injection for crimes under the Comprehensive Dangerous Drugs Act of 2002, and jokes that lawmakers seem uninterested, going by their lukewarm reception. The Batasan plenary hall is filled with applause after. Farmers and fishers – Duterte calls for the passage of a law establishing the Coconut Farmers’ Trust Fund and a P66-billion agricultural stimulus package through the Rural Agricultural and Fisheries Development Financing System Act. 18. summary of sona 2019​


A SUMMARY OF THE STATE OF THE NATION ADDRESS (SONA) 20191.) Duterte's Approval RatingsOne of the first things that the President talks about is his approval rating.He states that only 3% of the Filipino people disapprove of him.

2.) War on DrugsThe next topic of the President is in concern to his War on Drugs.He claims that he will end his term with a fight and that the War on Drugs will be relentless.He also notes that the  War on Drugs will not be finished, as long as corruption is prevalent in his country.

3.) Death PenaltyThe President then proceeded to push for the imposition of the Death Penalty on crimes related to drugs and plunder.

4.) CorruptionThe President then proceeds to condemn the Philhealth ghost kidney treatment scandal that occurred in the same year.He commends the National Bureau of Investigations (NBI) for the successful arrest of multiple high-profile criminals. He also reminds the attendees about the time that he arrested more than a hundred officials that were labelled as corrupt by the  Presidential Anti-Corruption Commission (PACC).

5.) Anti-red TapeHe then proceeds to mention the Land Transportation Office (LTO), Social Security System (SSS), Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR), Land Registration Authority (LRA), and  Pag-IBIG, threatening to kill them if they don't improve the speed in regards to the processing of permits.

6.) Rehabilitation of Tourist AttractionsThe President notes the rehabilitation of Boracay, proclaiming that it was a success, along with the rehabilitation of Manila Bay.He also proceeded to threatened establishments who would violate regulations, saying that he would burn them down.

7.) Telecommunications NetworksThe President then introduces Dito Telecommunity, the third established telecommunications network in the country.He promises congress that no corruption will exist within this new telecommunications network.

8.) Bangsamoro Organic LawThe President then proceeds to address the Bangsamoro government, telling them to speed up their movement so that the quality of life of the Muslim and indigenous people would improve.

9.) Communist ViolenceThe President then proceeds to note of his leadership in the National Task Force to End Local Communist Armed Conflict.He also intends to assign various members of congress to multiple regions in order to aid in his goal to end Communist violence.

10.) EducationHe then proceeds to commend the secretary of the Department of Education, Leonor Briones.He notes the improvements in the National School System, and also acknowledges his support for the National Academy for Sports.

11.) Barangay PollsHe then proceeds to request congress to temporarily suspend the May 2020 barangay and Sangguniang Kabataan elections.

12.) OFWsHe then mentions his signed agreement with Kuwait, in his effort to prevent abuse of OFWs from employers.He also states his plan of establishing a Department of Overseas Filipino Workers.13.) The West Philippine SeaHe then proceeds to claim that he can't assert his territorial rights against China, due to fears of sparking international conflict.He defends the presence of Chinese fishing vessels in the Philippine sea.

14.) PovertyHe states that the poverty rate decreased to 21% in the first half of 2018.

15.) Tax ReformHe then proceeds to address congress in concern to the Trabaho Bill, the second phase of the Comprehensive Tax Program.

16.) Liquor BanHe then proceeds to express his desires to close all drinking establishment by midnight.

17.) Disaster Risk Reduction and ManagementThe President then proceeds to express his wishes for the establishment of the Department of Disaster Resilience, which he hopes will help in the preparation for future calamities.

18.) Fire ProtectionHe then addresses congress in his regards to a law called the Fire Protection Modernization Program.

19.) Land Use PlanningHe then addresses congress in regards to passing the National Land Use Act, which will help in encouraging businesses to develop in places other than the NCR.

20.) TrafficHe addresses the Metropolitan Manila Development Authority and Metro Manila local government officials, telling them to reclaim public roads that are being utilized for private matters. He then orders Secretary Eduardo Año to suspend mayors who do not comply with his wishes.

21.) Rice and Coconut FarmersHe then proceeds to say that he promises to implement the Rice Tarrification Law.He also emphasizes that the coco levy fund should be used with care.He then proceeds to blast the Land Bank of the Philippines for allegedly prioritizing commercial transactions over agricultural activities.

Strengthening National SecurityHe then proceeds to express his support for the passage of the National Defense Act and the Unified Military and Uniformed Personnel Separation, Retirement, and Pension Bill.He also expresses his support for the passage of the Reserves Officers' Training Corps (ROTC) for Senior High School Students.
