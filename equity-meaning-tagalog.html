7. what you mean by equity​


Answer:

Put simply, equity is ownership of an asset of value. In corporate finance, equity (more commonly referred to as shareholders' equity) refers to the amount of capital contributed by the owners. Put another way, equity is the difference between a company's total assets and total liabilities.

23. What does it mean that the U.S economy is strong, but markets are expensive. Maintain a core allocation to U.S equity, but consider rebalancing toward international and emerging market equities.


Answer:

The statement suggests that while the U.S economy is performing well, stock markets in the country are currently overpriced. This means that stocks are trading at higher valuations than what is justified by the underlying fundamentals of the companies.

As a result, the recommendation is to maintain a core allocation to U.S equity, meaning that investors should still hold stocks in U.S companies, but they should also consider rebalancing their portfolios to include more international and emerging market equities. This is because the markets in these regions may offer better opportunities for growth and value, and may not be as expensive as the U.S markets.

Rebalancing is the process of readjusting the weightings of different assets in a portfolio to maintain its desired level of risk and return. By reallocating investments to international and emerging market equities, investors can potentially benefit from the growth opportunities in those markets, while also diversifying their portfolios and reducing their exposure to overpriced U.S equities.

In summary, the statement suggests that investors should continue to hold U.S equities as a core part of their portfolios, but should also consider diversifying their investments to international and emerging markets in order to take advantage of potential growth opportunities and to reduce their exposure to expensive U.S equities.
