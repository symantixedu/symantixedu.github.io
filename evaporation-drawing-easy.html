30. Draw an example of each 1. melting2. evaporation 3. condensation 4. freezing​


Answer:

In ice cube melting into liquid water is a familiar example of the process. Another common example is melting butter in a hot pan.

Evaporation is defined as the process in which the state of water from liquid to gaseous or to vapour state takes place. ... The melting of an ice cube is an example of evaporation. Evaporation of acetone which is used for removing nail paint is another everyday example of evaporation.

Having a cold soda on a hot day, the can "sweats." Water molecules in the air as a vapor hit the colder surface of the can and turn into liquid water.  The mirror in the bathroom during a shower becomes foggy because warmer water vapor in the air hits the cooler surface of the mirror.

Freezing is the process when a liquid turns into a solid. ... One example of freezing is when water turns into ice. Freezing is the opposite of melting, and two steps away from evaporation. Freezing occurs at below 0 degrees Celsius with water, while some other liquids become a solid at higher or lower temperatures.
