13. is air matter? why or why not? explain​


Answer:

Air is a matter because air is our most familiar example of the state of matter we call gas. We live immersed in it and depend on it to stay alive. It's also invisible, not particularly tangible, and can be challenging to investigate. But, like solids and liquids, air is matter. It has weight (more than we might imagine), it takes up space, and it is composed of particles too small and too spread apart to see. Air, a mixture of gases, shares properties with water vapor, the gaseous form of water that is part of air. Understanding air helps us understand water vapor.

Air is classified as matter because it has mass and takes up space, which is the definition of matter.

25. 1. explain why a boat is fluid friction2.explain why a cart is rolling friction3.explain why a book placing to table is static friction ​


Answer:

1. A boat is an example of fluid friction because it moves through a fluid medium, which creates resistance and slows down the boat's forward motion. The frictional force is generated due to the contact between the boat's hull and the water, and it opposes the direction of motion of the boat.

2. A cart is an example of rolling friction because it moves on wheels, which roll over the surface of the ground. Rolling friction occurs because of the deformation of the wheel's surface as it rolls over the ground, causing a resistance force that opposes the motion of the cart.

3. When a book is placed on a table, it experiences static friction. Static friction is the force that opposes the motion of an object on a surface when it is at rest. In this case, the book is not moving, and the table exerts an equal and opposite force on the book, preventing it from sliding off the surface. This force is called static friction because it exists when the book is not moving, but disappears once the book starts to slide.
