3. essay about Expository Text​


Expository text is a type of writing that is used to explain, describe, or inform. It is a text type that is used in many different forms, including essays, textbooks, instruction manuals, and encyclopedia entries. Expository text is used to convey information in an organized and logical way.

The purpose of expository text is to inform the reader. It is meant to explain a topic in an objective manner, without expressing any personal opinion or emotion. It is also used to describe a process or a concept, or to provide facts about a certain topic.

Expository text is usually organized in a logical way and follows a specific structure. It is commonly written in a five-paragraph format, with an introduction, body paragraphs, and a conclusion. The introduction explains the main topic and provides background information, while the body paragraphs provide more in-depth information on the topic. The conclusion sums up the main points in the essay and provides a final thought.

Expository text should always use clear language and be written in a straightforward manner. It should be concise and to the point, and should avoid using jargon or complex words. It should also be written in an objective, unbiased manner.

The Philippines is a country with a rich and diverse culture, shaped by a history of colonial and foreign influence. Many of the traditions and customs of the Philippines are influenced by the indigenous cultures of the Austronesian people, as well as by the Spanish, American, and Chinese cultures that have left their mark on the country.

One of the most distinctive traditions of the Philippines is the celebration of fiestas, or festivals. Fiestas are held to honor a patron saint or to mark an important event in the community. They typically involve religious ceremonies, parades, and cultural events such as dance performances and music.

Another important aspect of Philippine culture is the role of family and community. Filipinos place a strong emphasis on close relationships with family and friends, and it is common for extended families to live together or to be closely connected. Community involvement is also highly valued, and it is not uncommon for people to participate in neighborhood organizations or volunteer their time to help others.

Filipino cuisine is another aspect of the country's culture that is deeply ingrained in daily life. The Philippines has a wide variety of dishes that are influenced by both indigenous and foreign culinary traditions. Some popular dishes include adobo, a marinated meat dish; sinigang, a sour soup; and lechon, a roasted pig.

The Philippines is also home to a number of traditional crafts and art forms. These include basket weaving, wood carving, and the creation of intricate beadwork and jewelry. The country is also known for its vibrant and colorful textile arts, such as the creation of traditional garments and fabrics using natural dyes.

Overall, the traditions and culture of the Philippines are a vibrant and integral part of the country's identity. From the celebration of fiestas to the importance of family and community, these cultural practices and values are deeply ingrained in the lives of Filipinos and contribute to the country's rich and diverse cultural heritage.

Expository essays are an important part of academic writing, especially in humanities and social sciences. The essay aims to explain, clarify, or provide information about a specific topic in a logical and clear manner. A successful expository essay should contain a well-formulated thesis statement, clear and structured paragraph, and supporting arguments or evidence.

The expository essay format is an effective way to communicate a logical flow of information to an audience. It allows the writer to provide evidence and explanations that enable the reader to fully grasp the topic at hand. The format is also useful in conveying complex ideas in a simple and understandable way.

However, it is important to note that expository essays are not without limitations. The format is focused on facts and is not designed for opinion-sharing or argumentation. Therefore, the essay may lack a personal touch or voice. Introducing personal opinions or ideas may be difficult in expository writing. Furthermore, the monotonous and structured nature of the format may make it challenging to fully engage readers.

In conclusion, expository essays are an effective way to communicate information in a logical and structured way. However, it must be noted that the format may have limitations in conveying personal opinions and emotions. The success of the essay hinges on the writer's ability to create a clear and concise argument and convey the topic in a way that is accessible to their audience.

18. expository essay about teenage pregnancy: CAUSE AND EFFECT​


Answer:

Teenage pregnancy has been a major issue in society for decades. It refers to pregnancy that occurs in girls who are 19 years old or younger. There are several causes of teenage pregnancy, including lack of education, poverty, peer pressure, and media influence. The effects of teenage pregnancy are far-reaching and can have serious consequences for both the teenage mother and her child.

One of the main causes of teenage pregnancy is the lack of education about sex and contraception. Many teenagers are not properly informed about the risks of unprotected sex and the methods of birth control available to them. As a result, they engage in sexual activity without using any form of protection, which increases their risk of becoming pregnant.

Poverty is another cause of teenage pregnancy. Teenagers who come from low-income families often lack access to quality education and healthcare, which can make it difficult for them to make informed decisions about their sexual health. They may also be more likely to engage in sexual activity as a means of escape from their difficult circumstances.

Peer pressure can also contribute to teenage pregnancy. Teenagers who feel the need to fit in with their peers may engage in sexual activity before they are emotionally or physically ready. They may also feel pressured to engage in unprotected sex as a way of proving their maturity or popularity.

Media influence is another cause of teenage pregnancy. The media often portrays sex in a casual and carefree manner, which can influence teenagers to engage in sexual activity without considering the consequences. Additionally, the media may not adequately address the importance of contraception and safe sex practices.

The effects of teenage pregnancy are numerous and can be long-lasting. Teenage mothers are more likely to drop out of school and have lower levels of education, which can limit their job opportunities and earning potential. They are also more likely to experience poverty and social isolation, which can have a negative impact on their mental health.

The children of teenage mothers are also at risk for several negative outcomes. They are more likely to have low birth weights and suffer from developmental delays. They may also be at higher risk for abuse and neglect, as teenage mothers may not have the knowledge or resources to properly care for their children.

In conclusion, teenage pregnancy is a complex issue with several causes and effects. It is important for teenagers to receive proper education about sex and contraception, and for society to address the underlying issues of poverty and social isolation. By taking steps to prevent teenage pregnancy, we can help ensure a brighter future for both teenagers and their children.
