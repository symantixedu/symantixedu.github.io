20. The Spider and Fly 1. What are fables? 2. What do you think are the characteristics of Filipino are depicted in the given fable? 3. What is the moral lesson in the story?​


Answer:

1. Fables are short stories that often use animals as characters to teach a moral lesson or to convey a certain message or value.

2. In the given fable "The Spider and Fly," the characteristics of Filipinos depicted are their resourcefulness and ability to adapt to different situations. The spider in the story is shown as resourceful and cunning, using his skills to capture the fly. The fly, on the other hand, represents the innocence and naivety of Filipinos who can easily fall prey to those who are more cunning and manipulative.

3. The moral lesson in the story is that we should be cautious and wise in our dealings with others, especially those who may have ill intentions. We should not be easily swayed or deceived by flattery or empty promises. This is especially important in a society where there are those who may try to take advantage of the vulnerable or the unsuspecting. The fable also reminds us that even the smallest and seemingly weakest creature can use its wits and strengths to overcome its adversaries.

fable is a short story that teaches a lesson or conveys a moral. You'll find a lot of personified animals in fables, like talking turtles and wise spiders.
