19. fake friends experiece speech​


Answer:

"Ladies and gentlemen, have you ever had a friend who you thought was genuine and true, only to later discover that they were not who they seemed to be? I'm sure we've all been there. The experience of having a fake friend can be devastating and leave us feeling hurt, betrayed, and alone.

Fake friends can come in many forms. They may be the friend who only reaches out when they need something, or the one who talks about you behind your back. They may even be the friend who seems to be there for you, but never truly listens or supports you.

The thing about fake friends is that they can be hard to spot. They can be charming and convincing, making it difficult for us to see the truth about them. But as time goes on, their true colors begin to show, and we are left wondering how we could have been so blind.

But the thing to remember is that a fake friend is not a reflection of who you are. It is not a measure of your worth or value as a person. It is simply a reminder that not everyone in this world is genuine and true.

So, what can we do when faced with a fake friend? The first step is to recognize the truth about the situation and remove yourself from the friendship. It may be hard to let go, but it is important to surround yourself with people who are truly there for you and support you.

Next, take some time to heal and process the experience. It's okay to be hurt and to grieve the loss of the friendship. But know that this experience can also be an opportunity for growth and to learn more about yourself and the kind of friends you want in your life.

In conclusion, fake friends can be a painful experience, but it's important to remember that it's not a reflection of who you are. It's a reminder to surround yourself with people who are truly genuine and supportive, and to always be true to yourself."

1. Description

This is pretty straightforward. Texts that use this structure simply describe something. With few exceptions, these texts also present plenty of details about what they're describing.

A text using this structure might also:

Tell you why something is being described

2.Cause/Effect

Cause/Effect text structures explain, well, causes and effects. Sounds pretty simple! But works that use this structure can become complex when an effect has multiple causes (or vice versa).

Students will encounter complex examples of cause-effect when they read historical texts. Many events in history had more than one cause, all related in ways that can be difficult to unpack.
