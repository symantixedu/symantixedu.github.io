24. A big rock falls from the mountain .what energy has the failing rock


When a big rock falls from the mountain, its gravitational potential energy (GPE) is transformed into kinetic energy as the rock falls. Gravitational potential energy refers to the energy possessed by an object by virtue of its position in a gravitational field while kinetic energy is the energy of an object by virtue of its motion – whether its vertical or horizontal. Kinetic energy is equal to 0.5 • m • v^2, where m is the mass of the object and v speed of the object.
