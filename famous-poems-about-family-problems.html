1. poem about famous renaissance baroque artworks​


Answer:

They have carried the mahogany chair and the cane rocker

out under the lilac bush,

and my father and mother darkly sit there, in black clothes.

Our clapboard house stands fast on its hill,

my doll lies in her wicker pram

gazing at western Massachusetts.

This was our world.

I could remake each shaft of grass

feeling its rasp on my fingers,

draw out the map of every lilac leaf

or the net of veins on my father's

grief-tranced hand.

Out of my head, half-bursting,

still filling, the dream condenses--

shadows, crystals, ceilings, meadows, globes of dew.

Under the dull green of the lilacs, out in the light

carving each spoke of the pram, the turned porch-pillars,

under high early-summer clouds,

I am Effie, visible and invisible,

remembering and remembered.

Our family is the proverbial egg that we were hatched from. Everything about who we are, both our nature and nurture comes from our family. Often in western society, we stray far from our family and may not talk to them for years at a time. We may feel that we have nothing in common with them. 11. poem about family, friends ​


Answer:

Family and friends, the ties that bind

Through thick and thin, we always find

Support and love that never ends

From family and friends, we depend

In times of joy and times of sorrow

They are the ones who we turn to tomorrow

Their comforting words and gentle embrace

Help us find our way and navigate life’s race

In moments of laughter and moments of tears

They are there to share our hopes and fears

Their encouragement and unwavering trust

Help us stay true to ourselves and adjust

Through distance and time, we stay connected

Our love and memories never neglected

As we journey together, through life’s long song

So let us cherish these precious relationships

And never take for granted their endless gifts

For family and friends, the heart always yearns

Their love and support, forever burns.
