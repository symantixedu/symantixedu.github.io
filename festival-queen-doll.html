Festival dances use movements, outfits, and accessories from their country of origin to represent the people's culture and ways of life.

Dinagyang Festival, which has won the most awards in the Philippines for creativity, legacy, and popularity, is also known as the "Queen of all festivals.

An annual religious and cultural event known as the Dinagyang Festival takes place in Iloilo City, Philippines, on the fourth Sunday in January, immediately following the Ati-Atihan Festival in Kalibo, Aklan, and Sinulog in Cebu.

A fellow Augustinian priest in Iloilo received a replica image of the Santo Nio as a birthday present in 1967 from an Augustinian priest from Cebu, which marked the beginning of the Dinagyang festival. Since then, the San Jose Parish Church has hosted a novena liturgy every Friday.

The portrayal of the Ilonggo aborigines' way of life, which goes beyond the simple use of the bow, arrow, spear, and shield as their primary cultural symbols, is the essence of the Dinagyang Festival as a cultural event.

Red and yellow, which stand for fire, earth, light, and passion, are the most prominent hues.
