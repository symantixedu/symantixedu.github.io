5. heat of tranfer toasting of bread ​


Explanation:

Toasting bread involves the transfer of heat from a heat source, such as an electric heating element, to the surface of the bread. The heat is transferred through a combination of conduction, convection, and radiation.

When bread is placed in a toaster, the heating element heats up, causing the air around it to also heat up.


6. What bread is good to toast?


Different breads need different kinds of toasting. Tender, buttery brioche can't take high heat; denser, moister whole-grain breads can. Challah, ciabatta, semolina bread, baguettes split lengthwise, pain de campagne — all make fine toast (actually, day-old bread makes the best toast), given proper attention.
