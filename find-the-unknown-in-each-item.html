Combination

A combination could be a choice of all or a part of a collection of objects. The order of the chosen set is irrelevant. An example includes having a set of three letters: X, Y, and Z. We are looking for the number of ways we can select 2 letters from that set. A combination would be each possible selection. The complete list of possible selections would be: XZ XY, and YZ.

The number of tentative combinations of n objects taken r at a time is:

nCr = n(n - 1)(n - 2) ... (n - r + 1)/r! = n! / r!(n - r)! = nPr / r!

Note that XY and YX are considered to be one combination, because the order in which objects are selected does not matter. This is the key distinction between them and a permutation. A combination focuses on the choice of objects, order is irrelevant. A permutation, in distinction, focuses on the arrangement of objects with relevance regarding the order in which they're organized.

Types of combinationRepetitionNo repetition

Repetition

In a combination, a repetition is referred to as having the same numbers in a set

No repetition

In a combination, no repetition means that no numbers should be repeated. The numbers are drawn one at a time. The order does not matter.

Examples of combinationCombination: Picking a team of 5 people from a group of 10Combination: Choosing 8 desserts from a menu of 10.

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION:}}[/tex]

- Using the Combination Formula and substitute the given.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \bold{\color{lightblue}Formula:} \\ & \boxed{\: \rm _nC_r = \frac{n!}{r!(n - r)!} \:}\end{aligned} [/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned} \rm C(6,r) = \frac{6!}{r!(6 - r)!} \end{aligned} [/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned} \rm 15 = \frac{6!}{r!(6 - r)!} \end{aligned} [/tex]

- Give the values of 5!

[tex]\begin{aligned} \rm 15 = \frac{6\cdot5\cdot4\cdot3\cdot2}{r!(6 - r)!} \end{aligned} [/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned} \rm 15 = \frac{720}{r!(6 - r)!} \end{aligned} [/tex]

- Exchange 15 and r!(6 - r)!

[tex]\begin{aligned} \rm r!(6 - r)! = \frac{720}{15} \end{aligned} [/tex]

[tex]\rm r!(6 - r)! = 48[/tex]

- Factor 48 into factorial numbers.

[tex]\rm r!(6 - r)! = 24 \cdot 2[/tex]

[tex]\rm r!(6 - r)! = 4! \cdot 2![/tex]

- Let r! be 4! and 2!

[tex]\rm r! = 4! \quad ; \quad r! = 2![/tex]

[tex]\rm r=4 \quad ; \quad r=2[/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex] The values of r are 4 and 2.

A formula for the number of possible combinations of r objects from a set of n objects. This is written in any of the ways shown below.

(n¦r)  or nCr or C(n,r)  or occasionally C n¦r

All forms are read aloud "n choose r."

Formula: (n¦r)  or nCr=  n!/r!(n-r)!=  (n(n-1)(n-2)…(n-r+1))/r!.

    = 87,178,291,200/ 3,628,800 (24)

Cancelled out the number that are the same in the numerator and denominator.

    = 24,024/ 24

    = 1,001
