6. how the following scenarios cause soil erosion; deforestation, strong winds, floods, construction


Trees support soil. It keeps them in place and absorbs water and and provides shade from the sun and the wind. If too many are cut down, it can leave the soil vulnerable to water and wind, causing soil erosion.

Strong winds can easily take away the surface of the soil, and cause it to erode.

Floods (possibly caused by lack of trees or plants) can wash away the soil. Floods with strong currents can take away the surface of the soil and reduce its fertility.

Deforestation is often needed for construction, so when the ground is barren, both wind and water will take its course.

11. why does deforestation cause flooding in lowlands?​


Answer:

Deforestation plays several roles in the flooding equation because trees prevent sediment runoff and forests hold and use more water than farms or grasslands. Some rainwater stays on the leaves, and it may evaporate directly to the air (the more water used in the watershed, the less remains to run off).
