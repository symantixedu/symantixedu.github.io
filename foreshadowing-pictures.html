3. meaning of foreshadowing


Answer:

Foreshadowing is a literary device that alludes to a later point in the story. For example, if a character mentions offhandedly that bad things always happen to them in autumn, then the observant reader will be alert when the leaves in the story begin to fall.


4. flashbacking vs foreshadowing​


Answer:

Flashbacks and foreshadowing are tools that we can use to add dimension to our writing. Flashbacks give us the ability to see into a character's past in real time. Foreshadowing drops hints of what may happen in the future. ... Flashbacks interrupt the current action of the story to show a scene from the past.

15. What is the effect of foreshadowing?


Foreshadowing in fiction creates an atmosphere of suspense in a story, so that the readers are interested and want to know more. This literary device is generally used to build anticipation in the minds of readers about what might happen next, thus adding dramatic tension to a story.

23. halimbawa ng foreshadowing


Kasagutan:

Foreshadowing

Ang foreshadowing ay isang bagay o sitwasyon na nagbibigay suhestiyon, nagrerepresenta o nagpapahiwatig ng isang bagay na mangyayari kalaunan.

Halimbawa:

Sa mga aklat o pelikula lalo na dito sa ating bansa, kapag may nahulog na larawan ng isang tao ay maaaring mapahamak o mamaalam sa aklat o pelikula ang karakter na ito.

Isa pang halimbawa ay kapag ang isang tao halimbawa sa teleserye ay masyadong nagpapakita ng kanyang pagmamahal at suporta sa kanyang kapareha kahit wala namang importanteng okasyon ay maaaring mamaalam na ang tauhan na ito sa teleserye.

30. 5 example of foreshadowing​


Foreshadowing is a literary technique wherein the author hints or suggests events that will happen later in the story. Here are five examples of foreshadowing in literature:

1. In "Romeo and Juliet" by William Shakespeare, Romeo has a feeling of impending doom before he attends the Capulet's party. He says, "Some consequence yet hanging in the stars shall bitterly begin his fearful date with this night's revels." This foreshadows the tragic ending of the play.

2. In "To Kill a Mockingbird" by Harper Lee, the knothole in the tree where the children find gifts from Boo Radley is described as a "malevolent phantom." This suggests that there is something ominous about the tree and that it will play a role in the story's events.

3. In "The Tell-Tale Heart" by Edgar Allan Poe, the narrator constantly hears a beating heart, which he tries to convince himself is just his imagination. This foreshadows the revelation that the narrator has murdered someone and buried the body beneath the floorboards.

4. In "The Great Gatsby" by F. Scott Fitzgerald, the billboard of Dr. T.J. Eckleburg with its "blue and gigantic" eyes overlooks the Valley of Ashes. This suggests that there is a moral decay in the society and that the eyes are watching over and judging the characters in the story.

5. In "Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone" by J.K. Rowling, the mention of the "strange power" that Harry has over Voldemort when they first meet foreshadows the later revelation that Harry is the only one who can defeat Voldemort. This is also reinforced by the fact that Harry's scar, a result of his encounter with Voldemort, burns whenever Voldemort is near
