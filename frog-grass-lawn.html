1. Which food chain is in the correct order? grasshopper → grass → snake → frog → hawk grasshopper → frog → hawk → snake → grass grass → grasshopper → frog → snake → hawk grass → grasshopper → snake → frog → hawk


The food chain that is in the correct order is: grass → grasshopper → frog → snake → hawk

Remember that no food chain can start from a consumer since there will be no source of energy for the primary consumers. In this case, the producer is the grass, it produces its own food via photosynthesis; the grasshopper is the primary consumer, it consumes the producer; the secondary consumer is the frog which in turn eats the primary consumer; and finally the tertiary consumer is the hawk, which eats the secondary consumer.

Do note though that energy received per level in the food chain and energy pyramid is decreasing as you ascend. This is because some of the energy is lost by the organisms to the environment as heat while some is used by the organism.

28. A trapezoidal lawn has an upper or 8 meters and a lower base of 14 meters. The distance between the parallel bases is 5 meters. if the lawn is to be planted with Bermuda grass ,how many square meters of Bermuda grass will be needed​


Answer:

55 square meters of Bermuda grass will be needed to plant the trapezoidal lawn.

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the area of the trapezoidal lawn, we need to use the formula for the area of a trapezoid:

Area = (upper base + lower base) x height / 2

where the height is the perpendicular distance between the upper and lower bases.

Substituting the given values, we have:

Area = (8 + 14) x 5 / 2

Area = 22 x 5 / 2

Area = 55 square meters

Therefore, 55 square meters of Bermuda grass will be needed to plant the trapezoidal lawn.
