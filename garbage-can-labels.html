1. Label the garbage cans properly.


Answer:

Label means A brand Ex.a Coca cola is a brand

Answer:

Labeling Your Garbage Cans

Explanation:

Supplies

Stencils, sized to fit your trash cans

newspaper

scissor

painter’s tape

gloves

spray paint*

garbage cans

ruler, optional

*I really suggest getting Rust-oleum’s Painter’s Touch Ultra Cover. This works on a variety of surfaces, including plastic, and there were a lot of nice colors. I used Key Lime.

Process

Identify where you want the number and/or symbol to go on the trash cans. Put a strip, or several, of painter’s tape on the trash can to create guides to position the stencils. Use a rule to help center or keep lines level, as needed.

Tape the stencils(s) to the garbage can.

Cut a piece of newspaper the size of the stencil in the center of the piece of paper. Place the newspaper shield over the stencil and tape into place. 

While wearing gloves, evenly and gently spray paint the can at the stencil.

Remove the newspaper shield and the stencil gently. Lastly, remove the tape guides from the trash can.

24. the garbage challenge the concept is simple turn a piece of garbage into something that can be used again in a different way​


Answer:

The "garbage challenge" is a creative and environmentally-friendly concept that encourages people to find new and innovative ways to repurpose or upcycle waste materials that would otherwise end up in a landfill or pollute our environment. The challenge involves taking an item of garbage, such as a plastic bottle or old clothing, and transforming it into something new and useful, such as a planter or a tote bag.

By participating in the garbage challenge, individuals can help reduce waste and promote sustainability by giving old items new life. It also encourages people to think creatively and find new uses for items that may have been considered useless or disposable.

Some examples of the garbage challenge include creating artwork from recycled materials, building furniture from discarded wood pallets, or even turning plastic bags into fashionable accessories. The possibilities are endless, and the only limit is your imagination.

Explanation:

Overall, the garbage challenge is a fun and engaging way to promote sustainability and encourage individuals to think more critically about their waste habits.
