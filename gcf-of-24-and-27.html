Answer:

Learning Task 2: GCF Using Continuous Division

GCF stands for the greatest common factor of the numbers. Continuous division is one of the methods that you can use to find the GCF.

24 and 72 = 24

18, 27 and 45 = 9

24, 32 and 48 = 8

24 and 36 = 12

How To Find the GCF Using Continuous Division

Continuous division deals with prime numbers. These are the steps in finding the GCF using continuous division.

Write down the numbers.

Draw an "L" shape around the numbers.

Think of a common prime numbe and write it outside the "L" shape.

Divide the numbers by the common factor and write the quotient below the numbers.

Repeat the process until the numbers end up with no common factor.

Multiply all their common prime factors to get the GCF.
