4. A glass top measures 130 cm by 46 cm. Will it fit a table that measures 127 cm by 62 cm? Why?​


Solution:

• Area of the glass:

Area = 130 x 46

Area = 5,980 sq.cm

• Area of the table

Area = 127 x 62

Area = 7,874 sq.cm

Final Answer:

The glass won't fill the table because the obtained area of the glass which measured 5,980 cm² is lesser than the area of the table that measured 7,874 cm². In order to fill the table, the area of the glass must be equal or greater than to the area of the table.

Hi! First that we need to do is turn this problem into an equation:

16 1/8 - n = 14 3/4

And then, we derive the formula because we are looking for n.\

16 1/8 - 14 3/4 = n

Then, we can now proceed to the solution:
1. turn mixed fraction into an improper fraction
2. find the LCD (Least Common Denominator)
3. subtract the numerator

16 1/8 - 14 3/4 = n
129/8 - 59/4 = n
129/8 - 118/8 = n
11/8 = n
or 
1 3/8 = n
