8. answer the question of the story"THE AGED MOTHER"matsuo basho Question to ask in Moralist Approach 1.What view of life does the story present? which character best articulates this viewpoint? 2. According to this work of life, what is mankind's relationship to God? to the universe? 3. What moral statement, if any, does this story make? is it expicit or implict? 4. What is the authors attitude toward his world? toward fale? toward God? 5 What is the author's conception of good and evil? 6. what does the work say about the nature of good or evil? 7 What does the work Say about human nature?​


Answer:

1. The story of "The Aged Mother" presents a view of life that emphasizes the importance of maintaining faith, hope, and love even in the face of adversity. The best character to articulate this viewpoint is the aged mother herself.

2. According to this work of life, mankind's relationship to God is one of reverence, honor, and obedience. Mankind's relationship to the universe is one of appreciation and respect.

3. The moral statement in this story is implicit rather than explicit. The story encourages readers to maintain faith, hope, and love even in the face of adversity, and to remain steadfast in the face of suffering.

4. The author's attitude toward his world is one of reverence and appreciation. He is respectful of its beauty and magnificence. The author's attitude toward fate is one of acceptance. He does not strive against it, but rather accepts it as part of life's journey. His attitude toward God is one of reverence and submission.

5. The author's conception of good is faith, hope, and love. These qualities are portrayed as being essential in times of difficulty. The author's conception of evil is a lack of faith, hope, and love.

6. The story implies that faith, hope, and love are the only things that can bring true meaning and value to life. It implies that without these qualities, life has little purpose or value.
