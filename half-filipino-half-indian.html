Answer:

Time and Punctuality: The people in these countries take time for granted. If you invite an Indian or Filipino at 11:30 am, they'll arrive at 12 pm. Many of the times, they'd be unapologetic of their lack of punctuality. This a sharp contrast to many western countries and even eastern countries like Japan who don't tolerate even 1 minute delay.

Laziness & Discipline: People of both countries have quite many lazy people who are unwilling to work hard. (No generalizations, but observations.) They always look for shortcuts and easy methods. Also, they lack discipline and their living is pretty disorganized. This is a sharp contrast to people in Japan, Korea, Singapore,etc who work as long as 12 hours per day and overtime is quite common.

Marriage & Dating: People of both countries take relationships seriously with the intention of being together forever. Marriages are considered sacred and divorces are rare. It's encouraged to solve their problems together with mutual understanding, cooperation and compromising. It's discouraged to quit or give up a relationship unless it's a serious problem. This is in contrast to countries like USA or Japan or Korea where dating, breakups, divorces have become a common thing.

Jugaad (Life Hacks): People in both countries know how to reuse things in a productive way. They know how to use things in a creative way which costs less.

Settling abroad: Many people want to settle abroad and wish for a rich life. Preferred countries for Indians are USA and UK while for Philippines it's Japan and Singapore.

Spirituality: Many people are religious and God is a part of their lives. They regularly visit places of worship with true feelings of faith. People in the west and far east are more irreligious.

The culture of the Philippines is a combination of cultures of the East and West.[1] Filipino identity was created primarily as a result of pre-colonial cultures, colonial influences and foreign traders intermixing and gradually evolving together. In pre-colonial times, the Philippines was a divided set of nations, islands and tribes being ruled by their own kings, chieftains, lakans, rajahs, datus and sultans. Every nation has its own identity and some are even part of a larger empire outside of what is now the Philippines. Manila, for example, was once part of the Islamic Sultanate of Brunei, and the Sulu Archipelago was also part of the Hindu Majapahit. The advent of colonial rule in the islands marked the beginning of the Philippines as an entity, a collection of Southeast Asian countries united under Spanish Empire.
