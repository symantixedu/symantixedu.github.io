4. classification of human arm and whale flipper


Human arm and whale flipper has a closely related to evolutionary biology and phylogeny (the evolution of species) and is a common example of comparative anatomy. With regards to function, the whale fin needs to be longer to help in movement through water and thumbs are not necessary, as they don’t need to pick up and grasp things compared to a human arm. With regards to form, the whale has a much shorter and thicker humerus, radius, ulna and much longer metacarpals compared to human arm.

Human arm and whale flipper are one of the examples of homologous Structure. Since we have similar bone structure. 

Homologous Structure is when an organ or bone that appears in different animals which indicate common descent from a common ancestor.
