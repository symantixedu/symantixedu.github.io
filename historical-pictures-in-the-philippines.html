Answer:

The Philippines has a long and complex history of politics, dating back to the pre-colonial period. The country was ruled by various indigenous tribes and kingdoms before being colonized by the Spanish in the 16th century. During the Spanish colonization, the Philippines was ruled as a colony and was governed by Spanish laws and institutions. The Philippines was also occupied by the United States during the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

After the defeat of Spain in the Spanish-American War, the Philippines became a U.S. colony. The U.S. government implemented a policy of "benevolent assimilation" which aimed to Americanize the Philippines. This led to the suppression of local customs, traditions, and languages. The Philippines gained independence in 1946 after a successful revolution against U.S. rule.

After independence, the Philippines had a series of presidents who were either appointed or elected. The country experienced political turmoil, economic instability, and widespread corruption. This led to the imposition of martial law by President Ferdinand Marcos in 1972. The Marcos regime was marked by widespread human rights abuses, electoral fraud, and economic mismanagement.

In 1986, a peaceful revolution known as the People Power Revolution removed Marcos from power and installed Corazon Aquino as the new president. Aquino's presidency was marked by the crafting of the 1987 Constitution, which aimed to restore democracy and human rights in the Philippines. The new constitution established a presidential system of government, a bicameral legislature, and an independent judiciary. The constitution also provided for the protection of human rights, the establishment of a bill of rights, and the creation of an independent commission to oversee elections. The 1987 Constitution is still in effect today and serves as the foundation of the Philippine political system.
