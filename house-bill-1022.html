5. Calculate the mass in grams of 8.35 x 1022 molecules of CBr4


Answer:

To find the mass of 8.35 x 1022 molecules of CBr4, we need to determine the molar mass of CBr4 and then multiply that by the number of molecules. The molar mass of a compound is the sum of the atomic weights of all the atoms in the compound.

The atomic weight of carbon is 12.0107 g/mol, the atomic weight of bromine is 79.904 g/mol, and there are four bromine atoms in a molecule of CBr4, so the molar mass of CBr4 is 12.0107 g/mol + (4 * 79.904 g/mol) = 338.823 g/mol.

Multiplying the molar mass by the number of molecules gives us 338.823 g/mol * 8.35 x 1022 molecules = 2.83 x 1025 grams. This is a very large number, so to make it easier to read, we can express it in scientific notation as 2.83 x 1025 grams = 2.83 x 10^25 grams.

So, 8.35 x 1022 molecules of CBr4 have a mass of 2.83 x 10^25 grams.

7. Ethyl alcohol or ethanol (C2H60) is the main ingredient of many disinfectants. Find the massin grams of the substance that contains 9.8 x 1022 molecules.​


Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of C₂H₆O.

molar mass = (12.01 g/mol × 2) + (1.008 g/mol × 6) + (16.00 g/mol × 1)

molar mass = 46.068 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the mass of C₂H₆O.

[tex]\text{mass of C₂H₆O = 9.8 × 10²² C₂H₆O molecules} × \frac{\text{1 mol C₂H₆O}}{\text{6.022 × 10²³ C₂H₆O molecules}} × \frac{\text{46.068 g C₂H₆O}}{\text{1 mol C₂H₆O}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of C₂H₆O = 7.5 g}}[/tex]
