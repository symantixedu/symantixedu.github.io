8. Why do you think non-members of other groups perceive rallies negatively?


Answer:

There are many reasons why people might perceive rallies negatively. Some people may view rallies as disruptive or inconvenient, especially if the rally blocks traffic or interferes with their daily routine in some way. Others may have political or ideological differences with the group hosting the rally, and so they may view the rally as a threat to their own beliefs. Additionally, some people may be wary of large gatherings of people, particularly if there is a history of violence or clashes at similar events. It is also possible that media coverage of rallies could contribute to negative perceptions, especially if the coverage focuses on any negative or controversial aspects of the event

19. How do you think humans perceive atoms during the time when there was no technology?


By simple definition, Atom is the smallest particle of an element. There were ancient Greek philosophers named Leucippus and Democritus who first developed the concept of the atom, in the 5th century B.C. According to them, they perceived that there were five major points to the atomic idea which are:

• All matter is made of atoms which are too small to be seen and cannot be further split into smaller portions.

• There is a void or empty space between atoms.

• Atoms  are completely solid.

• Atoms are homogeneous and have no internal structure.

• Atoms are different in their sizes and shapes.

1. All matter is composed of atoms, which are too small to be seen and cannot be further split into smaller portions.

The word atom comes from the Greek word “atomos”, which means uncuttable. Democritus reasoned that if matter could be infinitely divided, it was also subject to complete dissolution from which it can never be put back together, destroying all permanent foundation of ultimate particles with which to build up everything we see. Since atoms are invisible by the naked eye, all matter that can be seen by humans is still divisible, therefore cannot be atoms.

2. There is a void or empty space between atoms.

According to them, all changes in matter must be as a result of the movement of atoms (the ultimate and unchanging particles.). On the other hand, in order to move there must be a space or void completely empty of matter, through which atoms can move from place to place.

3. Atoms are completely solid.

Another point that they perceived is that, if there should be a space between atoms, it then follows that there can be no void in atom itself. We now know this to be incorrect when Ernest Rutherford discovered the nucleus in 1911, which demonstrates that a single atom is mostly empty space.

4. Atoms are homogenous and have no internal structure.

The absolute solidify form of the atoms leads to the idea that atoms are the same all the way through, in other words, homogeneous. Another way to explain this idea is that an atom would have no internal structure. However it was not until 1897 that J.J. Thompson discovered the electron, to show that an atom has internal structure.

5. Atoms are different in their sizes and shapes.

Democritus said that “They have all sorts of shapes and appearances and different sizes.... Some are rough, some hook-shaped, some concave, some convex and some have other innumerable variations.”

In conclusion, Since Aristotle together with other prominent thinkers strongly opposed the idea of Leucippus and Democritus about the atom, their theory was disregarded and buried until the 16th and 17th centuries.
