4. in terms of musical style, what is primitivism? how does primitivism differ from other musical arts forms?​


What is primitivism?

Primitivism in music rarely suggests a lack of conventional technique. Rather, it seeks to express ideas or images related to antiquity or some "primitive" culture or attitude. Primitivism can also be understood as a late development of 19th-century nationalism.

In music, primitivism indicates an elevation of rhythm to a place of prominence, and the best early example of this movement is Stravinsky's Le Sacre du Printemps, completed in 1912. The work breaks with old rhythm-phrase accent structures, favouring violent asymmetrical rhythmic accents and shifting patterns.

How does primitivism differ from other musical art forms?

Its adherents favoured simple, clear-cut tunes of folk character that revolved around a central note and moved within a narrow compass; massive harmonies based on blocklike chords moving in parallel formation with harshly percussive effect; and a strong impulsion to a tonal centre which makes it more unique from other musical art forms.
