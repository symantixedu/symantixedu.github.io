3. how long will a speed boat cross a 2.o km lake with a speed of 40 km/h?​


Answer:

It will take 3 minutes for the speed boat to cross the lake.

Explanation:

In this problem, we will use the basic concept of speed where speed is equivalent to the quotient of distance to time.

Formula

speed = distance / time      (s = d/t)

Deriving this formula to solve for time, we have:

time = distance / speed      (t = d/s)

Given information

speed = 40 km/h

distance = 2 km

time = ?

Solving the problem

Let us use the given formula to solve for time

t = d / s

t = 2 km/ (40 km/h)

time = 0.05 hours   or

time = 3 minutes

Therefore, it will take 3 minutes for the speed boat to cross the lake.

6. 2. An airplane flies 525 km north with a velocity of 420 km/h. How long does it take for it to reach its destination?​


We can use the formula:

time = distance ÷ velocity

where:

distance = 525 km (since the airplane is flying 525 km north)

velocity = 420 km/h (since the airplane has a velocity of 420 km/h)

Plugging in these values, we get:

time = 525 km ÷ 420 km/h

time = 1.25 hours

Therefore, it takes the airplane 1.25 hours to reach its destination.


7. 2. Kristel walked at a speed of 5 km/h. How long did it take her to travel 15 km?​


Answer:

3 hrs

Step-by-step explanation:

In order to calculate the time taken by travelling a certain distance, we have to use the formula:

[tex]\sf Time = \frac{Distance}{Speed}[/tex]

[tex]\textsf{Given:}\begin{cases}\sf Distance = 15 \: km\\\sf Speed = 5 \: km/h\\\end{cases}[/tex]

Substituting those given values to the formula, we get:

[tex]\sf Time = \frac{15 \: km}{5 \: km/h}[/tex]

[tex]\sf Time = 15 \: \cancel{km} \times \frac{hr}{5 \: \cancel{km}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf Time = \boxed{\sf 3 \: hrs}[/tex]

In 1 hour Kristel traveled 5 kilometer. The question is how long did it take her to travel 15 kilometers? So the answer is 3 hours

10. How long will it take Billy to cover the 2-km distance from his school to his school at a speed of 5 km/h?


Answer:

3.6 hours

Explanation:

Average speed = Total Distance / Total Time Taken

We have given speeds S1 and S2

S1 = 2.5 km/hr

S2 = 5 km/hr

And Distance D1 and D2

D1 = 6 km

D2 = 6 km

Total Distance = D1 + D2 = 6 + 6 = 12 km

Now

T1 = D1/S1

T1 = 6/2.5 = 2.4 hr

T2 = D2/S2

T2 = 6/5 = 1.2 hr

Total Time = T1 + T2 = 2.4 + 1.2 = 3.6 hr

Now

Average Speed = Total Distance / Total Time = 12 / 3.6

Average Speed = 3.33 km/hr
