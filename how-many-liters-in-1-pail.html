20. A drum can contain 10 000 liters of water, a pail contains only 0.001 part of that ofthe drum. How many liters can the pail contain?​


Since a single drum of water contains 10,000 liters, while a single pail contains 0.1% of it (or in short, 0.001), then we need to multiply 10,000 and 0.001.

10,000 x 0.001 = 10. Because of that, you need tp move 3 decimal places to the left of 10,000 in order to get the answer, according to the decimal places of 0.001.

Therefore, the pail is 10 liters.
