17. The jogging lane of a park is a square with side length 50 meters. How many complete rounds must a jogger go around the square to make sure he has run 2 kilometers?


Answer:

10 times

Step-by-step explanation:

In order to make it easier to work with, let us convert km to m.

1 km = 1000m

2 km = 2000m

Take note that the jogging lane is a square, so let us compute the distance per round by getting the perimeter.

Perimeter = 4s

Perimeter = 4(50) = 200m

We can then simply divide to get the number of rounds.

Total distance = # of rounds * distance per round

2000m = x * 200m

2000/200 = x

10 = x

Therefore, the jogger must go around 10 times.
