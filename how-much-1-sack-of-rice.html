17. A 50 kg sack of rice is lifted to a height of 3 m How much is the gravitational potential energy of the sack of rice?​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The gravitational potential energy of an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{GPE = mgh}[/tex]

where:

GPE = gravitational potential energy

m = mass

g = acceleration due to gravity = 9.8 m/s²

h = height

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the gravitational potential energy

GPE = mgh

GPE = (50 kg)(9.8 m/s²)(3 m)

[tex]\boxed{GPE = \text{1,470 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the gravitational potential energy of the sack of rice is 1,470 J.
