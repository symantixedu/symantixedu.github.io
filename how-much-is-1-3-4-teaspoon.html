3. a recipe needs 3/4 teaspoon salt and 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper how much more salt does the recipe needs


SUBTRACTION OF SIMILAR FRACTIONSProblem:

A recipe needs 3/4 teaspoon salt and 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper. How much more salt does the recipe needs than black pepper?

Answer:

The recipe needs 1/2 teaspoon more salt than black pepper.

Step-by-step explanation:

To solve if how much more salt is needed in the recipe, just subtract given measurements: 3/4 and 1/4. Since both fractions are of the same unit (in teaspoon) and are similar fractions, we can directly subtract the numerators just like how we subtract integers. So 3 minus 1 is equal to 2. Then we will just copy the denominator which is 4. Hence the answer is 2/4 but we need to simplify this to its simplest form. Since the GCF of 2 and 4 is 2, we can divide the GCF on numerator and denominator and we will obtain 1/2.

Therefore, there is ½ teaspoon more salt than black pepper in the recipe.

23. 1.) A recipe needs 3/4 teaspoon black pepper and 1/4 red pepper. How much more black pepper is needed than red pepper for this recipe ?


Answer:

1/2

Step-by-step explanation:

Here's how to subtract 1/4 from 3/4:

3/4-1/4

Step 1

Since our denominators match, we can subtract the numerators.

3 − 1 = 2

So the answer is:

2

4

Step 2

Last of all, we need to simplify the fraction, if possible. Can it be reduced to a simpler fraction?

To find out, we try dividing it by 2...

Are both the numerator and the denominator evenly divisible by 2? Yes! So we reduce it:

2

4

÷ 2 =

1

2

Let's try dividing by 2 again...

No good. 2 is larger than 1. So we're done reducing.

There you have it! The final answer is:

3/4-1/4 = 1/2
