14. How can Research improves the quality of life


Research is an active, diligent, and systematic investigative process with the aim of finding, interpreting, and revising facts. Research has many benefits and can improve human life, especially in the field of science.

There are several benefits of research that can improve the quality of life, such as:

1. As a source of information

In a study, we can name a lot of information related to certain things. Information in a study is reliable information because it is based on theory and also supporting evidence.

2. Research answers the problem

As we know that a reason for research is because there are unsolved problems. And by conducting research, the problem will be resolved or answered and which is certainly credible and trustworthy.

3. Research as an effort to discover new things

With the things being researched, it is determined that there will be new things that are found. Not only that, research can also be proof of the truth of existing knowledge and develop it.

4. To test or verify a topic or problem

To test or verify a topic or problem where the results can strengthen a particular theory or view and can also reject the results of that theory or view.

There are several types of research as follows:

1. Basic Research

Basic research is data collected to increase knowledge. The main motivation is the expansion of knowledge.

2. Secondary Research

Secondary research or desk research is a research method that involves the use of existing data.

3. Applied Research

Applied research focuses on analyzing and solving real-life problems.

4. Problem-Oriented Research

As the name suggests, problem-oriented research is conducted to understand the true nature of the problem in order to find relevant solutions. The term "problem" refers to several options or problems when analyzing a situation.

6. Qualitative Research

Qualitative research is a process of inquiry. This helps to create an in-depth understanding of the problem or issue in its natural setting. This type of research is a non-statistical method. Qualitative research relies heavily on the experience of the researcher and the questions used to investigate the sample.

7. Quantitative Research

Qualitative research is a structured way of collecting data and analyzing it to draw conclusions. Unlike qualitative methods, this method uses computational and statistical processes to collect and analyze data. Quantitative data is data in the form of numbers.
