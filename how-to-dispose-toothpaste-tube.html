Answer:

To determine if there is a significant difference in the mean content of the two brands of toothpaste, you can conduct a two-sample t-test. This test will allow you to determine whether the difference between the means of the two samples is statistically significant, or if it could have occurred by chance.

To perform the test, you will need to calculate the difference between the means of the two samples, and then determine the standard error of the mean difference. The standard error can be calculated using the formula:

standard error = sqrt(((std1^2)/n1) + ((std2^2)/n2))

Where std1 and std2 are the standard deviations of the two samples, and n1 and n2 are the sizes of the two samples.

Once you have calculated the standard error, you can then use the t-test formula:

t = (mean1 - mean2) / standard error

To determine the t-value for the sample means. You can then use a t-table or a statistical software package to determine the p-value associated with this t-value.

If the p-value is less than 0.05, you can conclude that there is a statistically significant difference between the mean contents of the two brands of toothpaste. If the p-value is greater than 0.05, you cannot conclude that there is a significant difference between the means.

25. a school paid 450.00 for 8 toothbrushes and 4 tube of toothpaste. if each tube of toothpaste cost 45 pesos how much did each toothbrush cost?​


PROBLEM:

A school paid 450.00 for 8 toothbrushes and 4 tube of toothpaste. If each tube of toothpaste cost 45 pesos, how much did each toothbrush cost?

If tube of toothpaste cost 45 each.

[tex]45 × 4 = 180[/tex]

Subtract it from the total amount.

[tex]450 - 180 = 270[/tex]

Divide 270 by 8, because they paid 8 toothbrushes.

[tex]270 ÷ 8 a = 33.75[/tex]

Therefore, each toothbrush cost 33.75 pesos.
