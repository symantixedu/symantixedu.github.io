Answer:

1.An earthquake is a weak to violent shaking of the ground produced by the sudden movement of rock materials below the earth's surface.

2.A seismograph is the primary earthquake measuring instrument. The seismograph produces a digital graphic recording of the ground motion caused by the seismic waves. The digital recording is called a seismogram. A network of worldwide seismographs detects and measures the strength and duration of the earthquake's waves.

Triangulation method is a method to identify the epicenter of an earthquake. in order to do this, each of at least 3 seismic stations must determine the distance from their respective station to the epicenter of the quake. then after that, each of them must draw a circle and their respective radius is the distance from their station to the epicenter. after drawing circles, the intersection point of the circles must be determined to identify the epicenter of an earthquake.
