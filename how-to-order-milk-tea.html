11. how will you evaluate the milk tea​


Answer:

What doesn’t work:

The milk tea tastes watered down, even before the ice has melted, or tastes too bitter (oversteeped)

The milk tea is too sweet, even after customizing for sweetness

What to look for:

The milk tea tastes refreshing — creamy but not too heavy

The milk tea is sweet enough to keep you wanting more, but not so sweet that you fear you’ll end up with diabetes

Bonus points for fresh brewed tea from tea leaves and use of organic milk / cream instead of non-dairy creamer

Criteria #2: The tapioca

The tapioca should have the right amount of chewiness (see QQ explained in this previous post). This can often make or break your boba experience.

14. You are a young entrepreneur who ventures into selling MILK TEA. On the soft opening day, you sold all 50 glasses of classic and flavored milk tea, and brought in ₱4700. You need to determine how many glasses of classic milk tea and how many glasses of flavored milk tea were sold on that day if a glass of classic milk tea costs ₱100 and a glass of flavored milk tea costs ₱80.


Answer:

27 classic milk teas and 25 flavored milk teas.

Step-by-step explanation:

Suppose that there are equal numbers of classic milk teas and flavored milk teas sold that day. (25 flavored and 25 classic)

Classic Milk Tea: 25 x 100(P100, refers to the cost of the classic milktea)

You get: 2,500.

DO the same to flavored milk tea:

25 x 80(flavored milk tea price)= 2000

Add the sum of 25 flavored milk tea and 25 classic milk tea's price:

2500 + 2000= 4500.

Now, to get the value of ₱4700, you need to add ₱200 more, right?

to get that value, you need 2 more classic milk teas that costs ₱100 each. It would be wrong if you add 2 flavored milk tea because it would cost ₱160 only. adding 1 more will make it ₱240.

If you add the 2 classic milk teas to the 25 supposed classic milk tea value, you get 27 classic milk teas ordered.

Do the math again.

27 x 100= 2700

25 x 80= 2000

2700+2000=4700

Final Answer: ₱4700.
