26. this model emphasizes the immediate-feedback communication, which means that as a receiver, you not only have to listen attentively, but you must also look out for cues from the speaker.


Answer:

Barnlund’s Transactional Model of Communication. In this model, feedback for the sender is considered as the reply for the receiver, hence there is a need for the receiver to listen attentively and to look out for cues from the speaker.

Explanation:

Models of Communication are used to explain how humans communicate. Here are some of the models.

Aristotle Model

This is the oldest communication model used to determine how to become a better and a more convincing communicator. In this model, Aristotle identified three elements that will improve communication: ethos (credibility), pathos (connection to the audience), and logos (the message).  

Berlo’s Model

This model takes the emotional aspect of the message into account. Communication in this model operates in four steps: Source, Message, Channel, and Receiver.  

Shannon and Weaver

In this model looks into the roles of the ender, encoder, channel, decoder, receiver of the message. Along the way, noise could occur which can disrupt the message.  

Schramm’s Model

In this model, it is important for the receiver to interpret a message in the desired form the sender wants and then give feedback or response to the sender accordingly.  

Westley and MacLean’s Model

This model delves into the environmental and cultural factors that can influence communication.

30. give example scenario about the following feedback in classroom set up: oral feedback, written feedback, formal feedback, descriptive feedback and evaluative feedback.​


Oral feedback: During a classroom discussion, a teacher provides feedback to a student by giving praise for their contribution and suggesting ways for improvement. For example, "That was a great point you made about the main character's motivation. Can you also think of other possible reasons for their actions?"

Written feedback: After grading a set of essays, a teacher provides written feedback to each student on their strengths and areas for improvement. For example, "Your essay shows a clear understanding of the topic, but could benefit from more specific examples to support your arguments."

Formal feedback: At the end of a semester, a teacher provides a formal written evaluation of each student's performance. For example, "Student X has consistently demonstrated a strong work ethic and a deep understanding of the course material. They have shown exceptional critical thinking skills and have actively engaged in class discussions."

Descriptive feedback: During a writing workshop, a teacher provides descriptive feedback to a student by pointing out specific elements of their writing that are effective and suggesting ways to improve. For example, "Your use of imagery in this paragraph is powerful and really adds to the overall impact of the piece. To make it even stronger, consider using more varied sentence structures."

Evaluative feedback: During a science experiment, a teacher provides evaluative feedback to a student by assessing the accuracy of their measurements and calculations. For example, "Your results seem to be slightly off. Can you double-check your measurements and calculations to make sure everything is accurate?"

Answer:

Oral Feedback: The teacher reviews her student's presentation in front of the class each day, giving them verbal praise or constructive criticism.

Written Feedback: The teacher gives a written assessment of the student's work at the end of the term, providing detailed comments and guidance for improving the work.

Formal Feedback: The teacher has a formal end-of-year review with each student, discussing their progress and setting goals for the next year.

Descriptive Feedback: The teacher gives the student feedback on their performance each week, noting both strengths and areas for improvement with specific examples.

Evaluative Feedback: At the end of the year, the teacher evaluates the student's overall performance, giving them a final grade.
