Introduction:

Recess is an important part of the school day that provides children with a much-needed break from academic work. However, some people argue that the length of recess should be increased to provide children with more time to play and socialize. In this essay, I will examine the reasons why some people believe recesses in school should be longer.

Body:

One reason why some people believe that recess should be longer is that it provides children with an opportunity to be physically active. Longer recess periods can give children more time to run, play, and engage in physical activities, which can be beneficial for their overall health and well-being. Additionally, longer recess periods can help children burn off excess energy and improve their focus and attention during academic work.

Another reason why some people think recesses in school should be longer is that it can help children develop social skills. During recess, children have the opportunity to interact with their peers, form friendships, and develop important social skills such as cooperation, communication, and problem-solving. Longer recess periods can give children more time to engage in social activities and develop these skills, which can benefit them both academically and personally.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, some people argue that recesses in school should be longer to provide children with more time to play, be physically active, and develop important social skills. While longer recess periods may have some benefits, it is important to balance these with the need for academic work and classroom instruction. Ultimately, the length of recess should be determined based on the needs of individual schools and the students they serve.
