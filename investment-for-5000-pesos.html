1. How much interest will you have after a year for an investment of 5000 pesos at 3% interest per annum?​


Answer:

Php. 5,150

Step-by-step explanation:

Formula:

[tex]Amount = P(1 + \frac{r}{100} )^{t} [/tex]

P = principal amount

R = rate

T = time

How much interest will you have after a year for an investment of 5000 pesos at 3% interest per annum?

[tex]Amount = 5000(1 + \frac{3}{100} )^{1} \\ \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: = 5000( \frac{103}{100} )^{1} \\ \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: = \frac{5000 \times 103}{100} \\ \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: = \frac{515000}{100} \\ \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: = 5150[/tex]

19. Adrian received a 100,000-peso cash award for his winning project in a science fair. His parents decided to invest his money. The family’s financial adviser presented two investment options: Investment A was a risk-free investment where they stood to gain 5000-pesos and lose nothing. Investment B offered a gain of 9000-pesos with 2/3 probability and a loss of 6000 pesos with 1/3 profitability. a. Construct the probability mass function for each of the two investment options. b. Find the expected profit and variance of each options c. Determine which investment will give a higher expected profit


Answer:

a. Probability mass function (PMF) for Investment A:

If the investment is risk-free and they stand to gain 5000 pesos with no loss, then the PMF is:

P(X = 5000) = 1, where X is the profit.

PMF for Investment B:

The gain of 9000 pesos occurs with a probability of 2/3, so:

P(X = 9000) = 2/3

The loss of 6000 pesos occurs with a probability of 1/3, so:

P(X = -6000) = 1/3

All other outcomes have zero probability.

b. Expected profit and variance for Investment A:

The expected profit is:

E(X) = 1 * 5000 = 5000 pesos

The variance is:

Var(X) = (5000 - 5000)^2 * 1 = 0

Expected profit and variance for Investment B:

The expected profit is:

E(X) = (2/3 * 9000) + (1/3 * (-6000)) = 6000 pesos

The variance is:

Var(X) = [(9000 - 6000)^2 * 2/3] + [(-6000 - 6000)^2 * 1/3] = 6000000

c. Investment B has a higher expected profit of 6000 pesos, compared to the expected profit of 5000 pesos for Investment A. However, Investment B is riskier because it has a higher variance of 6000000, compared to the zero variance for Investment A. Therefore, the decision to choose between the two investments depends on Adrian and his family's risk appetite. If they are risk-averse, they may choose Investment A, but if they are willing to take on more risk, they may choose Investment B.specifically, if they prioritize maximizing expected profit, they should choose Investment B. But if they prioritize minimizing risk, they should choose Investment A.
