11. 1. What are the importance of orthographic and isometric drawing?2. When does isometric drawing are used for?​


Answer:

1.orthographic projections provide the detailed instructions needed to construct an object, the isometric drawing provides a fairly natural view of the object, i.e. close to what it will look like to the eye, and usually the violation of the rules of perspective does not pose a problem.

2.Isometric drawings are commonly used in technical drawing to show an item in 3D on a 2D page. Isometric drawings, sometimes called isometric projections, are a good way of showing measurements and how components fit together. Unlike perspective drawings, they don't get smaller as the lines go into the distance.


12. Define Isometric Drawing.​


Answer:

Isometric projection is a method for visually representing three-dimensional objects in two dimensions in technical and engineering drawings. It is an axonometric projection in which the three coordinate axes appear equally foreshortened and the angle between any two of them is 120 degrees.


13. What are the difference of Isometric Drawing and Multiview Drawing?​


Answer:

Isometric, or pictorial drawings, which represent an object in a three dimensional fashion by showing 3 surfaces of the object in one drawing. Orthographic, or plan view drawings, which represent an object in a two dimensional fashion by showing each surface of the object in its actual shape.

Explanation:

A multiview drawing is one that shows two or more two-dimensional views of a three-dimensional object. Multiview drawings provide the shape description of an object. When combined with dimensions, multiview drawings serve as the main form of communication between designers and manufacturers.

isometric (adj.)

1838, literally "of the same measure," from iso- "the same, equal" + -metric. The components are Greek: isos "equal, identical" + metron "a measure." Originally a method of using perspective in drawing; later in reference to crystals.
