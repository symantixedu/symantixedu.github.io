7. The resultant vector with a negative summation of horizontal component and a negative summation of vertical component lies at Quadrant 3.


The resultant vector is a vector that represents the combined effect of two or more individual vectors. In this case, if the horizontal component and the vertical component of the resultant vector have a negative summation, it means that both components are pointing in a negative direction. This would indicate that the resultant vector lies in the third quadrant of the coordinate plane, which is the quadrant that contains the points with negative values for both the x- and y-coordinates. This quadrant is also sometimes referred to as the "southwest" quadrant.

9. what is summation/sigma notation??


Answer:

A series can be represented in a compact form, called summation or sigma notation. The Greek capital letter, ∑ , is used to represent the sum.

The series 4+8+12+16+20+24 can be expressed as ∑n=164n . The expression is read as the sum of 4n as n goes from 1 to 6 . The variable n is called the index of summation.

To generate the terms of a series given in sigma notation, successively replace the index of summation with consecutive integers from the first value to the last value of the index.

To generate the terms of the series given in sigma notation above, replace n by 1,2,3,4,5,  and  6 .

∑n=164n=4(1)+4(2)+4(3)+4(4)+4(5)+4(6)             =4+8+12+16+20+24             =84

The sum of the series is 84 .

About Argument

An argument is a set of statements related to the topic in the opinion being conveyed. Argument Components Arguments are an attempt to make more than just statements. Arguments are statements that are accompanied by expressions of conclusions. Arguments fall into two groups:

The premise is the statement before the word 'so' (hypothesis) Conclusion is a statement after the word 'so' (conclusion)

Before arguing, it is important to understand what are the basic components that make up an argument, namely premises, inferences, and conclusions.

Premise: A statement in the form of facts explaining reasons and or evidence to believe a claim (inference). Inference: Is what is resolved at the end of the argument. However, in a simple argument, there may be no inference, but only consists of premises and conclusions. Conclusion: Reasoning from an argument or often also called final inference.
