"WALANG MAIIWAN"

A reaction paper from a famous documentary TV called iWitness.

The subject of the documentary Walang Maiiwan, directed by Kara David and produced by iWitness under the GMA Network, is the proportion of pupils at Burgos East Elementary School in San Guillermo, Isabela, who are at risk of dropping out. The documentary demonstrates the difficulties faced by pupils who must cross a river and walk a mile to get to school each day. This is one of the main causes of absenteeism among students. Everyone should be able to pursue their educational goals; poverty did not destroy their aspirations; instead, it was a lack of child assistance.

I was conflicted after watching the video because it made me realize how fortunate I am in life. I have the opportunity to attend school or take classes online, I have the technology necessary for a quality education, I don't have to work hard to get into a respectable university, and yet I still have the audacity to gripe about small inconveniences.

And I believe that living in the city makes us even luckier. However, I'm more impressed with those who reside in the province because they must cross rivers, mountains, and other hazards to get to the only school that is available to them. I also salute the teachers who take the risk of only being able to educate the children who reside in remote areas despite having to travel through rivers, forests, and mountains.

But the unfortunate reality of our time is that no one meets face to face anymore; instead, everyone communicates online. Fortunately, the department of education has come up with a solution: radio learning that can be viewed on television as a module for people who live in rural areas. And young people assert that, to the best of our ability, throughout the pandemic, no student would be left behind because they are unable to attend class. Even if there is a pandemic, we can withstand it and complete the study.
