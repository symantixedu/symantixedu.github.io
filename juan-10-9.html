1.Lakandula

Lakandula was the regnal name of the last lakan or paramount ruler of pre-colonial Tondo when the Spaniards first conquered the lands of the Pasig River delta in the Philippines in the 1570s.
Born: 16 December 1503
Died: 1575
Full name: Bunao Lakandula
Noble family: Tondo
Reign: c. 1521–1571
Children: Magat Salamat

2.Sultan kudarat

Muhammad Dipatuan Kudarat was the 7th Sultan of Maguindanao from 1619 to 1671. During his reign, he successfully fought off Spanish invasions and halted the spread of Catholicism on the island of Mindanao, much like the other Muslim rulers in the southern Philippines.
Born: 1581, Maguindanao
Died: 1671, Maguindanao
Parents: Kapitan Laut Buisan
Reign: 1619–1671
Children: Sultan Dundang Tidulay
Grandchildren: Sultan Kahar Ud-Din Kuda, Sultan Barahaman

3.Gabriala silang

María Josefa Gabriela Cariño de Silang was a Filipina military leader best known for her role as the female leader of the Ilocano independence movement from Spain.
Born: 19 March 1731, Santa
Died: 20 September 1763, Vigan City
Full name: María Josefa Gabriela Cariño
Parents: Anselmo Cariño
Spouse: Diego Silang (m. 1757–1763), Tomás Millan (m. 1751)

4.Francisco maniago

Maniago Revolt was an uprising in Pampanga that stemmed from the oppressive policies imposed during the Spanish colonial rule. It was led by Francisco Maniago who heeded the call of the Kapampangans to rise in arms against the mandatory labor and inequitable bandala system.

5.Baylon tamblot

Tamblot was a babaylan or native priest from Bohol, Philippines, who led the Tamblot Uprising in 1621 to 1622 during the Spanish era. He opposed the new religion spread by the Spaniards and fought against the subsequent conversion of the Boholanos to the Catholic faith.

6.Juan sumuroy

Agustín Sumuroy (better known as Juan Sumuroy) was a Filipino hero and Waray leader of the Sumuroy Rebellion, a rebellion of native Filipinos against colonial Spanish forces that occurred in eastern Visayas in 1649-1650.[1]

7.Francisco dagohoy

Francisco Dagohoy was a Filipino revolutionary who holds the distinction of having initiated the longest revolt in Philippine history, the Dagohoy Rebellion. This rebellion against the Spanish colonial government took place on the island of Bohol from 1744 to 1829, roughly 85 years.
Born: 1724, Inabanga
Died: 1800, Talibon
Full name: Francisco Sendrijas

8.Andres malong

The Malong Revolt was one of the earliest insurrections in the Philippines against the Spanish regime. It was spearheaded by Andres Malong, who was spurred by the uprising in Pampanga. A short-lived revolt, it ended within a span of two months.26 Feb 2021

9.Juan de la cruz

Juan dela Cruz is a 2013 Philippine superhero television series starring Coco Martin. The drama series is broadcast in full high definition and is dubbed to be ABS-CBN's "most expensive and biggest drama" to date.
First episode date: 4 February 2013
Final episode date: 25 October 2013
Number of episodes: 188

10. Diego Silang; was a Filipino revolutionary leader who allied with British forces to overthrow Spanish rule in the northern Philippines and establish an independent Ilocano state.
Born: 16 December 1730, Aringay
Died: 28 May 1763, Bantay
Full name: Diego Silang y Pineda
Cause of death: Assassination
Spouse: Gabriela Silang (m. 1757–1763)
Parents: Miguel Silang, Nicolasa de los Santos
