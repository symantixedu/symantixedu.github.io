28. real life situation(unfair) connect padrino system in real life situation. ​


One real-life situation where the padrino system or patronage system is often observed is in the context of employment or career advancement. In many cases, job seekers or employees are hired, promoted, or given favorable treatment not based on their qualifications, skills, or performance, but rather on their personal connections or relationships with those in power.

For example, a qualified and capable job candidate may be passed over for a position in favor of someone with lesser qualifications who has a personal connection to the hiring manager or decision-maker. Similarly, an employee who performs well and deserves a promotion may be overlooked in favor of a less deserving candidate who has a powerful sponsor or patron.

This system can be unfair as it undermines the merit-based selection and promotion process, and can lead to a lack of diversity and innovation within organizations. It can also create a culture of favoritism, where personal connections and relationships take precedence over professionalism and performance.
