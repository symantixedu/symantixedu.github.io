6. Are the interactions among living things and non-livingthings important? Why?​


Answer:

Living things need nonliving things to survive. Without food, water, and air, living things die. Sunlight, shelter, and soil are also important for living things. Living things meet their needs from living and nonliving things in ecosystems.

Explanation:

The interactions between living things and their non living environment makes up a total ecosystem; understanding any one part of it requires knowledge of how that part interacts with the others.

30. Are there other livingthings beside plant and animals?


The other living things that can be found on Earth, aside plants and animals, are bacteria, protozoans, algae, and fungi organisms. All of these things are considered living organisms because they are living and demonstrate all properties of life, including reproduction, growth, self-sustenance, and some degree of response to stimuli or reaction to things that happen around them.
