4. The work done in pulling a loaded box along a 2 m distance is 150J. What is the force applied in pulling the load?


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The work done by an object exerting a force F at a distance d is given by

[tex]\boxed{W = Fd}[/tex]

where:

W = work

F = force

d = distance

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for F

W = Fd

[tex]F = \dfrac{W}{d}[/tex]

[tex]F = \dfrac{\text{150 J}}{\text{2 m}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{F = \text{75 N}}[/tex]

Therefore, the amount of force being applied is 75 N.

9. A load with mass of 5kg was lifted up by pulley to the height of 0.8m for pile work. What is the potential energy of the load? pls help


The potential energy of the load is 39.24 J.

Step-by-step explanation:

1. Identify the given and required quantities:

mass of the load (m) = 5 kg.

gravitational pull (g) = 9.81 m/s^-2 — constant

height (h) = 0.8 m.

potential energy (Ep) = ?

2. Use the formula:

Ep = m • g • h

3. Substitute the values and solve for the potential energy.

Ep = 5 kg. • 9.81 m/s^-2 • 0.8 m.

Ep = 39.24 J

15. As you pull down on a pulley, the load attached to the pulley is lifted. How has the pulley changed the way work is done? 1. it made the load lighter 2. it made the load heavy 3. it lifted the load quickly 4.it changes the direction of the force


Answer:

The pulley changes the way work is done by changing the direction of the force applied to lift the load. Instead of lifting the load directly, the pulley allows the force to be applied in a different direction, making the work easier to perform. The force applied to the pulley is transferred to the load, allowing the load to be lifted with less effort. The pulley does not make the load lighter or heavier, nor does it lift the load more quickly. However, by changing the direction of the force, the pulley can help to distribute the force more evenly and reduce the amount of force required to lift the load, making the work more efficient.
