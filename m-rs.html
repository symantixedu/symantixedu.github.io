[tex]\large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:}[/tex]

We know that the midpoint formula is:

[tex]\boxed{\sf{M = \bigg( \dfrac{x_1 + x_2}{2}, \dfrac{y_1 + y_2}{2} \bigg)}}[/tex]

Where M is the midpoint of a line segment with endpoints at (x₁, y₁) and (x₂, y₂). Plug in the midpoint M(9, 8) and plug in S(8, 7) for (x₁, y₁). Point R will be at (x₂, y₂).

[tex]\sf{M = \bigg( \dfrac{x_1 + x_2}{2}, \dfrac{y_1 + y_2}{2} \bigg)}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{(9, 8) = \bigg( \dfrac{8 + x_2}{2}, \dfrac{7 + y_2}{2} \bigg)}[/tex]

Take the first coordinate (Red text) from each side of the equation.

[tex]\sf{(\red{9}, 8) = \bigg( \red{\dfrac{8 + x_2}{2}}, \dfrac{7 + y_2}{2} \bigg)}[/tex]

Use those coordinates to solve for x₂.

[tex]\sf{9 = 8 + \dfrac{x_2}{2}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{18 = 8 + x_2 \: \: \: \: \: \: \: (Multiply \: both \: sides \: by \: 2) }[/tex]

[tex]\sf{10 = x_2 \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: (Subtract \: 8 \: from \: both \: sides) }[/tex]

Now take the second coordinate (Purple text) from each side of the equation:

[tex]\sf{(9, \purple{8}) = \bigg( \dfrac{8 + x_2}{2}, \purple{\dfrac{7 + y_2}{2}} \bigg)}[/tex]

Use those coordinates to solve for y₂.

[tex]\sf{8 = 7 + \dfrac{y_2}{2}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{16 = 7 + y_2 \: \: \: \: \: \: \: (Multiply \: both \: sides \: by \: 2) }[/tex]

[tex]\sf{9 = y_2 \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: (Subtract \: 7 \: from \: both \: sides) }[/tex]

Since x₂ = 10 and y₂ = 9, the coordinates of point R are (10, 9).
