2. saan ginagamit ang machinist vise, expensive bit, at backsaw​


Answer:

Machinist vise

An engineer's vise, also known as a metalworking vise, machinist's vise, or, informally, a "bench vise", is used to clamp metal instead of wood. It is used to hold metal when filing or cutting. It is sometimes made of cast steel or malleable cast iron, but most are made of cast iron.

expensive bit

An expansive bit is a tool for boring holes of variable width. Expansive bits are made with adjustable cutters that can be extended or retracted. They were designed to eliminate the need for DIYers to buy sets of drill bits.

backsaw

A backsaw is any hand saw which has a stiffening rib on the edge opposite the cutting edge, enabling better control and more precise cutting than with other types of saws. Backsaws are normally used in woodworking for precise work, such as cutting dovetails, mitres, or tenons in cabinetry and joinery

A bench vise can be exceedingly useful when you utilize a saw to cut wood or metal for your different DIY ventures.

2. When You Need to Work with a Drill

Drilling requires basic dependability and exactness. A bench vise can be utilized to hold the driller at the exact position and in this way drill, the holes required.

3. For a Metalworking Project

Bench vises hold anything you need in place. In the event that you want to bend some iron rods, you will place a metal template to the jaw of the vise, tighten it up, and then you are all set to bend your iron rods.

4. For Woodworking Jobs

In most instances, carpentry works include the utilization of glue for the completing touch. There must be an additional weight separated from the gravitational power, to hold the pieces together, until they stick dry.

5. As You Need a Workbench

A bench vise in a general sense serves all that a workbench does-giving a stronghold. For every valid reason, bench vises can supplant workbenches, contingent upon the material, size and devices utilized.

6. For Cutting an Electric Conduit

In electrical works, cutting plays a pivotal job. Slicing an electrical channel must be sufficiently secure with the goal that a shrewd cut is made.
