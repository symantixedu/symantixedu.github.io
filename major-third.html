B. Root + minor third+ major fifth

In any major chord that involves more than three strings, either the root, third or fifth will be repeated. For major triads, use this as your formula: root, third, fifth. Usually you will see this written like this: 1-3-5.

5. 5. Higher Do in the key of C Major is located in the a. Third line of the staff. b. Third space of the staff. c. Third so-fa syllable.​


Answer:

b. Third space of the staff.

Explanation:

From that sharp, go up to the next line or space – that next line or space will be the name of “do” as well as the name of the key. In the illustration above, the sharp furthest to the right is on the “C” space (so it is C#). The line directly above that space is “D”, so “do” is “D”, and we are in the key of “D”.
