2. 2. A colorful cotton tube skirt that can be worn by both men and women.a. sarongb. malongc, kimono​


Answer:

b.malong

Explanation:

The malong is a traditional Filipino rectangular or tube-like wraparound skirt bearing a variety of geometric or okir designs. The malong is traditionally used as a garment by both men and women of the numerous ethnic groups in the mainland Mindanao and parts of the Sulu Archipelago.

The patadyong (pronounced pa-tad-jóng, also called patadyung, patadjong, habol, or habul), is an indigenous Philippine rectangular or tube-like wraparound skirt worn by both men and women of the ____________________ islands and the Sulu Archipelago, similar to the Malong, or Sarong. 17. 8. This type of malong features the langkit, which are strips with hand-sewn geometric designs. A. malong a andon B. malong a landap C. malong a ampik D. malong a patola​


Answer:

B. malong a landap

Explanation:

The "malong a landap" is characterized by "langkit", or decorative strips of profuse geometric designs, hand-sewn on the malong. This type is considered the most popular. The two narrow "langkit" are called "tobiran" while the wider band is called the "lakban".

22. characteristic of SARONG?


Brightly coloured fabric 4 or 5 yards (up to 4 1/2 m) long is wrapped around the lower part of the body and tucked in or tied at the waist, forming a draped dress or skirt varying in length from knees to ankles.
