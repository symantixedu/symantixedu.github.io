Masjid AL-Dahab​ PRINCIPLE AND HISTORY

Masjid Al-Dahab is ( The Golden Mosque; Filipino: Gintong Masjid) is the largest mosque in Metro Manila. It is located in the largely Muslim area of the Quiapo district in Manila, Philippines.

Because of its gold-painted dome and its position on Globo de Oro Street, the Golden Mosque was given that name. Construction began on August 4, 1976, under the direction of former Philippine First Lady Imelda Marcos, in preparation for the visit of Libyan President Muammar al-Gaddafi, though his visit was canceled. It presently serves a large portion of Manila's Muslim community, and it is particularly busy on Fridays during Jumuah prayers. The mosque has a capacity of 22,000 worshipers.(MUSLIM)

The structure was created for the state visit of Libyan strongman Muammar al-Gaddafi under President Ferdinand Marcos' rule, under the supervision of First Lady Imelda Marcos. The goal was to assist in the mediation of a peace deal with Muslim separatists in Mindanao. The tour did not take place; instead, the First Lady paid a visit to Libya. The mosque endured and eventually grew to become Manila's largest mosque and Islamic center.(MUSLIM)

28. Ilarawan ang disenyo ng Masjid Agung DjenneTagalog dapat​


Ang Mahusay na Mosque ng Djenné (Pranses: Grande mosquée de Djenné, Arabik: الجامع الكبير في جينيه) ay isang malaking gusali ng banco o adobe na isinasaalang-alang ng maraming mga arkitekto na isa sa mga pinakadakilang tagumpay ng istilong arkitektura ng Sudano-Sahelian. Ang mosque ay matatagpuan sa lungsod ng Djenné, Mali, sa kapatagan ng baha ng Ilog Bani. Ang unang mosque sa site ay itinayo noong ika-13 siglo, ngunit ang kasalukuyang istraktura ay nagmula noong 1907. Pati na rin ang pagiging sentro ng pamayanan ng Djenné, ito ay isa sa pinakatanyag na palatandaan sa Africa. Kasabay ng "Old Towns of Djenné" itinalaga ito bilang isang World Heritage Site ng UNESCO noong 1988.

29. proper etiquette to come in masjid and waiting for the standing preyer​


Answer:

People will stand, bow, prostrate and sit in unison at different points during the prayer. You don't have to join in; you can just observe quietly. When a Muslim is praying, they will not talk or respond to you until they have completed the prayer. You can ask questions before or after.


30. masjid (arkeitekturang Muslim) kultura​


Answer:

Ang isang mosque ( masjid sa Arabic) ay isang lugar ng pagsamba sa Islam. Kahit na ang mga panalangin ay maaaring gawin nang pribado, alinman sa loob ng bahay o sa labas, halos lahat ng komunidad ng mga Muslim ay naglalaan ng espasyo o gusali para sa panalangin ng congregational. Ang mga pangunahing arkitektural na bahagi ng isang moske ay praktikal sa layunin at nagbibigay ng parehong pagpapatuloy at isang pakiramdam ng tradisyon sa mga Muslim sa buong mundo.
