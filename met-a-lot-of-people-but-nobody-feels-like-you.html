Some S-V-A (Subject Verb Agreement) rules:

Rule 1: A verb must agree with its subject in number.

1. A singular subject takes a singular verb.

Examples:

The child sings.

The priest celebrates the Holy Mass.

2. A plural subject takes a plural verb.

Examples:

The children sing.

Priests celebrate Holy Masses.

3. The helping verb must agree in number with the subject.

Examples:

I have prepared some gifts.

The department store is selling items on discount.

4. Compound Subjects

• When two subjects are referring to different entities or ideas joined by and, the verb is plural.

Example: My coach and my father are speaking to each other about my summer basketball training

schedule.

• When subjects are joined by and but refer only to one person or thing, the verb must be singular.

Example: My father and coach supports my love for basketball.

• Use a singular verb with a collective noun that is thought of as a unit. Use a plural verb with a collective noun of which members in a group are thought of as separate individuals.

Examples: The staff meets for monthly evaluation

The company's staff plan their advertising strategies.

Rule 2: The verb must agree with the subject even with intervening phrases and in inverted sentences.

• The agreement of a verb with its subject is not changed by interrupting words

Examples: My mother in the province, as well as my brothers abroad, calls me every day

The families of college students in the province send them love through calls and emails.

• The subject and the verb of an inverted sentence must agree in number

Examples: There goes Lily in her pink pajamas.

There go Lily and her friends in their pink pajamas.

Rule 3: Indefinite pronouns and special cases

• A verb must agree in number with an indefinite pronoun used

Singular:

another, anybody, anyone, anything, each, either, everybody, everyone, everything, little, much,

neither nobody, no one, nothing, one, other, somebody

Plural: both, few, many, others, several

Singular or plural depending on who or what they refer to all any, more, most, none, some

Example: None was left on the plate.

• A subject that expresses an amount, measurement or weight is usually singular and takes a

singular verb.

Example: Ten kilos of rice is enough to feed the children at the orphanage

• Use a singular verb with subjects that are plural in form but singular in meaning

Example: Physics is the subject that my brother loves best.

• A title is singular and takes a singular verb.

Example: Cloudy with a Chance of Meatballs was written by Judi Barrett.

18. How does the speaker feel about discovering another nobody? in the poem Nobody​


Answer:

Put generally, the speaker believes that it's better to be "Nobody" and that it's actually quite boring—"dreary"—to be "Somebody." This is where the anaphora comes in—it helps create this sense of boredom and weariness, as though the speaker can hardly put up with the idea of another "Somebody" in the world.
