1. What could be the symbolism behind the melting milkshake? could this be a commentary on the american influence of the philippines?


MILKSHAKE AS SYMBOL OF AMERICAN INFLUENCE IN THE PHILIPPINES

The melted milkshake represents the American people's influence on Filipino culture because this influence was instantaneous and quickly embraced by the Filipinos.

In 1922, Ivar "Pop" Coulson, a Walgreens employee in Chicago, took an old-fashioned malted milk (milk, chocolate, and malt) and added two scoops of ice cream, creating a drink that became popular, quickly becoming a high-demand drink for young adults across the country.

Milkshakes were a popular drink in malt shops by the 1930s. After the invention of freon-cooled refrigerators provided a safe, reliable way of automatically making and dispensing ice cream in the 1930s, milkshake automation began.

Milkshakes were just among the influences of the Americans which fitted right in the Philippines. The usage of cow or carabao milk as the availability of the ingredients. What's more is that because of its tropical climate, Milkshake was a good beverage to refresh during summers.

America has had a significant impact on Philippine culture.

The most notable American influence on Filipino cuisine was the introduction of convenience foods such as canned foods, pre-packaged foods, and fast food. Canned goods were first introduced to the Philippines during WWII and has since become a staple in Filipino breakfasts.

19. pricing strategy for business plan like milkshakes etc.


Answer:

When developing a pricing strategy for a business plan, particularly for products like milkshakes, it's essential to consider various factors such as costs, competition, target market, and perceived value. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you develop a pricing strategy for your milkshake business:

1. Determine your costs: Calculate all the costs associated with producing and selling milkshakes. This includes ingredients, packaging, labor, overhead expenses, and any other relevant costs. Make sure to consider both variable costs (those that change with the quantity produced) and fixed costs (those that remain constant regardless of production levels).

2. Understand your target market: Identify your target customers and gain insights into their preferences, buying behavior, and price sensitivity. Determine what value proposition your milkshakes offer and how they differ from competitors' products. Consider factors like quality, taste, variety, convenience, and any unique selling points.

3. Analyze the competition: Research your competitors' pricing strategies to understand the current market dynamics. Identify whether you want to position your milkshakes as premium products or target a more price-sensitive segment. Consider the pricing range of similar products and determine where you want to position your offerings relative to them.

4. Determine your pricing objectives: Define your pricing objectives based on your business goals. Are you aiming for market share, profit maximization, or a balance between the two? Your objectives will influence the pricing decisions you make.

5. Choose a pricing approach: There are several pricing approaches you can consider, such as cost-based pricing, value-based pricing, competitive pricing, or a combination of these. Each approach has its pros and cons, so select the one that aligns with your business goals and market positioning.

6. Set your initial price: Based on the above considerations, determine the initial price for your milkshakes. Consider factors such as your costs, target market, perceived value, and competition. Keep in mind that your pricing strategy may evolve over time based on customer feedback, market response, and changing business dynamics.

7. Monitor and adjust: Regularly evaluate your pricing strategy's effectiveness and monitor its impact on sales, profitability, and customer satisfaction. Collect feedback from customers and make adjustments as needed. Consider offering promotions, discounts, or bundling options to attract new customers and retain existing ones.

8. Continuously refine your pricing strategy: As your business grows and market conditions change, refine your pricing strategy accordingly. Regularly analyze your costs, monitor competitor pricing, stay attuned to customer preferences, and adapt to market trends to ensure your pricing remains competitive and profitable.

Remember, pricing is a critical aspect of your overall business strategy, so it's important to strike a balance between profitability and customer value. Regularly review and refine your pricing strategy to stay competitive and meet the evolving needs of your target market.
