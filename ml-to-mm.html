2. Boyle's LawSolvea. P1= 150 mL V1= 550 mm Hg P2= 400 mm Hg V2 = ?b. P1= 400 mLV1= 600 mm Hg V2 = 400 mL P2= ?c. P1= 500 mm Hg V1= 500 mL P2= 800 mm Hg V2= ? ​


Answer:

Boyle's Law states that the pressure and volume of a gas have an inverse relationship, assuming the temperature and amount of gas remain constant. The formula for Boyle's Law is P1V1 = P2V2, where P1 and V1 are the initial pressure and volume, and P2 and V2 are the final pressure and volume.

a. Given P1 = 150 mL, V1 = 550 mmHg, P2 = 400 mmHg, and V2 = ?

Using the formula: P1V1 = P2V2

150 mL x 550 mmHg = 400 mmHg x V2

V2 = (150 mL x 550 mmHg) / 400 mmHg

V2 = 206.25 mL

b. Given P1 = 400 mL, V1 = 600 mmHg, V2 = 400 mL, and P2 = ?

Using the formula: P1V1 = P2V2

400 mL x 600 mmHg = P2 x 400 mL

P2 = (400 mL x 600 mmHg) / 400 mL

P2 = 600 mmHg

c. Given P1 = 500 mmHg, V1 = 500 mL, P2 = 800 mmHg, and V2 = ?

Using the formula: P1V1 = P2V2

500 mmHg x 500 mL = 800 mmHg x V2

V2 = (500 mmHg x 500 mL) / 800 mmHg

V2 = 312.5 mL

3. 5.500.0 mL of a gas is collected at 745.0 mm Hg. What will the volume be at 760 mm Hg?​


Answer:

We can use Boyle's law to solve the problem, assuming constant temperature.

P1V1 = P2V2

Where:

P1 = initial pressure

V1 = initial volume

P2 = final pressure

V2 = final volume

Plugging in the given values, we have:

P1 = 745.0 mm Hg

V1 = 500.0 mL

P2 = 760 mm Hg

V2 = ?

745.0 mm Hg × 500.0 mL = 760 mm Hg × V2

372500.0 mm Hg · mL = 760 mm Hg · V2

V2 = 372500.0 mm Hg · mL / 760 mm Hg

V2 = 490.13 mL

Therefore, the volume of the gas at 760 mm Hg would be approximately 490.13 mL.

10. A sample of gas occupies a volume of 450.0 mL at 740 mm Hg and 16°C. Determine the volume of this sample at 760 mm Hg and 37°C.


Problem:

A sample of gas occupies a volume of 450.0 mL at 740 mm Hg and 16°C. Determine the volume of this sample at 760 mm Hg and 37°C.

Note:

To solve problem in gas law, temperature must be in kelvin unit. We have to convert degree celsius to kelvin by the formula ( Kelving = °C  +273.15).

Kelvin = 16°C + 273.15 = 289.15 K

Kelvin = 37°C + 273.15 = 310.15 K

Given Data:

P₁ (initial pressure) = 740 mmHg         P₂ (final pressure) = 760 mmHg

T₁ (initial temperature ) = 289.15 K      T₂ (final temperature)  = 310.15 K

V₁ (initial volume)  =  450 ml                 V₂ (final volume) = ? unknown

Solution:

We can use the formula  V₂  =  P₁ × T₂ × V₁  / P₂T₁

V₂  =  P₁ × T₂ × V₁  / P₂T₁

     = 740 mmHg  × 310.15 K × 450 ml  / 760 mmHg ×  289.15 K

     = 103, 279, 950 ml / 219,754

     = 469.98 ml

Answer:

Final volume is equal to  469.98 ml

14. What is the old volume in liters at 810 mm Hg when the new volume is 350.0 mL at 740.0 mm Hg?


The old volume in liters : 0.32 L

Further explanation

In Boyle's law, it is stated that  

In a closed system, the absolute pressure by an ideal gas is inversely proportional to the volume it occupies if the temperature is constant  

 

[tex]\rm p_1V_1=p_2.V_2\\\\\dfrac{p_1}{p_2}=\dfrac{V_2}{V_1}[/tex]

From the problem :

V₁ = 350 ml = 0.35 L

P₁ = 740 mmHg

P₂ = 810 mmHg

Then :

[tex]\rm V_2=\dfrac{P_1\times V_1}{P_2}\\\\V_2=\dfrac{740\times 0.35}{810}\\\\V_2=\boxed{\bold{0.32~L}}[/tex]

16. A gas has a volume of 460 mL at 500 mm Hg. What will be the volume at 1.2 atm? (252.2 mL)


Given:

[tex]P_{1} = \text{500 mm Hg}[/tex]

[tex]V_{1} = \text{80 mL}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \text{1.2 atm} × \frac{\text{760 mm Hg}}{\text{1 atm}} = \text{912 mm Hg}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]V_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

Using Boyle's Law, the final volume of a gas can be written as

[tex]P_{1}V_{1} = P_{2}V_{2}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = V_{1} × \frac{P_{1}}{P_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{460 mL} × \frac{\text{500 mm Hg}}{\text{912 mm Hg}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{V_{2} = \text{252.2 mL}}[/tex]

25. The pressure of a gas is compressed from 1300.0 mm Hg to 950.0 mm Hg as moving a piston from 205.0 mL to 330.0 mL. What would the final temperature be if the original temperature was 35.0 °C?


Given:

[tex]P_{1} = \text{1300.0 mm Hg}[/tex]

[tex]T_{1} = \text{35.0°C + 273.15 = 308.15 K}[/tex]

[tex]V_{1} = \text{205.0 mL}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \text{950.0 mm Hg}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{330.0 mL}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]T_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\frac{P_{1}V_{1}}{T_{1}} = \frac{P_{2}V_{2}}{T_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = T_{1} × \frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}} × \frac{V_{2}}{V_{1}}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = \text{308.15 K} × \frac{\text{950.0 mm Hg}}{\text{1300.0 mm Hg}} × \frac{\text{330.0 mL}}{\text{205.0 mL}}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = \text{362.495 K}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = \text{362.495 K} - 273.15[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{T_{2} = \text{89.3°C}}[/tex]
