1. Peter bought a high-end laptop computer that costs 65000 pesos on installment basis. He paid 20000 pesos down payment. The remaining balance was paid in 36 monthly installment basis at 15% compounded monthly. How much is the monthly payment.


65,000 minus 20,000 = 45,000 ÷ 36 =1,250 is the monthly payment


The formula for calculating the fixed loan installment P is

[tex]P=\frac{Lq^n(q-1)}{q^n-1}[/tex]

where:

L = value of the loan (in this question L = 65000 - 20000 = 45000)

n = number of loan repayments

q = 1  + m,  where m = y / (12 * 100)  is mouthly interest rate in the form of the number (in our question yearly interest rate y = 15%)

[tex]q=1+\frac{15}{12\cdot100}=1+\frac{1}{80}=\frac{81}{80}\\\\P=\frac{45000\cdot(\frac{81}{80})^{36}\cdot\frac{1}{80}}{(\frac{81}{80})^{36}-1}=1559.94\ pesos[/tex]

14. 1. At 8% converted monthly, what single payment now is equivalent to 24 monthly payments of PHP 3500.00 each, the first payment being due at the end of the 3 month?​


Answer:

—This is an annuity problem with an interest rate of 8% per month. To solve for the present value, we can use the formula:

PV = PMT * ( (1 - (1 + i)^-n) / i)

where PV is the present value, PMT is the monthly payment, i is the interest rate (8% per month in decimal form, which is 0.08), and n is the number of months (24 in this case).

Plugging in the values:

PV = 3500 * ( (1 - (1 + 0.08)^-24) / 0.08)

PV = 3500 * ( (1 - 0.0422) / 0.08)

PV = 3500 * 0.9599

PV = PHP 3319.65

Therefore, a single payment of PHP 3319.65 now is equivalent to 24 monthly payments of PHP 3500.00 each, the first payment being due at the end of the 3 month.
