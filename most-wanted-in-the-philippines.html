If in the ocean floor, an oceanographer notices a portion in the ocean floor that is deeper than the rest of the ocean floor it is most likely an OCEAN TRENCH.

To understand how ocean trenches are formed an understanding of the Plate Tectonic Theory must be gained. The tectonic plate theory posits that the outermost layer of the Earth, called the crust, is composed of “plates”. There are two types of plates, the oceanic plates, and the continental plates. In prehistoric times all continents used to be one big continental plate called the Pangaea. Over time, movements from the Earth’s second layer, the mantle, caused this prehistoric supercontinent to break up and eventually settle into the present

The movement and collision of these plates cause various landforms and ocean forms to be made. A type of collision of continental plates is the reason for the formation of mountains.

In the case of ocean trenches, it is formed through a type of interaction between oceanic plates where one plate goes under called subduction.

26. you are an oceanographer and want to map the ocean floor on the east coast of the Philippines. as you do your study, you notice that there is a portion in the ocean floor which is relatively much deeper than the rest. what most likely is that deeper part. - Linear Sea - Oceanic Ridge - Rift Valley - Trench


ANSWER:

The correct answer is Trench. A trench is the portion in the ocean floor which is relatively much deeper than the rest of the ocean floor.

EXPLANATION:

Linear sea is not the correct answer because linear sea is no way related to uneven ocean floor. Linear sea is the result of the increase in length and depth of a rift valley due to the continues spreading of the plates.

Oceanic ridge is not the correct answer because it is a seafloor or underwater mountain range. Clearly, it does not point to a depression in the ocean floor which is what we are looking.

Rift valley is not the correct because rift valley points to the fracture of the Earth's surface that widens over time.
