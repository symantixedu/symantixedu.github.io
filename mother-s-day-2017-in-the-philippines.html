1. according to cfo 2017 how many filipino leave the philippines every day


2.3 million of emigrant Filipinos leave the Philippines everyday

The total number of Overseas Filipino Workers (OFWs) who worked abroad between April and September 2017 was estimated to be 2.3 million. Overseas Contract Workers (OCWs), or those with existing work contracts, made up 97.0 percent of all OFWs from April to September 2017. The remainder (3.0%) worked without contracts in other countries.

TOP DESTINATION

The Arabic world is the top destination for emigration.

Kingdom of Saudi Arabia and Arab countries(25.2%)East Asian Countries: Hong Kong, China, Japan, South Korea, Taiwan (6.5%)Singapore (5.3%)Australia (1.6%)Europe  (6.4%)Americas (5.4%)Africa (1.1%)Other Asian Countries (20.8%)

GENDER OF IMIGRANTS

More female OFWs than male OFWs exist.

Females had a higher proportion of OFWs (53.7%) than males (46.3%). The age group 30 to 34 years comprised the largest proportion of OFWs, accounting for 21.7 percent of all OFWs, followed by those aged 25 to 29 years, accounting for 20.4 percent. Female OFWs were younger than male OFWs. Female OFWs had a higher percentage (24.1%) in the age group 25 to 29 years, while male OFWs had a higher percentage (19.8%) in the age group 30 to 34 years. Male OFWs outnumbered female OFWs in the 35-and-up age group.

19. cybersecurity law 2017 philippines need conclusion ​


Answer:

In conclusion, the Cybersecurity Law of 2017 is an important step towards ensuring the safety and security of the Philippines' digital landscape. It seeks to protect critical information infrastructure, prevent cybercrime, and establish a framework for cybersecurity management in the country. While there have been concerns about its potential impact on online privacy and freedom of expression, the law provides measures to balance these rights with the need for security.
