Mount Kalugong is named as such because it resembles the shape of a hat if viewed from the town center. “Kalugong” means hat in the local term. There are many best sights to see in Mount Kalugong. Like Pine forests cover most of the summit of Mt Kalugong. That gives the place a relatively cooler and refreshing atmosphere. There are tables under the shade of pine trees where you can hold a family picnic. There’s just nothing like it sitting on one of the benches while relishing the rustic sight of the pine forests. Limestone rock formations abound atop Mt Kalugong, especially along the mountain’s edges. I could not help but wonder why these rocks are atop a mountain when they’re supposed to be found on sea floors or at least, on coastal areas.
