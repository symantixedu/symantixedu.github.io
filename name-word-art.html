EXPLANATION

» It originates from the Greek mousike, which combines the words "art" and "muse", to refer to the female characters of Greek mythology who had the mission to please the Gods of Olympus. ... Despite this, music as a fully developed cultural manifestation began in Ancient Greece.

» Guido d'Arezzo was the creator of modern notation, with the creation of the tetragrammaton, ending with the use of neumes in the History of Music, and he baptized the musical notes with the names we know today: do, re, mi, fa, sol, lá e si (before, ut, re, mi, fa, sol, la and san), based on a sacred Latin text of the hymn to Saint John the Baptist: [1]

THE ANSWER!1 - A.) Art of the muses.2 - D. Hymn to Saint John the Baptist

Why?

Art of the muses.The term music in its Greek origin makes a reference to the female personalities of Greek mythology, their objective was to please the gods with music and dances, so the term mousikê means something similar to the art of the muses.

Hymn to Saint John the BaptistMusical notes were created by Guido D'Arezzo, from Latin. He was inspired by a hymn to Saint John the Baptist, also in Latin, which at each beginning of a sentence had a specific sound, which are the same musical notes known today.
