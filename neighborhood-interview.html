Yes, neighborhood communities exist. A neighborhood community is a group of people who live in the same area or neighborhood and who share common interests, concerns, and goals. These communities can be formal or informal and can be organized around a variety of factors, such as geography, culture, or shared interests. Neighborhood communities can provide social support, promote a sense of belonging and identity, and help residents address issues and challenges in their local area.
