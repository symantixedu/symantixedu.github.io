4. Research about Net Wall Games ​


Answer:

Net wall games refer to a group of sports and physical activities that involve the use of a net or wall as a primary component of the game. These games are typically played on a court or field and involve two teams or players who score points by hitting a ball or other object over the net or wall. Examples of net wall games include:

Volleyball: A team sport in which two teams of six players hit a ball back and forth over a high net using their hands, arms, and bodies.

Tennis: A racket sport in which two players (or teams of two) hit a ball back and forth over a net using racket.

Badminton: A racket sport in which two or four players hit a shuttlecock back and forth over a high net using lightweight rackets.

Squash: a racket sport in which two players hit a small rubber ball back and forth over a high front wall using racket.

Table Tennis: also known as ping-pong, is a sport in which two or four players hit a lightweight, hollow ball back and forth over a net using small rackets.

Net wall games are popular around the world and are played at various levels of competition, from casual play to professional leagues. These games can be enjoyed by people of all ages and skill levels and are considered to be an excellent form of physical activity, promoting cardiovascular health, flexibility, and coordination.

Net wall games have a lot of benefits for players including endurance, reaction time, balance, and coordination. Additionally, these games are also known to improve mental well-being, as they are considered a good stress buster, and a good way to improve social connections
