CALCULATING EQUILIBRIUM CONCENTRATION

A chemical reaction is considered to be in a condition of chemical equilibrium when both the reagents and the byproducts are in a concentration that does not change over time any more. The forward reaction rate and the backward reaction rate are equal in this state.

The chemical compound nocl in this instance is undergoing a chemical reaction to split into two distinct compounds. The following equation can be used to model the reaction:

The formula 2 NOCL (g) = 2 NO (g) + CL2 (g)

The concentrations of the reactants (NOCL) and byproducts (NO and CL2) will not change at equilibrium. You can determine that 28.0% of the initial amount of nocl has been transformed into NO and CL2 by looking at the proportion of nocl that has dissociated (28.0%).

You can use the procedures below to determine how many nocl moles have separated:

Divide the amount of nocl that has dissociated by 100 to get the  percentage as a decimal:

28.0% / 100% = 0.280

To determine how many nocl moles have dissociated, multiply the decimal by the initial mole count:

0.280 * 2.50 moles = 0.700 moles.

Therefore, at equilibrium, 0.700 moles of nocl have dissociated. The number of moles of NO and CL2 that have been created can be calculated using the mole ratios from the balanced chemical equation.

For instance, if 0.700 moles of nocl have dissociated, then 0.700 moles of NO have also been created (since the mole ratio between nocl and NO is 2:2). Additionally, 0.700 moles of CL2 would have been created (since the mole ratio between nocl and CL2 is 1:1).

3. . Nitrosyl chloride, NOCl, decomposes to NO and Cl2 at high temperatures. 2NOCl(g) ⇌ 2 NO(g) + Cl2(g) Suppose you place 2.00 mol NOCl in a 1.00-L flask and raise the temperature to 462°C. When equilibrium has been established, 0.66 mol NO is present. Calculate the equilibrium constant Kc for the decomposition reaction from these data.


Answer:

To calculate the equilibrium constant Kc for the reaction 2NOCl(g) ⇌ 2 NO(g) + Cl2(g), we need to use the equilibrium concentrations of the reactants and products.

Since the volume of the flask is 1.00 L, the concentration of NOCl at equilibrium is 2.00 mol / 1.00 L = 2.00 M. The concentration of NO at equilibrium is 0.66 mol / 1.00 L = 0.66 M. The concentration of Cl2 at equilibrium is also 0.66 M, since the ratio of NO to Cl2 in the products is 2:1.

With these concentrations, we can now calculate the equilibrium constant Kc:

Kc = ([NO]^2 [Cl2]) / [NOCl]^2

= (0.66^2 * 0.66) / (2.00^2)

= 0.22 / 4.00

= 0.055

Therefore, the equilibrium constant Kc for the decomposition of NOCl is 0.055.
