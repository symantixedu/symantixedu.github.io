3. An object at rest started to travel at 10 m/s south in 10 s, what is theacceleration of the object?​


First, let’s assume the object starts with a velocity vi=0 , and furthermore for the first part let’s also assume a uniform acceleration. I’m also assuming you are asking this for a non-calculus based course.

Part 1:

Over 10 seconds, the velocity increases from vi=0 to vf=10 m/s, or in other words, each second the velocity increases by 1 m/s. This is an acceleration of a=1 m/s/s. Using the kinetics equation:

vf=vi+at

10=0+a(10)

a=1

Part 2:

The distance traveled can be found using:

d=vi+vf2t

d=102(1)=50

Or, alternatively:

d=vit+12at2

d=0(1)+12(1)(102)

d=50

7. An applied force of 3N, right is applied to a stationary object. What is the effect of this force if starting friction is 0.5N, left? A. Object will move to the left B. Object will move to the right C. Object will remain on its position D. Object will gain velocity of 2.5 m/s


NET FORCE

B. Object will move to the right

Explanation:

Newton's first law of motion or law of inertia states that an object at rest will stay at rest and an object in motion will continue to be in motion not unless acted by an outside unbalanced force. The unbalanced force refers to that force that is not canceled or balanced by another force. This can be determined by the net force which is the vector sum of all the forces that act upon the object. It means it is the sum of all the forces taking into account the magnitude and direction.

In the question, the object which is initially at rest was acted by two forces - 3N to the right and 0.5-N force to the left. We can represent 3N with a positive sign since it is moving to the right and a negative sign for frictional force (- 0.5 N). Combining the two forces will give us a net force of + 2.5N force:

         Net Force = +3 N + (-0.5N)

         Net Force = +2.5N

This will cause the movement of the object to the direction of the net force which is right. Thus, the object is subjected to 2.5N-force to the right.

vf = 30 m/s

vf = vi + at

Substituting all the values and solving for a

30 m/s = 0 + a(60 s)

a(60 s) = 30 m/s

a = 30 m/s / 60 s

Therefore, the acceleration is

Problem:

A car starts from rest and accelerates uniformly to reach a speed of 21 m/s in 7 seconds. What was the speed of object after 2 seconds?

Given Data:

Vi (initial velocity) = 0 m/s

Vf (final velocity)  = 21 m/s

t (time)    = 7 seconds

a (acceleration ) = ? unknown

Solution:

First:

We have to find the acceleration using the formula a = Vf-Vi / t

a = Vf-Vi / t

  = 21 m/s - 0 m/s / 7 s

  = 21 m/s / 7 s

  = 3 m/s^2

Second:

We can the final velocity of an object in 2 seconds using the formula

Vf = at + Vi

    = 3 m/s^2 × 2 seconds + 0 m/s

    = 6 m/s + 0 m/s

    = 6 m/s

Answer

The velocity in 2 seconds is 6 m/s

24. How long will it take for an object starting at rest to travel a distance of 100 meters if it will accelerate at a constant rate of 1.5 m/s²?Help me please. ​


KINEMATICS MATHEMATICAL EQUATIONS

How long will it take for an object starting at rest to travel a distance of 100 meters if it will accelerate at a constant rate of 1.5 m/s²?

Given:

Initial Velocity (Vᵢ) = 0 (at rest)Acceleration = 1.5 m/s²Displacement (d) = 100 meters

Required:

Time (t)

Solution:

Let us first summarize all the kinematics equations for constant acceleration motion, (Please see the Picture).

Looking at the given variables, we can use the following equation in order to solve for t:

[tex] \tt \: d = Vᵢt \: + \: \frac{1}{2} at^{2} [/tex]

(Because V, is zero, V,t expression can be cancelled)

[tex] \tt \: d = \frac{1}{2} at^{2} [/tex]

Deriving the expression to get t, will result to:

[tex] \tt \: t \: = \sqrt{ \frac{2d}{a} } \\ [/tex][tex] \large{ \boxed{ \tt{t = 11.55 \: s}}}[/tex]
