5. colors green orange and purple these are formed by mixing the primary colors​


Answer:

Primary colors are arbitrary and you can have as many as you like and they can be any color you like. The only issue is that the less you have, the more limited a color gamut, or range, you can achieve by using them. Using orange, green, and purple, say for printing, would pretty much negate the possibility of producing yellow at all. A mustard color would result from combining your orange and green but since colors can never lighten each other on the page (or canvas), a light yellow would impossible to create. Reds and Blues would also be fairly muted compared with what you’d get using Cyan, Magenta, and Yellow, to say nothing of Magentas and Cyans. That’s just how color mixing works.

17. : The ratio of orange and purple glasses is 4 : 2. If there are 60 glasses in all, howmany purple glasses are there?​


Reklamo:

Putakte tagal ng sagot

In the given problem, apply your knowledge in algebra and basic mathematics specially in solving word problem about ratio and proportion.

Given

60 glasses in all

The ratio of orange and purple glasses is 4 : 2.

Problem

How many purple glasses are there?

Solution

Let x is the number of orange glasses, and y is the number of purple glasses.

4:2 = x:y → 4/2 = x/y

x + y = 60

x = 60-y

4/2 = x/y

4/2 = 60-y/y

4y = 120 - 2y

4y + 2y = 120

6y = 120

y = 120/6

y = 20

x = 60-y

x = 60-20

x = 40

Answer

20 purple glasses
