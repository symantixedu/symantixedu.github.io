1. Description of body parts of butter fly​


Answer:

Anatomy - A butterfly's three body parts are the head, thorax (the chest), and abdomen (the tail end). The butterfly's body is covered by tiny sensory hairs. The four wings and the six legs of the butterfly are attached to the thorax. The thorax contains the muscles that make the legs and wings move.

24. body parts /spider/fly mosquito1.body segment/ /2.number of feet/______/​


Answer:

1. Spiders have only two body segment (tagmata).

Mosquitoes have three major body parts: head, thorax and abdomen.

2. Spiders typically have eight walking legs.

Mosquitoes have six legs like other insects.

Explanation:

1. Unlike an insect, the spider's body is in two sections. The head and thorax, bearing the eyes, mouthparts and legs, are fused together to form the cephalothorax.

The head is the sensory center for the mosquito, where the compound eyes and antennae are located, as well as the proboscis used for feeding.

2. They do not have antennae; the pair of appendages in front of the legs are the pedipalps (or just palps). Spiders' legs are made up of seven segments. Starting from the body end, these are the coxa, trochanter, femur, patella, tibia, metatarsus and tarsus.
