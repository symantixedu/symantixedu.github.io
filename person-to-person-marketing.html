1. what is the meaning of person marketing


A person whose duties include the identification of the goods and services desired by a set of consumers, as well as the marketing of those goods and services on behalf of a company. See also advertising.



2. 5 example of person marketing​


Answer:

1.Influencer Marketing: This involves collaborating with social media influencers who have a large following to promote your product or service to their audience. This is an effective way of reaching a targeted audience and building brand awareness.

2.Personal Branding: This involves creating a unique brand image around oneself as a person. It involves showcasing your skills, experience, and achievements to position yourself as an expert in your field.

3.Thought Leadership: This involves sharing your insights and knowledge on a particular topic or industry through content creation, speaking engagements, and networking. By positioning yourself as a thought leader, you can gain credibility and attract new clients or job opportunities.

21. What is your personal meaning of Marketing?​


Answer:

Marketing is the process of understanding the needs of customers and creating products or services that meet those needs in order to drive profitable growth. It involves research, analysis, planning, execution, and measurement of effective strategies to increase brand awareness, create customer loyalty, and drive sales.

Explanation:

Pa brainliest po

Answer:

When we think of the term “marketing,” it’s easy to think of it as simply a way to promote a product, service, or brand and increase its sales. While that’s certainly one of the core principles of marketing, it’s actually much more than that. Marketing is an essential part of a company's success, and its meaning extends beyond generating sales and profits.

At the root, marketing is about relationships and creating a bond between the company and its target audience. The goal of marketing is to create compelling messages that connect with an audience in an emotional, engaging way. This is done by understanding their wants, needs, and pain points and then offering them a solution in the form of a product, service, or brand.

To achieve this, marketers use a variety of tools, such as market research, advertising, public relations, and customer service, to build and maintain relationships. Market research allows marketers to gain insight into the needs of a target audience and develop products and services that will meet their needs. Advertising then helps to spread awareness and promote the product or service to the right audiences. Public relations is used to help the company gain credibility and build relationships with the public. Finally, customer service ensures that customers are satisfied and remain loyal to the brand.

Overall, the goal of marketing is to create relationships between companies and their customers by understanding their needs and delivering what they want. It is so much more than just selling a product or service—it’s about connecting with customers and building trust. Marketing is about creating an emotional connection between a company and its customers, so that customers become loyal and keep coming back. When a company does this successfully, it solidifies its place in the market and builds its brand.

27. List down (5) Examples of:•Person Marketing•Place Marketing•Cause Marketing•Event Marketing•Organization Marketing​


Answer:

1.Marketing activity aimed at creating target market awareness, and a favourable opinion, of a particular person.

2.Place marketing is a kind of marketing strategy to do business in a particular place. Taking it as a strategy marketers use its local attractions, cultures, business opportunities, economic and political power to attract people from different places of the world and invest their money in the business at that place.

3.Cause Marketing is defined as a type of Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) in which a company's promotional campaign has the dual purpose of increasing profitability while bettering society. Or, more colloquially: cause marketing occurs when a company does well by doing good.

4.Event marketing is the experiential marketing of a brand, service, or product through memorable experiences or promotional events. It typically involves direct interaction with a brand's representatives. It should not be confused with event management, which is a process of organizing, promoting and conducting events.

5.Organizational marketing can be defined as business to business marketing or B2B marketing. It means that the targeted customer of any organization would also be an organization rather than an individual. In simple words, it is important for the seller and the buyer to be an organization for marketing.
