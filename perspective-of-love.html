30. Why do people fall in love in biological perspective?


Answer:

Neuroscience also tells us that when you"re falling in love, your hormones go through a period of elation. Basically, the brain produces more “monoamines". Specifically, your body produces more norepinephrine, dopamine, and serotonin. Each one of these hormones causes different reactions that provoke certain behaviors.

One can see that, with both the involvement of emotion and thoughts, and stimulating the hypothalamus to release hormones, biologically speaking, dopamine is crucial to falling in love.
