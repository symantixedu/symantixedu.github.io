PNRI: PHILIPPINE NUCLEAR RESEARCH INSTITUTEBRIEF DESCRIPTIONPNRI is under DOST (Department of Science and Technology)

ROLE

Its purpose calls for the development and control of nuclear science and technology's safe and peaceful applications in the Philippines.Users of ionizing radiation can get services from the Radiation Protection Services Section (RPSS) of PNRI to monitor, evaluate, and manage radiation levels and exposures in order to support public and worker safety.Users of radioisotopes at academic and research institutions, hospitals and medical institutions, and other industrial firms are frequently trained in nuclear science and technology and radiation protection by the Philippine Nuclear Research Institute (PNRI).

IONIZING RADIATIONIonizing radiation is a type of energy that works by taking electrons away from the atoms and molecules of various substances, such as water, biological tissue, and air. Ionizing radiation can pass through these materials and move undetectably. In the diagram below, it is on the right side of the electromagnetic spectrum.

EFFECT ON THE BODYIonizing radiation may harm cells and genetic material when it interacts with them (i.e., deoxyribonucleic acid, or DNA). If this damage is not successfully repaired, the cell may die or the DNA may alter in a way that is potentially hazardous (i.e., mutations).

13. RESEARCH ABOUT BATAAN NUCLEAR POWER PLANT


Answer:

The Bataan Nuclear Power Plant is a nuclear power plant, completed but never fueled, on the Bataan Peninsula, 100 kilometers (62 mi) west of Manila, Philippines. It is located on a 3.57 km2 (1.38 sq mi) government reservation at Napot Point in Morong, Bataan. It was the Philippines' only attempt at building a nuclear power plant. It was mothballed due to safety concerns in the wake of the Chernobyl disaster in Ukraine in 1986 and issues regarding corruption.
