7. how to avoid physical bullying​


Answer:

Here are some steps that can help someone avoid physical bullying:

Try to avoid being alone: Physical bullies often target people who are alone and more vulnerable. Being with a group of friends or in a public place can make it harder for bullies to physically bully you.Speak up: If you are being physically bullied, it is important to speak up and tell someone. This can be a teacher, counselor, or a trusted adult. They can help you find ways to stay safe and intervene in the situation.Stand up for yourself: It's important to stand up for yourself and assertively but calmly let the bully know that their behavior is not acceptable. This can help to show the bully that you are not an easy target.Learn self-defense: Knowing how to defend yourself can give you the confidence to stand up to bullies and protect yourself in case of a physical altercation.Seek help: If the physical bullying continues or if you feel unsafe, it's important to seek help from a professional who can provide support and guidance on how to stay safe and handle the situation.

It's important to remember that physical bullying is not acceptable and that there are ways to stay safe and protect yourself.

What Is Physical Bullying and How to Prevent It?

Positive Action Staff•Bullying

You’ve probably faced some form of physical bullying at one point or another in your school life — or known someone who has been a victim.

The saddest part is that bullying can have long-term damaging effects on its victims. It can affect them physically, socially, mentally, academically, and emotionally well into the future.

As much as you try to eliminate physical bullying from your school, it may feel like you’re fighting a losing battle. We understand.

What you need is a winning game plan against this pervasive problem. And we’re here to provide you with one.

But first, let’s take a more in-depth look at what constitutes physical bullying.

What Types of Bullying Behavior Count as Physical Bullying?

Physical bullying is the use of physical actions to intimidate and gain control over a target. The physical actions are unwanted by the victim and can either cause damage to their body or possessions. This behavior is all too common in schools. According to the National Center for Education Statistics, 6.7% of students in the 2016-2017 school year were physically bullied.

Some common types of violence enacted through physical bullying include:

Hitting or kicking

Shoving or pushing

Tripping

Pinching

Damage of property
