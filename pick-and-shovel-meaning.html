27. ANSWER SHEET IN TLE - 8 QUARTER 2 WEEK 3 rection: Give the meaning of the following other fishery tools. 1. Round Point Shovel 2. Scoop Shovels 3. Garden Shovel Spading Fork Barn Fork Level Head Rake Bow Rake Pruning Pick Mattock​


Answer:

1. Round Point Shovel

- It uses to extend past digging to scooping and are useful in transplanting, trenching, moving compost or mulch and many more applications.

2. Scoop Shovels

- a hand tool for lifting loose material; consists of a curved container or scoop and a handle.

- scoop shovel is commonly used for moving or spreading mulch, compost, snow, grain, gravel, or similar materials.

3. Garden Shovel Spading

- The fork can be used to loosen soil all around a perennial so it lifts gently and intact, ready to carry elsewhere to replant or pot.

4. Fork Level

- Are designed to detect low or high levels of liquids.

5. Head Rake

- Is a head with a row of straight or curved tines connected directly to the handle.

- Level head rakes can be used for similar tasks as the bow rake but are especially good at breaking up spaded soil and eliminating debris.

6. Bow Rake

- A bow rake is shaped very differently from your average leaf rake. The tines are short, only a few inches (5 to 10 cm.) long, and they are parallel to each other, setting them apart from the fanning shape of a leaf rake’s tines. The tines are perpendicular to the long, straight handle. They are strong and rigid, usually made of metal.

7. Pruning Pick

- Pruning is when you selectively remove branches from a tree. The goal is to remove unwanted branches, improve the tree’s structure, and direct new, healthy growth.

8. Mattock

- an agricultural tool shaped like a pickax, with an adze and a chisel edge as the ends of the head.

- A mattock is a hand tool used for digging, prying, and chopping.
