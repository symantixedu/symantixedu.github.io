18. agawang sulok picture plsss pasend ng picture ng agawang sulok​


Numbers of Players Needed: 5, 7, or 9

Game’s Venue: Any Outdoors or Gymnasium

A quadrangular playground is marked off on the ground or floor, where the game is played.

The diagram below explains how it is set up:

Diagram A is for five players, B for seven players, and C for nine players.

All of the players, except for the IT or TAGGER, stay in the corner and on the bases. The tagger would be the one to stand in the middle of the ground. By the count of three, all the players in the corners will attempt to exchange places by running through across from place to place. The tagger should try to lock a corner or base by rushing to any of it when no one is there or it is vacant.

For a strategy or techniques in order to puzzle the tagger, the players on the bases or corners may do attempt to leave their bases and abruptly hurry back to their own corners, as if thieving a base. However, by the time the tagger has secured a base or corner, the odd player becomes the new tagger for the next round of the game.

Additionally, others called it "Agawan Base", "Vende, Vende Candela" while some do call it "Bilaran".

26. Paki basa po ng first picture And Pasagot naman po ng Second Picture Tungkol po lahat yan sa First Picture


Answer:

Title: The Gift of Magi

Setting: New York City, the day before the christmas

Character: Della Dillingham Young, Jim, and Madame Sofronie

Point of View: Third Person

Plot

"The Gift of the Magi" is a short story by O. Henry first published in 1905. The story tells of a young husband and wife and how they deal with the challenge of buying secret Christmas gifts for each other with very little money.

Exposition

In “The Gift of The Magi”, the expostion happens when the main character is introduced. There was a woman named Della. She was counting her money and realized that it was not enough to byu a present for Jim, her husband. She felt really sad that the next day would be Christmas and she still did not know what to do.

Rising Action

The rising action of the short story 'The Gift of the Magi' by O. Henry is when Della decides to sell her hair and goes to visit Madame Sofronie. This is followed by her shopping trip to buy Jim's gift.

Climax

The climax in this story happens when Jim finally went home from work. Seeing his wife's hair cut off, he suddenly just stand still at his place without being angry, surprised, or disapproval. He asked his wife to make sure that her hair had been really gone. Della cried and told him that she cut it off and sold it.

Falling Action

The falling action of the story is the realization of Della and Jim that they are unable to use each other's gifts as of now as they both sacrificed their favorite things to buy gifts for each other but it proved that their love for each other is invaluable just as the biblical gift of Magi.

Resolution

The resolution is when they realize that the true gift is that they both gave up each of their greatest possessions for each other. They care so much for each other. The falling action is when they both put away their gifts, celebrate Christmas together, and eat.

Conflict

In "The Gift of the Magi," the main conflict is that Della does not have enough money saved to buy the gift she thinks Jim deserves.

Theme

The theme of the story is that the greatest gift of all is unconditional love. Through the sacrifices Della and Jim make for one another, they prove love is more important than material possessions. As the narrator says: “Of all who give and receive gifts, such as they are wisest.”
