2. The temperature of a piece of a metal was 32°C. It was then lowered into a glass of hot water and the temperature of the piece of metal increased to 36°C. What is the percentage increase in temperature of a piece of metal?​


Answer:

1.31%

Step-by-step explanation:

The temperature of a piece of a metal was 32°C. It was then lowered into a glass of hot water and the temperature of the piece of metal increased to 36°C. What is the percentage increase in temperature of a piece of metal?​

Convert first both initial and final temperatures into absolute temperature (kelvin unit)

initial temperature = 32 +273.15 = 305.15 K

final temperature = 36 +273.15 = 309.`15 k

temperature difference = 4 K

percent increase = 4K/ (305.15K) *100% = 1.31%

25. 5/9 of piece of metal has a mass of 7kg what is the mass of 2/7 of piece of metal​


Answer:

3.6 kg

Step-by-step explanation:

Let

y = total mass if metal

x = 2/7 piece of metal

first we find the total mass of metal

[tex]7 = \frac{5}{9} y \\ 7 \times \frac{9}{5} = \frac{5}{9} y \times \frac{9}{5} \\ y = \frac{7 \times 9}{5} \\ y = \frac{63}{5} [/tex]

Now let's find x.

[tex]x = \frac{2}{7} y \\ x = \frac{2}{7} \times \frac{63}{5} \\ x = \frac{2 \times 63}{7 \times 5} \\ x = \frac{126}{35} = 3.6 \: kg[/tex]
