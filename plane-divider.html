11. What divides the Cartesian plane into parts?


Before answering that question:

What is Cartesian Plane?

Cartesian Plane is defined as a plane used to locate specific points, for example for a unit length. Cartesian Plane have its horizontal line and a vertical line that are called x-axis and y-axis respectively.

It has the point of origin, also called the vertex, which has the coordinate of (0,0). The first zero in the coordinate is the position in X-axis (Abscissa), while the second zero the position in Y-axis (Ordinate).

X-Axis

- It is the horizontal line passing through the vertex or the origin point of the plane. It divides the plane into 2, top part and bottom part.

Y-Axis

- It is the vertical line passing through the vertex or the origin point of the plane. It divides the plane into 2, right part and left part.

When X-Axis and Y-Axis is combine, it will divide the plane into 4. The divided parts are called quadrants.

1st Quadrant: The top-right part of the Cartesian plane. Coordinates placed in this quadrant has positive Abscissa and Ordinate (+, +)

2nd Quadrant: The top-left part of the Cartesian plane. Coordinates placed in this quadrant has negative Abscissa and positive Ordinate(-, +)

3rd Quadrant: The bottom-left part of the Cartesian plane. Coordinates placed in this quadrant has negative Abscissa and Ordinate(-, -)

4th Quadrant: The bottom-right part of the Cartesian plane. Coordinates placed in this quadrant has positive Abscissa and negative Ordinate(+, -)

17. what is the meaning of plane divider​


A plane divider is a tool or device that is used to separate or divide a plane surface into two or more equal or proportionate parts. It can be a physical tool like a ruler, compass, or protractor, or a mathematical concept used to create geometrical shapes or designs. In architecture and interior design, plane dividers may refer to partition walls, screens or sliding doors that divide a large open space into individual sections.
