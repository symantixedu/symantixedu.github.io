8. Rob is painting large polka Dots on a sheet for the backdrop of the school musical. He painted 16 polka dots ,each with a radius of 3 feet. What is the total area that the polka dots cover?


Answer:

The area of a circle is given by the formula A = πr^2, where A is the area and r is the radius of the circle.

In this case, each polka dot has a radius of 3 feet, so its area is:

A = π(3)^2 = 9π square feet

To find the total area covered by all 16 polka dots, we simply multiply the area of one polka dot by the number of polka dots:

Total area = 16 x 9π = 144π square feet

So the total area covered by all 16 polka dots is 144π square feet. This is an exact answer in terms of π, and if you want an approximate numerical answer, you can substitute 3.14 or another value for π to get an approximate value.

19. 1. Alex is painting large polka dots on a sheet for the backdrop of the schoolmusical. He painted 16 polka dots, each with a radius of 3 feet. What isthe total area that the polka dots cover?​


Answer:

452.16  feet²

Step-by-step explanation:

Required Answer:

Total area that the polka dots cover

Given:

r = 3 feet, for each polka dot.

Formula:

A = πr²

Solution:

A = πr²

  = π × (3 feet)²

  = 9π feet²

Assume that π = 3.14, we have

A = 9 × 3.14 feet²

   = 28.26 feet²

Hence, 28.26 feet² is the area of each polka dot

Since there are 16 polka dots, the total area that the polka dots cover is

16 × 28.26 feet² = 452.16 feet².
