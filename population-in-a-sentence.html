25. what will happen to an ecosystem if a population of one species was removed?(note 3 sentence)â€‹


Explanation:

The most obvious result of the removal of the top predators in an ecosystem is a population explosion in the prey species. Predators keep herbivore populations in check. The reverse is also true, of course -- predator populations are limited by the availability of prey. When prey is abundant, predator populations increase because more young are able to survive. More predators kill more prey, which, along with food scarcity, decreases the population. When prey becomes more scarce, the predator population declines until prey is again more abundant. Therefore, the two balance each other. When the predators are removed, prey populations explode.

28. reflection about population growth and carrying capacity 10 to 15 sentences


Answer:

Population growth and carrying capacity are two important concepts that are closely linked. Population growth refers to the increase in the number of individuals in a population over time, while carrying capacity refers to the maximum number of individuals that a given environment can support over the long-term.

Population growth can have both positive and negative consequences. On the positive side, population growth can lead to economic growth and development, increased innovation and creativity, and a larger labor force. However, population growth can also lead to negative consequences, such as increased competition for resources, environmental degradation, and social and political instability.

Carrying capacity is an important concept because it determines the sustainability of a population. When a population exceeds the carrying capacity of its environment, it may experience a decline in resources, increased disease, and decreased reproductive success. Ultimately, if a population continues to grow beyond its carrying capacity, it may experience a collapse.

The relationship between population growth and carrying capacity is complex and can be influenced by a number of factors, including technological advancements, resource availability, and social and economic policies. In order to ensure sustainable population growth, it is important to carefully consider the carrying capacity of the environment and implement policies and practices that promote sustainable resource use and management.

In conclusion, population growth and carrying capacity are critical concepts for understanding the sustainability of human societies and ecosystems. By carefully managing population growth and resource use, we can ensure a sustainable future for ourselves and future generations.
