18. 4. A rectangular prism is 20 cm long, 8 cm high and 5 cm wide, what is the surface area of the rectangular prism? What unit is used to measure the surface area of a rectangular prism? ​


Answer:

The surface area of a rectangular prism can be calculated using the formula:

Surface Area = 2lw + 2lh + 2wh

where l is the length, w is the width, and h is the height of the rectangular prism.

Substituting the given values into the formula, we get:

Surface Area = 2(20)(5) + 2(20)(8) + 2(5)(8)

= 200 + 320 + 80

= 600 cm²

Therefore, the surface area of the rectangular prism is 600 cm². The unit used to measure the surface area of a rectangular prism is square centimeters (cm²).

23. The lateral edge of an oblique square prism measuring 8 meters is inclined at an angle 45 degrees with the altitude. What is the altitude of the prism?


Answer:

5.66 meters

Step-by-step explanation:

In trigonometry, always remember SOACAHTOA, it is very helpful in finding unknown sides or angle of a triangle. It is helpful for remembering the definitions of the trigonometric functions sine, cosine, and tangent.

SOACAHTOASine is equal to oposite side over adjacent sideCosine is equal to adjacent side over hypotenuseTangent is equal to opposite side over adjacent side

In the given problem, Oblique Square PrismOblique means slanted,A prism in which the bases are parallel, Not having one directly over the other, andBases of a square prism are not perpendicular to each other.

Solution

Use

[tex] \cos( \alpha ) = \frac{a}{h} [/tex]

where a= adjacent side, h= hypotenuse

NoteEach base are congruent and parallel to one another, but NOT perpendicular to the bases do NOT represent the height of the prismHeight of an oblique prism does not parallel to, a lateral edge,Oblique faces of a prism are parallelogram

Thus, the cosine of altitude degrees is equal to the adjacent side (lateral lenght) over the length of its altitude.

[tex] \cos( \alpha ) = \frac{a}{h} [/tex]

[tex] \cos( 45 ) = \frac{8}{h} [/tex]

[tex]h = 8 \cos(45) [/tex]

[tex]h = 5.66 \: m[/tex]

Therefore, the altitude of the oblique square prism is 5.66 meters.
