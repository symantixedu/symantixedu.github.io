18. If mPR = 45 and MQS =49 what is M PTR? m RTS?​


You will get m∠PTR is 47.5 and an m∠PTR is 132.5

a) m∠PTR = 1/2 (mPR + mQS)

= 1/2 (45 + 49)

= 47.5

Because they form a straight line, the linear pair of angles are always supplementary. To put it another way, the sum of two angles in a linear pair is always 180 degrees.

b) m∠RTS = 180 - m∠PTR → (Linear pair)

= 180 - 47.5

= 132.5

Linear Pair Of Angles

When two lines intersect at a single point, a linear pair of angles is formed. If the angles are adjacent to each other after the two lines intersect, they are said to be linear. The sum of the angles of a linear pair is always 180°. These angles are also referred to as supplementary angles. The adjacent angles are those that share a vertex. As a result, the linear angles have a common vertex here as well. There will also be a common arm that represents both angles. A ladder placed against a wall forms linear angles at the ground, which is an example of a linear pair in real life.

A linear pair of angles is always supplementary because the non-adjacent sides of a linear pair form a line. However, the fact that two angles are supplementary does not imply that they form a linear pair. ABC and DBE are supplementary in the diagram below because 30°+150°=180°, but they do not form a linear pair because they are not adjacent.

22. how many 6 digit license plate can be manufactured if no license number begins with zero​


Answer:

If no license number can begin with zero, then there are 9 choices for the first digit (1-9) and 10 choices for each of the remaining five digits (0-9).

Therefore, the total number of possible 6-digit license plates is:

9 x 10 x 10 x 10 x 10 x 10 = 9,000,000

So, there can be 9,000,000 different 6-digit license plates manufactured if no license number begins with zero.
