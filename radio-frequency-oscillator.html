6. What EM wave is produced by electromagnetic oscillators at low frequencies by accelerating electrons in an electronic circuit?


What EM wave is produced by electromagnetic oscillators at low frequencies by accelerating electrons in an electronic circuit?

The answer is Radio waves.

Many radio waves are emitted  by oscillating charges in antennas.  The way the charges  move is around the antenna.  A radio wave propagates along a straight-line path called  the line of sight from the antenna to the receiver.  The position of the electromagnetic field E of the electromagnetic radiation produced by the antenna lies in  a plane that includes the receiver's antenna and sight line, which is perpendicular to the sight line.  The wave is polarized so  E has a well-defined direction.

The electric field is strongest with the highest strength in the directions perpendicular  to the antenna, and in the direction of the antenna, it goes to zero.  When you're standing under the  antenna you get really bad reception.

The electromagnetic wave must be  modulated to carry the information.  The information which a  radio wave carries is sound.  The amplitude of a radio wave AM (modulated amplitude)  represents the variations in pressure which make up the signal.  The frequency of FM (frequency modulated) radio waves may  be slightly changed from the frequency of their nominal carrier.  The amount of shift is proportional to  the pressure variations which make up the sound.

8. A mass-spring system makes 25 complete oscillations in 5 seconds. What is the period and frequency of the oscillations?​


Explanation:

The period of oscillation is the time it takes for the system to make one complete oscillation. In this case, the system makes 25 oscillations in 5 seconds, so the period is 5/25 = 0.2 seconds.

The frequency of oscillation is the number of oscillations that occur in a given period of time. It is the reciprocal of the period. In this case, the frequency is 1/0.2 = 5 oscillations per second
