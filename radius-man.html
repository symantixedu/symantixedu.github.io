To find the distance it would take to walk once around a circular lagoon with a radius of 22 meters, you would need to calculate the circumference of the lagoon. So, to find the circumference of the circular lagoon:

C = 2 * pi * 22

C = 2 * 3.14 * 22

C = 138.48 m

Therefore, it would take 138.48 meters to walk once around the circular lagoon.

8. A 90-kilogram man on the moon weighs 146 N. If the radius of the moon is 1.74 x 10 6 meters, what is the mass of the moon?


Answer:

The mass of the moon is 4.23 x 10¹⁶ kg

Explanation:

Here, we are to solve for the mass of the moon. In this problem, we will use the concept of Newton's law of gravitation.

The weight of any object changes depending on the gravity. The weight of a person on Earth differs from his weight on the moon because the gravity on the moon and Earth is different. But the mass of any object do not change because it is not affected by gravity.

We can use the formula W = mg where the weight of any object is affected by gravity.

To solve for the mass of the moon, we will use the formula:

M = gR²/ G

where

M     is the mass of the moon, unit is in kg

g     is the the gravity of the moon, unit is in m/s²

R     is the radius of the moon, unit is in meters

G     is the universal gravitational constant, 6.67 x 10 ⁻¹¹ m³/kg·s²

For the given information

m = 90 kg

W = 146 N

R = 1.74 x 10⁶ meter

M = ?

Solving the problem

1. First, let us solve for the moon's gravitational constant using

W = mg

146 N = (90 kg) g     but N = kg·m/s²

146 kg·m/s² = (90 kg) g    

Divide both sides by 90 kg, we have:

g = 146 kg·m/s² / 90 kg

g = 1.62 m/s²

2. Now, let us use the formula to solve for the mass of the moon, we have:

M = gR²/ G

M = (1.62 m/s²)(1.74 x 10⁶ m)² / 6.67 x 10 ⁻¹¹ m³/kg·s²

M = 4.23 x 10¹⁶ kg

Therefore, the mass of the moon is 4.23 x 10¹⁶ kg.

9. Assuming a successful man-made satellite rotates around the earth at a rate of 4.9 rev in 7.5 days. Assuming the has an orbital radius of 348.2 km. Find the satellite's tangential speed.


Answer:

The number of revolutions per second is:

4.9 revolutions / 7.5 days = 4.9 rev / (7.5 days * 24 hours/day * 60 minutes/hour * 60 seconds/minute) = 0.0000070608 rev/sec

The distance traveled in one revolution is equal to the circumference of the circle with radius equal to the orbital radius:

C = 2 * pi * r = 2 * pi * 348.2 km = 2185.7 km

The tangential speed is equal to the distance traveled per second, which is equal to the product of the distance traveled per revolution and the number of revolutions per second:

v = C * (number of revolutions per second) = 2185.7 km/rev * 0.0000070608 rev/sec = 15.43 meters/second

Therefore, the satellite's tangential speed is 15.43 meters/second.

13. Find the centripetal force of a 50 kg man moving around a rotunda with a speed of 15 m/s along radius of 1.5 m2? Tamako​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The centripetal force exerted on an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{F_{c} = \frac{mv^{2}}{r}}[/tex]

where:

[tex]F_{c} =[/tex] centripetal force

m = mass

v = velocity

r = radius

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the centripetal force

[tex]F_{c} = \dfrac{mv^{2}}{r}[/tex]

[tex]F_{c} = \dfrac{(\text{50 kg})(\text{15 m/s})^{2}}{\text{1.5 m}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{F_{c} = \text{7,500 N}}[/tex]

Therefore, the centripetal force of the man is 7,500 N.

36. 37.68

37. 87.92

38. 12.56

formula for circumference - 2πr ( 2 × 3.14 × radius)
