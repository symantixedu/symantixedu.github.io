8. In a car lift, compressed air with a gauge pressure of 4.0 × 10 5 Pa is used to raise a piston with a circular cross-sectional area. If the radius of the piston is 0.17 m, what is the maximum mass that can be raised using this piston?


Given:
Pressure = P = 4.0 x 10^5 Pa
radius = r = 0.17 m

Required:
Maximum mass = m

Solution:
P = F/A
P = F/π•r^2
(4.0 x 10^5 Pa) = F / π(0.17 m)^2
F = (4.0 x 10^5 Pa)[(π)(0.17 m)^2]
F = 36,316.81 N

Assume: Earth settings
F = mg
(36,316.81 N) = m (9.8 m/s^2)
m = (36,316.81 N) / (9.8 m/s^2)
m = 3,705.80 kg (Final Answer)

21. Jessy bought a car for $2,200,000. He sold it for a 20% profit. From the money raised from this sale, he bought another more expensive car, which unfortunately he had to sell for a 10% loss. What was his overall profit or loss? ​


Jessy bought the car for $2,200,000. Selling it for a 20% profit means he sold it for $2,640,000 (original price + 20% profit).

He then used the money from the sale to buy another car. Let's say the second car cost $x. But unfortunately, he had to sell it for a 10% loss, which means he sold it for $0.9x.

Jessy's overall profit or loss can be calculated as the difference between the money he made from selling the first car and the money he got from selling the second car, minus the original cost of the first car.

Profit/Loss = ($2,640,000 - $0.9x) - $2,200,000

We don't know the exact value of x, but we do know that the second car was more expensive than the first car, so x must be greater than $2,200,000.

Therefore, the minimum possible overall profit/loss can be calculated using x = $2,200,001:

Profit/Loss = ($2,640,000 - $1,980,000) - $2,200,000

Profit/Loss = $660,000 - $2,200,000

Profit/Loss = -$1,540,000

This means Jessy had an overall loss of $1,540,000.

23. A hydraulic lift used at an automotive repair shop raises a 1000-kilogram car two meters off of the ground. What is the potential energy given to the car? A 1000 J B 2000 J C 9800 J D 19,600 J


Answer:

D. 19,600 J  

Explanation:

In our case, we are to solve for the potential energy of the car above the hydraulic lift. For potential energy, we will use the formula:

PE = mgh

where

PE      is the potential energy, unit is in N.m or Joules (J)

m       is the mass of the car, unit is in kg

g        is the acceleration due to gravity, 9.8 m/[tex]s^2[/tex]

h         is the height lifted by the hydraulic to the car, unit is in meters (m)

For the given information

m = 1000 kg

h = 2 m

PE = ?

Solving the problem

Let us use the formula for potential energy then substitute the given information, we have:

PE = mgh

PE = (1000 kg)(9.8 m/[tex]s^2[/tex])(2 m)

PE = 19,600 J

Therefore, the potential energy of the car is 19,600 J.
