However, I can provide you with the five criteria in target marketing and how they can help you determine whether a target market is viable or not.

1. Size - The size of a target market is important because it determines the potential revenue that can be generated from that market. A viable target market should be large enough to provide significant revenue opportunities.

2. Expected growth - A viable target market should have the potential for growth in the future. This means that there should be a demand for the product or service being offered, and that demand should be expected to grow over time.

3. Competitive position - It is important to consider the competitive landscape when selecting a target market. A viable target market should have a favorable competitive position, meaning that there should be a clear need for the product or service being offered and limited competition.

4. Cost to reach - The cost of reaching a target market is an important consideration, as it can impact the profitability of the marketing effort. A viable target market should have a cost-effective way to reach the target audience.

5. Compatibility - A viable target market should be compatible with the company's overall mission and values. This means that the target market should align with the company's goals and objectives, and that the company should be able to serve the target market effectively.

By evaluating your selected target market against these five criteria, you can determine whether it is a viable target market or not.

27. The water fountain in Circuit City, Makati are designed to spray a jet of water with a laminar flow at an angle of sixty degrees from a distance of 1.2 meters. How high are the jets expected to reach? Give your final answer in meters? ​


To find the height that the water jets are expected to reach, we need to use the formula for the range of a projectile, which is:

range = (initial velocity * time of flight) * sin(2 * launch angle)

where the time of flight is the time it takes for the water to reach the highest point in its trajectory, and the initial velocity is the speed at which the water is being propelled from the fountain.

To solve for the time of flight, we can use the equation:

time of flight = (2 * initial velocity * sin(launch angle)) / acceleration due to gravity

where the acceleration due to gravity is approximately 9.8 m/s^2.

Plugging in the given values, we get:

time of flight = (2 * (1.2 m/s) * sin(60 degrees)) / 9.8 m/s^2

time of flight = 0.244 seconds

Now that we have the time of flight, we can plug it back into the range formula to solve for the range, which is the height that the water jets are expected to reach:

range = (1.2 m/s * 0.244 s) * sin(2 * 60 degrees)

range = 0.29 meters

So the water jets are expected to reach a height of approximately 0.29 meters.
