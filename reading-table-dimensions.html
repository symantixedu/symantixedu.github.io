Spatial Dimension:

Recognizing social benefits: This refers to identifying the positive impacts of a particular action or decision on the society as a whole. Recognizing social costs: This refers to identifying the negative impacts of a particular action or decision on the society as a whole. An example of this could be the environmental pollution caused by a factory, which could lead to health problems and decreased quality of life for nearby residents.

Recognizing future benefits: This refers to identifying the positive impacts of a particular action or decision that may not be immediately apparent but will manifest in the future. Temporal Dimension:

Recognizing future costs: This refers to identifying the negative impacts of a particular action or decision that may not be immediately apparent but will manifest in the future.
