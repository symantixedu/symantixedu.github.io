25. me: d the probability of the following: 1. A card is drawn from a standard deck of cards. Find the probability of choosing a a. black card or a spade card b. red card or ace


Answer:

The standard deck of cards has 52 cards, divided equally into four suits: spades, hearts, diamonds, and clubs. Each suit contains 13 cards, with 9 numbered cards (2 to 10), and 4 face cards (Jack, Queen, King, and Ace). Among the 52 cards, half are black (26 cards) and half are red (26 cards).

a. To find the probability of choosing a black card or a spade card, we need to count the number of cards that are either black or spades or both. There are 13 spades, and 13 black cards (including spades), but we have counted the Ace of Spades twice. Therefore, the total number of cards that are black or spades or both is 13 + 13 - 1 = 25. Hence, the probability of choosing a black card or a spade card is:

P(black or spade) = 25/52 = 0.4808 or approximately 48.08%

b. To find the probability of choosing a red card or an ace, we need to count the number of cards that are either red or aces or both. There are 26 red cards, and 4 aces (including 2 red aces), but we have counted the two red aces twice. Therefore, the total number of cards that are red or aces or both is 26 + 4 - 2 = 28. Hence, the probability of choosing a red card or an ace is:

P(red or ace) = 28/52 = 0.5385 or approximately 53.85%
