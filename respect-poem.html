1. poem of self respect​


Answer:

I Will Be Brave

No matter what, I’II always be brave,

For I’m a warrior, and respect I crave,

Without a legend, there will be no story,

And I’m too determined to not seek glory,

Settling down is not my way,

Towards success, I let myself sway,

I’m too proud to take a bow,

I’II rather sail the seas and say, ‘Yo! Ho! Ho!’

So no matter what, I will be brave,

I’II become the cliff, being hit by a wave,

I’II make mistakes, but those will make me strong,

I’II learn from them, and never do the wrong,

Bit by bit, I’II earn what I desire,

Of achieving new heights, I will never tire,

Alas! Age will catch on, and it will wear me down,

But even when they bury me, in my own grave,

I’II leave behind a legacy, of a man being brave.

Answer: I should pay respect to my family for the they gives me what I need especially love, comfort, and care. They work hard for my needs and they lift me to reach my dreams.

2. In the second stanza of the poem, why should you give respect to your teachers and friends?

Answer: I should give respect to my teachers and friends for they teach me and care for me at school. Teachers are considered as our second parents for they teach us on how to pronounce correctly, write, and draw. My friends cheers me up and they trully accept me for who I am.

3. In the third stanza of the poem, why should you bestow respect to strangers and to your maids?

Answer: I should bestow respect to strangers and to our helpers/maids for they might be the instrument of God to me whenever I need help and they work hard for us as they serve to us everyday.

Answer: I should grant respect to the religion of others and to the food being served on my plate to also respect my religion by them and for the food that's being served were blessings to me that many people needs it more than myself.

Answer: Yes, because respect builds feelings of safety around us and it builds the feelings of trust. If we want others to respect who we are then we should respect them.

16. What rhyme schemes are used in the poems "Douglass" and "Richard Bone" respectively?


The poem "Douglass" by Paul Laurence Dunbar follows an ABAB rhyme scheme in each stanza. The poem "Richard Bone" by Edgar Lee Masters follows an ABCB rhyme scheme in each stanza.

Everyone Is Worthwhile

Poet: Julie Hebert, ©2015

Life is for the go-getters,

The ones who change the world.

But never forget the few of us,

That work till the tides are hurled.

We are not known across the globe,

Or have a hefty pay.

We may not even have a car,

But we do our part each day.

We make a contribution,

To the world and all who live.

And although our jobs may not seem significant,

We still work hard and smile and give.

We are the people who serve you,

The gas attendant, gardener or cashier,

We are also hidden behind the scenes,

Wishing that we could appear.

We are often overlooked,

Not even given a smile.

Next time you run a quick errand,

Remember, respect and everyone is worthwhile
