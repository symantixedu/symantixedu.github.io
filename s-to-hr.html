24. A car is moving in straight path from 60 km/hr to 45 km/hr in 0.8 hr. What is the acceleration of the car? [A] km/hr Convert your answer in m/s: [B] m/s


We can use the formula for acceleration:

a = (v_f - v_i) / t

where
a = acceleration
v_i = initial velocity
v_f = final velocity
t = time

In this problem, we have:
v_i = 60 km/hr
v_f = 45 km/hr
t = 0.8 hr

Substituting the values into the formula, we get:

a = (45 km/hr - 60 km/hr) / 0.8 hr
a = -15 km/hr / 0.8 hr
a = -18.75 km/hr^2 (Note that the negative sign indicates deceleration)

To convert the answer to m/s^2, we need to multiply by a conversion factor:
1 km/hr^2 = 0.2778 m/s^2

So, we have:

a = -18.75 km/hr^2 x 0.2778 m/s^2/km/hr^2
a = -5.2083 m/s^2

Therefore, the acceleration of the car is -18.75 km/hr^2 or -5.2083 m/s^2 (rounded to four decimal places).

30. whichof the following shows velocity? a. 10m/s East b. 10km/hr c. 10k./s/s d. 10m/hr North​


Answer:

D. 10m/hour North​

Explanation:

Multiply the acceleration by the time the object is being accelerated. For example, if an object falls for 3 seconds, multiply 3 by 9.8 meters per second squared, which is the acceleration from gravity. The resultant velocity in this case is 29.4 meters per second.

Velocity (v) is a vector quantity that measures displacement (or change in position, Δs) over the change in time (Δt), represented by the equation v = Δs/Δt. Speed (or rate, r) is a scalar quantity that measures the distance traveled (d) over the change in time (Δt), represented by the equation r = d/Δt.
