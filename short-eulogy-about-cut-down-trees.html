“My aspens dear, whose airy cages quelled,

 

  Quelled or quenched in leaves the leaping sun,

 

  All felled, felled, are all felled;

 

    Of a fresh and following folded rank

 

                Not spared, not one

 

                That dandled a sandalled

 

         Shadow that swam or sank

 

On meadow & river & wind-wandering weed-winding bank.

 

    

 

  O if we but knew what we do

 

         When we delve or hew —

 

     Hack and rack the growing green!”

4. Eulogy about mother nature


Eulogy about Mother NaTure

Mother Nature is slowly fading
Into the emptiness of "civilization."
The birds no longer sing of freedom,
But of sorrow and pain.
Lands are mangled and eaten by the steel-toothed tractors of man
And in their places are put condominiums and parking lots,
Shopping malls and golden arches.
Then, we try to connect with Mother Earth,
Interspersing bits and pieces of "untouched" land—
A few standing trees and shrubs, with a family of cardinals,
Allowed to keep their home for today—
Put on display as a dying part of our world
Between some new mega chain store and a strip mall.

She is lonely now,
Nobody to keep her company,
As everyone has somewhere they should’ve been five minutes ago.
They fly by in their mass-produced cars,
Polluting the world around them.
“There’s plenty of trees.
Let our children worry about that.”

Now, everywhere you look:
Concrete, asphalt, chain link fences.
The air is gray and brown with the pollution of years gone by.
It’s hard to breathe.
There is no more beauty in the world.

Here's an example of eulogy speech to help you through one of the most emotional speeches you'll probably ever have to give in your lifetime.

A eulogy or funeral speech is a speech given at a funeral or memorial service to honor the deceased. You may be asked to give a eulogy speech for a family member or a close friend.

A eulogy is often one of the most difficult types of speeches to deliver due to its emotional nature, but delivering a good eulogy can be very meaningful for the attendees of the service and particularly for the family members.

Important elements of a eulogy are

to let the audience know how you knew the deceasedto share a personal story or two that sheds a positive light on the character of the personto let the family members and friends of the deceased know how much their loved one contributed to you or the community and how he or she will be missed.

It is perfectly OK to tell a humorous story during a eulogy as long as it is in good taste. Poems on death or quotes on death may be something you want to weave into your eulogy as well.

24. example of eulogy about nature


Eulogy for Nature

A eulogy about nature itself requires positive things and aspects, most of it will be for something that served as well in our lifetime and never ceased to amaze us by showing wondrous things about life and living with nature.

Nature, our ecosystem or Mother Nature we call it many names due to its care for other living creatures and organisms whether big or small. This is where we first started to learn how to live and how to survive. It gives us life itself by supplying us with:

Food Water Shelter

The way we live now is considered “modern” since it involves technology and more electricity. But back when all those things didn’t exist, the place we once called home in Nature. Up to now we still rely on nature very much since it has the ability to heal itself over the time being. What we humans destroy she grows it back again. But what is Mother nature gets tired of doing things all over again? We humans continue to give her:

Pollution Greenhouse Gases Effects of Fossil Fuels Cutting trees for other purposes Sell animals that truly belongs to the wild

Eulogy for Nature:

To Mother Nature,

You are a gift that God created with varieties of color and life, from little living creatures up to the ignorant humans. You shine with beauty and peacefulness, with every tree that blew through your breeze and those waves in every ocean. You are part of every one of us whether we are from different countries and from different nations. You make us think that we always have one in common with everyone.

For every boat that safely got back from the sea; for every hungry human you give them fruits and berries. The leaves that grow in every tree and its shade, the sunlight gently passing through every color of flowers and leaves. The weather that enables us to grow crops and more trees.

You gave us more than we deserved and more than we asked for, yet we still weren’t content. We took for granted your blessing to us, the life you make us and the other creatures too. We sought power and more land to conquer and improve wealth we lost touch of what was fading. But I believe that even though we continue to hurt you, even more, you still are forgiving and will start a new.

To the nature that never ceased to amaze us.

From a tiny human,

27. Eulogy about grandmother


Answer:

To my grandmother

Explanation:

I was born into this world without my parents taking care of me. I was abandoned by my father and a few months after giving birth to me, my mother had to go somewhere else to work--an act considered as good by many but I felt it was another form of abandonment. You were my father, you were my mother.

Even though we did not have the most money and at times we have to eat  just rice and no viand on certain days, we survived. You loved me more than anyone else could, despite all my shortcomings. You were there for me when I was lost. You were there for me even when you were sick.

I feel the loss now that you are gone and I wish I could have done more for you. I wish that I could turn back time and be with you once again. But no, time has expired for us and that I could not do anything about anymore. As we more forward with our lives I could only pray that you rest in peace as I cherish our times together.
