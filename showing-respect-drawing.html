30. Problems Three cards are drawn without replacement. The first and the second cards show 4 and 6 respectively. Find the probability of selecting an even number in a third draw.​


Answer:

Since the first and second cards have already been drawn, there are 50 cards left in the deck. Out of these 50, there are 24 even numbers (2, 4, 6, 8, 10, J, Q, K of diamonds, clubs, hearts, and spades), and 26 odd numbers (ACE, 3, 5, 7, 9, J, Q, K of diamonds, clubs, hearts, and spades).

Since we know that the first and second card drawn were a 4 and a 6 respectively, we know that there are only 2 even numbers left in the deck (8 and 10) and 48 cards remaining.

Therefore, the probability of drawing an even number on the third draw is:

2 even numbers left / 48 cards remaining = 1/24

So the probability of selecting an even number in a third draw is 1/24.
