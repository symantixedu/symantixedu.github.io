10. discuss the differences between push-up, shuttle run and running?​


Push up, shuttle run and running are all physical fitness tests but different in practice. The push-up test is used to assess upper body fitness. Shuttle run test measures agility and speed while running between two lines 10m apart to pick up small blocks. The running test is for aerobic fitness, in which participants try and cover as much distance as they can in 12 minutes. Physical fitness tests are a series of exercises that help evaluate the overall health and physical status of a person.

Components of Physical Fitness

Physical fitness is often used to gauge if a person is in good shape.  The components that make up total fitness are:

Cardiovascular strength is the capability of the heart and lungs to work together to provide the needed oxygen and fuel to the body during nonstop activities. Muscular strength is the extent of force a muscle can produce. Muscular endurance is the ability of the muscles to perform continuously without losing energy.  Flexibility is the ability of each joint to move through the available range of motion towards a specific joint.  Body composition is the amount of fat mass matched to lean muscle mass, bone, and organs.

16. how do you perform shuttle run?​


Answer:

https://www.verywellfit.com/build-sports-endurance-with-shuttle-runs-3120584

Explanation

To do a basic shuttle run exercise:

Set up markers such as cones about 25 yards apart.

Make sure you are warmed up; consider adding this drill to the end of a brisk jog.

Sprint from one marker to the other and back. That is 1 repetition.

Do 6 repetitions as fast as you can (300 yards total).

Time your result for the entire 6 repetitions.

Rest for 5 minutes.

Repeat the drill.

Add the times for each run together and divide by two to find the average time.

Record this time.

You can use this shuttle run test score monthly to monitor your progress over a period of time.
