6. Makes or approves all significant decisions related to the allocation of resources.​


Answer:

The entity that makes or approves all significant decisions related to the allocation of resources is typically the management or leadership team of an organization. This group of individuals is responsible for setting strategic goals, determining budgets, and making decisions about how resources such as time, money, and personnel will be allocated to achieve those goals. In some cases, there may be a single individual who holds ultimate authority over resource allocation decisions, such as a CEO or board chairperson. However, in most organizations, resource allocation is a collaborative process that involves input and decision-making from multiple leaders and stakeholders.

24. If the level of significance is 0.05 and the p-value is 0.043 the decision would be to


If the level of significance is 0.05 and the p-value is 0.043, the decision would be to reject the null hypothesis. 

Basis: Set the significance level, α, the probability of making a Type I error to be small — 0.01, 0.05, or 0.10. Compare the P-value to significance level. If the P-value is less than (or equal to) α, reject the null hypothesis in favor of the alternative hypothesis. If the P-value is greater than α, do not reject the null hypothesis.
