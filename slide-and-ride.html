6. Darnel climbs a 9-meter tower to ride a straight water slide. After landing in the splash pool, he walks 12 meters back to the base of the tower. How far did he travel in all?


Answer:

Darnel climbed 9 meters up the tower and then slid down the water slide, which is a straight path. This distance is not given, but it is not needed to solve the problem. After landing in the splash pool, he walked 12 meters back to the base of the tower.

Therefore, the total distance Darnel traveled is the sum of the distance he climbed, the distance he slid down the water slide, and the distance he walked back to the base of the tower. Since the distance he slid down the water slide is not given, we cannot calculate the total distance he traveled.

However, we can calculate the minimum distance he traveled, assuming he slid down the water slide vertically. In this case, the distance he traveled is the hypotenuse of a right triangle with legs 9 meters and 12 meters, where 9 meters is the height of the tower and 12 meters is the distance he walked back to the base of the tower.

Using the Pythagorean theorem, we can find the hypotenuse:

h = sqrt(9^2 + 12^2) = sqrt(81 + 144) = sqrt(225) = 15

Therefore, the minimum distance Darnel traveled is 15 meters. However, if he did not slide down the water slide vertically, the actual distance he traveled would be greater than 15 meters.
