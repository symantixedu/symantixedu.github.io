Answer:

The speed of the wave = 6.64 m/s.

Explanation:

Wave: A wave is a disturbance which travels through a medium and transfer energy from one point to another without causing any permanent displacement of the medium itself.

The velocity of a wave (v) = λf. . Equation 1

Where λ = wave length of the wave, f = frequency of the wave.

Given: λ = 8 m, and

f = 1/t where t = period.

t = 1.2 s

f = 1/1.2 = 0.83 hertz

Substituting these values into equation 1

v = 8 × 0.83

v = 6.64 m/s.

Therefore the speed of the wave = 6.64 m/s.

moves along in different layers of the Earth and causes Earthquakes, Eruptions of Volcanoes,  Movement of Magma underground and Landslides.

Explanation:

Here are the steps in using a Slinky for creating Seismic Waves:

1. You can either get another person to hold the other side of the Slinky or else use masking tape to connect the other part of the Slinky into a wall or bar.

2.  To create a wave, Pull and Push away the Slinky towards you.

3. To understand the different kinds of waves, Shaking the one side end of the Slinkyby doing an Up and Down Motion.

4. This time, combine the Push and Pull and the Up and down motions.

It should come out in a circular way.

5. Last is to get another Slinky and place it above the first one.

Make a movement of side to side for both of the Slinky.

This movement will help you understand the Love wave which is known

as a transverse type of wave which has a similarity to the Rayleigh Wave which is compacted to the surface of the Earth.

Seismic Waves

The kind of seismic wave produced by the activity using a slinky is the primary wave or P-waves, secondary waves or S-waves, and the surface waves.

Explanation:

During an earthquake, the initial waves that will register on seismographs are called P-waves. They can go through solid, liquid, and gas materials. After the initial wave, a secondary wave or S-wave will follow suit. These waves cannot pass through liquid materials. After the initial body waves have occurred, the next batches of waves are the most destructive ones. These are called the surface waves, and it can be classified further as either love waves or Rayleigh waves. These waves move vertically or horizontally, and can cause significant damage to an area.
