2. How to spread awareness about STD/STI?


Answer:

One of the best ways to spread awareness about sexually transmitted diseases (STDs) and sexually transmitted infections (STIs) is through education. This can include providing information about the risks of unprotected sex, the symptoms of STDs and STIs, and the importance of regular testing. Additionally, it is important to provide resources for those who may be affected by STDs and STIs, such as support groups, counseling, and medical care. Finally, it is important to create a safe and supportive environment for people to talk openly about STDs and STIs.

15. Essay :How do raise awareness for STDs especially for young ones? ​


Answer:

What can Providers do to help young people avoid STDs?

Explanation:

Ensure a sexual history is taken during a patient’s initial visit, during routine preventive exams, and if you see signs of STDs. Providers should discuss the 5 “Ps” with their patients: partners, practices, protection from STDs, past history of STDs, and prevention of pregnancy.

Encourage STD testing among young people. Of the nearly 16 million American sexually active women aged 15 to 25, only 38 percent report being tested within the past year for chlamydia. This means more than 9 million young sexually active women were not screened as CDC recommends.

Adhere to screening recommendations. CDC recommends annual screening for chlamydia for all sexually active women aged 25 and younger.

Explore provider resources from CDC: STD Awareness - Reaching Young People
