Snake and chicken:

Chicken help with a snake - but they're not snake hunters, like guinea fowl. Chickens can kill snakes but they don't go out of their way to do it. Guinea fowl on the other hand, seem to have a vendetta against snakes and are wily enough and tough enough to catch, kill, and eat them. Chickens have been known to try to hatch snakes.

Cat and mouse:

Cat and mouse, often expressed as cat-and-mouse game, is an English-language idiom that means "a contrived action involving constant pursuit, near captures, and repeated escapes." The "cat" is unable to secure a definitive victory over the "mouse", who despite not being able to defeat the cat, is able to avoid capture.

Mosquito and child:

When an infected mosquito bites a human, the parasite passes from cells in the salivary glands of the mosquito into the human's blood stream via the mosquito's saliva.

Caterpillar and leaves:

Caterpillars, the larvae of butterflies and moths, damage plants by chewing on leaves, flowers, shoots, and fruit and sometimes other parts of the plant. Caterpillars hidden in rolled leaves or among foliage can be difficult to see or manage.

FOOD CHAIN THAT OCCUR IN THE FOREST

The food chain depicts the process by which energy is transferred from one living organism to another via food. It is critical that we understand how the food chain works so that we can identify the important living organisms that comprise the food chain and how the ecology is balanced. The food chain describes how energy and nutrients circulate in an ecosystem. Plants produce energy at the most basic level, and then it progresses to higher-level organisms such as herbivores. When herbivores eat carnivores, energy is transferred from one to the other. Energy is transferred from one living organism to another in the form of food along the food chain.
