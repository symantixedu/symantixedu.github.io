28. B. Direction. Given the following images, write a short speech describing the scenario following the speech purpose. 1. Informative 2.Persuasive 3. Intertainment​


Speech is the activity of conveying a message from the speaker to the listener.

Here are examples of speeches based on their purpose:

1. An informative speech is a speech with the aim of conveying important knowledge or information to the audience. An example is a speech about the information on how to avoid infection with the Covid-19 virus.

Good morning everyone, I hope we are all always given health.

Today, I will tell you about ways to avoid the Covid-19 virus.

The first is to use a mask and rarely stay in public spaces.

Second, maintain your immune system by eating healthy foods and vitamins, and exercising regularly.

Third, diligently wash your hands after every activity or before eating.

By carrying out these three things, we can minimize the risk of being infected with the Covid-19 virus.

2. Recreative speech is speech with the aim of conveying joy to the audience. An example is a speech to congratulate on a birthday.

Good morning everyone, I'm very grateful that we can gather on this happy day. Today, we all celebrate the birthday of my sister, Hana, who turns 17 years old. For my sister, happy-birthday, I pray that all your wishes can come true and you can be a happy person.

3. Persuasive speech is speech with the aim of inviting or persuading listeners to do things according to the purpose of the speech.

An example is a speech to invite the audience to dispose of trash in its place.

Good morning all. Hopefully, we are always in good health.

Do you realize that lately, the weather is getting hotter and erratic?

One of the reasons for this is global warming. This global warming can occur of them because of the accumulation of garbage. This accumulated garbage will produce greenhouse gases that can increase the temperature of the earth. Therefore, let's take care of our earth so that global warming does not get worse. One of the ways we can do this is by disposing of trash in its place and managing waste properly so it doesn't pile up. Because if global warming gets worse, it will also have a bad impact on us. So let's take care of our earth together.

29. Describe other elements that appear in the poem. Images and imagery (colorful language, figures of speech)


Task 10: Conversing in Verse

Answer:

The purpose of the task is to help the student’s apply their knowledge about the elements of a poem. As the title implies it is conversing in verse, understanding the given poem “Death, be not proud” by John Donne. It was the best example for students to determine every corresponding element in the poem given.

Figures of Speech:

Simile Metaphor Personification Hyperbole Symbolism Irony

Figures of Speech used in the poem:

Metaphor-“Thou art slave to fate, chance, kings, and desperate men” Personification- “Death, be not proud” Simile-“Death be not proud, though some have called thee Mighty and Dreadful”

Colourful Language used in the poem:

“And poppy or charms can make us sleep as well” “Rest of their bones, and soul’s delivery” “And dost of poison, war, and sickness dwell”

The writer of the poem seems to be undermining death and saying that even if there is death there is something before it that has the same effect to a person.
