4. A big rectangle has 9 rows of smaller rectangle with 9 squares in each row. One third of the rectangle are colored red, One ninth is colored green, 2/3 of the remainder are blue, and the rest are yellow. How many small rectangles are red? Green ? Blue? And yellow​


Answer:

there are 27 small rectangles that are colored red, 9 small rectangles that are colored green, 36 small rectangles that are colored blue, and 18 small rectangles that are colored yellow.

Step-by-step explanation:

The big rectangle has a total of 9 x 9 = 81 smaller rectangles.

One third of the rectangle are colored red, which is (1/3) x 81 = 27 small rectangles.

One ninth is colored green, which is (1/9) x 81 = 9 small rectangles.

The remainder of the rectangle that is not red is (81 - 27) = 54 small rectangles.

Two-thirds of the remainder are blue, which is (2/3) x 54 = 36 small rectangles.

The remaining small rectangles are yellow, which is (54 - 36) = 18 small rectangles.

Therefore, there are 27 small rectangles that are colored red, 9 small rectangles that are colored green, 36 small rectangles that are colored blue, and 18 small rectangles that are colored yellow.

The big rectangle has a total of 9 x 9 = 81 small rectangles.

One third of the rectangle are colored red, which is:

(1/3) x 81 = 27

So, there are 27 small rectangles that are red.

One ninth is colored green, which is:

(1/9) x 81 = 9

So, there are 9 small rectangles that are green.

The remainder after the red and green rectangles have been counted is:

81 - 27 - 9 = 45

2/3 of the remainder are blue, which is:

(2/3) x 45 = 30

So, there are 30 small rectangles that are blue.

The rest of the rectangles are yellow, which is:

45 - 30 = 15

So, there are 15 small rectangles that are yellow.

Therefore, there are 27 small rectangles that are red, 9 that are green, 30 that are blue, and 15 that are yellow.

Construct a Punnett square showing this cross between red and white flowers. What are the genotypes of the offspring? Identify the phenotypic and genotypic ratios. Punnett Square: Questions: What are the genotypes of the offspring? What are the genotypic and phenotypic ratios?​


Answer:

1. The genotypes of the offspring will be all RW

2. Genotypic ratio: 4/4 or % RW flowers

Phenotypic ratio: 4/4 or % pink flowers.

Explanation:

Incomplete dominance means the inheritance that is intermediate or blended between the genes of the parental parents. The phenotypes of the parents are not totally dominant or expressed on the physical appearance of their offsprings. In this case, the red flower is crossed with the white flower, and the offspring is neither red nor white but pink. So the offspring's appearance shows the intermediate color of the parental flowers which are red and white. However, if the pink flowers are crossed with themselves, the offsprings' phenotypes become 1/4 red; 1/2 pink; and 1/4 white. So the phenotypes and genotypes of the parental flowers are not lost because it appears in the second filial generation.
