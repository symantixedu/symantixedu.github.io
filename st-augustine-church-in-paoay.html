4. the confessions of st Augustine ​


Answer:

The Confessions of Saint Augustine is a spiritual autobiography written by Augustine of Hippo, a Christian bishop and theologian in the fourth century. It is considered one of the most influential works of Western literature and has been widely read and studied for centuries.

In the Confessions, Augustine reflects on his life and the events that led him to convert to Christianity. He describes his early life as a sinner and his journey towards enlightenment and spiritual redemption through his encounter with the teachings of Jesus and the writings of the Bible. Augustine also writes about his struggles with faith and his relationship with God, and he reflects on the role of grace in his own life and in the lives of others.

The Confessions is notable for its honest and candid portrayal of Augustine's personal struggles and its exploration of the nature of faith and the role of God in human life. It has had a profound impact on the development of Christian theology and has influenced the thinking of countless people throughout the centuries.

10. describe paoay church​


Known as the “Earthquake Baroque” church in the Philippines, Paoay church was built of baked bricks, coral rocks, salbot (tree sap) and lumber, and has 24 carved massive buttresses for support. It is an architectural solution to the area's challenging, natural setting.

Answer:

Paoay church was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1993 as one of the Baroque Style Churches in the Philippines. ... This is known for its intricate architecture with its huge buttresses on the sides of the church used as a support against earthquake

18. Destcribe the Paoay Church?


Answer:

Destcribe the Paoay Church?The Saint Augustine Church, commonly known as the Paoay Church, is a Roman Catholic church in the Municipality of Paoay, Ilocos Norte in the Philippines. Completed in 1710, the church is famous for its distinct architecture highlighted by the enormous buttresses on the sides and back of the building. It is declared as a National Cultural Treasure by the Philippine government in 1973 and a UNESCO World Heritage Site under the collective group of Baroque Churches of the Philippines in 1993.

25. list down five great contribution of st. Augustine to the history of the church.please answer •3•​


Explanation:

1. His numerous written works, the most important of which are Confessions (c. 400) and The City of God (c. 413–426), shaped the practice of biblical exegesis and helped lay the foundation for much of medieval and modern Christian thought. In Roman Catholicism he is formally recognized as a doctor of the church.

2. He adapted Classical thought to Christian teaching and created a powerful theological system of lasting influence

3. He also shaped the practice of biblical exegesis and helped lay the foundation for much of medieval and modern Christian thought.

4. He is best known for the "Confessiones" ("Confessions", a personal account of his early life, completed in about 397), "De civitate Dei" ("The City of God", consisting of 22 books started in 413 and finished in 426, dealing with God, martyrdom, Jews and other Christian philosophies) and "De Trinitate" ("On the Trinity", consisting of 15 books written over the final 30 years of his life, in which he developed the "psychological analogy" of the Trinity).

5. In both his philosophical and theological reasoning, he was greatly influenced by Stoicism, Platonism and Neo-Platonism, particularly the "Enneads" of Plotinus (his generally favorable view of Neo-Platonic thought contributed to its entrance into the Christian, and subsequently the European, intellectual tradition). He was also influenced by the works of the Roman poet Virgil (for his teaching on language), Cicero (for his teaching on argument) and Aristotle (particularly his "Rhetoric" and "Poetics").
