In linguistics, a mass noun, uncountable noun, or non-count noun is a noun with the syntactic property that any quantity of it is treated as an undifferentiated unit, rather than as something with discrete elements. Non-count nouns are distinguished from count nouns.

examples:

weather: rain, snow, sleet, sunshine. feelings: anger, happiness, fear, courage. liquids: orange juice, tea, water. gasses: air, helium, argon. states of existence: childhood, sleep, sickness. ideas: advice, motivation, existentialism.

In linguistics, a count noun is a noun that can be modified by a numeral and that occurs in both singular and plural forms, and that can co-occur with quantificational determiners like every, each, several, etc.

examples:

dog, cat, animal, man, person. bottle, box, litre. coin, note, dollar. cup, plate, fork. table, chair, suitcase, bag.
