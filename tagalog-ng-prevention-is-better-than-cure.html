15. Explain ( Prevention is better than cure )​


Answer:

Prevention is better than cure is a widely accepted proverb, which outlines the importance of preventing a problem rather than treating it after it has occurred. It is crucial to adopt preventive measures to avoid the undesirable consequences that come in the wake of an unexpected situation. This proverb holds true in every aspect of life, be it health, finance, relationships, or knowledge. The following essay elucidates why prevention is better than cure.

To begin with, prevention and precautionary measures are essential to safeguard one's health. In this fast-paced era, people have become more prone to diseases due to the constant stress, unhealthy diet, and sedentary lifestyle. Therefore, it is crucial to follow a healthy lifestyle, maintain a balanced diet, and perform regular physical activities, which can ensure good health and prevent any health issues from arising. Regular medical check-ups can detect any anomalies in the body at an early stage and allow doctors to take remedial measures without delay. People who are mindful of their health and follow healthy habits are less likely to suffer from diseases, which illustrates the importance of prevention.

Moreover, prevention is more cost-effective than cure. For instance, consider the case of a person who does not follow a healthy lifestyle and indulges in unhealthy habits such as smoking and consuming alcohol. If such people fall sick due to their habits, the cost of their medical treatment would be significantly higher than that of a person who follows a healthy lifestyle. In addition, preventive measures such as buying health insurance, regularly saving money for emergencies, and investing in a pension plan, can prevent future financial losses. It is, therefore, evident that by investing in preventive measures, we can avoid incurring higher costs in the future.

Furthermore, preventive measures can help avoid accidents and save lives. For example, wearing seat belts while driving, following traffic rules, being cautious when handling electrical equipment, and wearing protective gear in hazardous workplaces can prevent accidents from occurring. Similarly, vaccines and timely medical interventions can prevent life-threatening conditions such as heart attacks, strokes, and cancers. By taking preventive measures, we can avoid accidents and illnesses, which can save us from pain, trauma, and even death.

In conclusion, the adage "prevention is better than cure" is a timeless truth that stresses the importance of prioritizing preventive measures over reactive measures. By following a healthy lifestyle, taking financial precautions, and adopting safety measures, we can prevent future problems and avoid incurring significant losses in the future. It is, therefore, essential to inculcate the value of prevention, which can go a long way in ensuring a happy, healthy, and safe life.
