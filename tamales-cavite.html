Answer:

2. Compare the mexican tamales and filipino tamales.​


Answer:

Mexican tamales, features a corn-based masa (dough) wrapped around a filling and steamed in a corn husk. They are traditionally filled with either chicken, pork, beef, cheese and/or beans.

Meanwhile Filipino tamales, is made of ground rice, coconut milk, and various toppings like chicken, ham, nuts, and egg that is perfect for breakfast or snack.


3. Explain the differences of mexican tamales and filipino tamales.​


Answer:

The differences between them is that Mexican Tamales are a traditional Mexican dish made with a corn based dough mixture that is filled with various meats or beans and cheese while Filipino Tamales are a traditional Kampangan delicacy that are made of rice flour, coconut milk, and peanut butter, topped with chicken and eggs and steamed to perfection.
