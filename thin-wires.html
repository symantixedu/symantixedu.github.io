2. which among these wires give the smallest resistance?a. short, thin wireb. short, thick wirec. long, thick wired. long, thin wire​


Answer:

Letter C

Explanation:

A thick wire (with a large cross-sectional area) has a lower resistance than a thin one* (with a small cross-sectional area). Resistance is inversely proportional to cross-sectional area: R ∝ 1/A. This means the larger the area, the less resistance.

24. Which component may you use that will make a bulb get dimmer?A. a short, thick wireB. a long, thin wireC. a short, thin wireD. a long, thick wire ​


Answer:

Explanation:

A piece of thin wire has higher resistance than a piece of thicker wire with the same length, which means that it's harder for electric current to move through it. Using a longer wire will also increase resistance.
