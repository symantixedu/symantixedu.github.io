2. What are the things that you're curious about?​


Answer:

Human brain.

Answer:

I was at the bus stop one afternoon. The place wasn’t crowded and there wasn’t much traffic at that time of the day. People are usually busy in their office, or taking an afternoon nap.

A man, probably in his thirties passed by the bus stop. He glanced at me and looked back straight, avoiding eye contact and walked away. I had this urge to leave my seat and go see him. He kept walking. This seemed unusual to me, without any reason. I tried remembering how he looked like. If there was any signs of emotion on his face.

Did he fight anyone in his office, and decided to take a walk? All I could remember was his blank face.

I wondered if he is returning to his home.

He would kiss his wife and pick his baby from the cradle into his arms and smile.

Or simply turn on the TV and fall asleep on the sofa.

His browsing history could be showing solo travelling tips and ‘dealing with financial crisis’ on recent closed tabs. And his wife checking the history in his absence, worried if she should talk to him about it.

Or he could be a bachelor and his astrologer told him to wear coral on ring finger.

He may be known as a cheerful colleague in his office but inside he might be poetic and empty.

I wondered if he was fighting with his inner conflicts or making peace with them. I could walk up to him and ask how he’s doing.

I kept staring at him as he walked away.

The peacefulness in the empty road gripped me with clarity.

I am curious about people around me, realizing that each random passerby is living a life as vivid and complex as my own—populated with their own ambitions, friends, routines, worries and inherited craziness. They are the heroes in their own epic.
