19. Tractor A can plow the field in 5 days, and tractor B can do the same in 7 days. Another tractor, tractor C can plow it in 3 days. How long it would take for the three tractors to plow the field if they are used all together?


Answer:

1 34/71 days

or

1 day, 11 hours, 29 minutes, and 34.65 seconds

Step-by-step explanation:

We can deal with work problems like distance problems with the use of the following formulas:

Rate = Work / Time

Time = Work / Rate

The work in this problem would be the number of fields,

Rate of A = 1 field / 5 days = 1/5 field per day

Rate of B = 1 field / 7 days = 1/7 field per day

Rate of C = 1 field / 3 days = 1/3 field per day

We then combine their rates,

Time = 1 / (1/5 + 1/7 + 1/3)

Time = 1 / (71/105)

Time = 105/71

Time = 1 34/71 days

Time = 1 day, 11 hours, 29 minutes, and 34.65 seconds

Therefore it would take approximately 1 34/71 days or 1 day, 11 hours, 29 minutes and 34.65 seconds.

Answer:

2.4 Days

Step-by-step explanation:

A farmer can plow a field in 4 days by using a tractor. A hired hand can plow the same field in 6 days by using a smaller tractor. How many days will be required for the plowing if they work together?

Let x be the number of days required if they work together.

[tex] \frac{1}{4} + \frac{1}{6} = \frac{1}{x} [/tex]

__________________

LCD is 12.

[tex] = \frac{12 \times 1}{4} + \frac{12 \times 1}{6} \\ = \frac{12}{4} + \frac{12}{6} \\ = 3 + 2 \\ = 5[/tex]

_______________________

[tex] \frac{5}{12} = \frac{1}{x} \\ \\ \frac{5}{12} x = 1 \\ \\ \frac{5x}{12} = 1 \\ \\ 5x = 1 \times 12 \\ \\ 5x = 12 \\ \\ \frac{5x}{5} = \frac{12}{5} \\ \\ x = \frac{12}{5} \\ \\ x = 2.4[/tex]

_____________

2.4 Days is required for the plowing if they work together.
