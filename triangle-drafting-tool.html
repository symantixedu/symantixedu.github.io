15. • Explain the function of the following drafting tools: • Explain how to use it properly- T-square - Compass - Erasing shield - Protractor - Triangles ​


Answer:

T-square: A T-square is a drafting tool typically used to draw perpendicular lines and to help maintain straight, accurate lines while drawing. It is a flat rectangular board with one side having a perpendicular bar affixed to it, which is used as a straightedge. To use a T-square, one end of the board is placed against the edge of the drawing surface while the perpendicular bar is used to guide the pencil as you draw.

Compass: A compass is a drafting tool used to draw shapes and circles to create precise geometry. The compass consists of two parts: a pointed metal end which is used to draw the circle, and a pair of adjustable arms connected to the pointed end. The arms can be adjusted to set the desired diameter for the circle. To use a compass, the pointed end is placed on the paper at the desired center point and the arms are adjusted to the desired radius for the circle.

Erasing Shield: An erasing shield is a drafting tool used to protect specific areas of a drawing from erasure. The shield is a semi-transparent sheet with a cutout that has been designed to protect areas of the drawing while erasing the area surrounding it. To use an erasing shield, place the shield over the area you wish to protect, and then erase the desired area.

Protractor: A protractor is a drafting tool used in geometry to measure and draw angles. It consists of a flat, semi-circular base with an adjustable arm that has degrees marked along its circumference. To use a protractor, place the base of the protractor over the vertex of the angle and the arm along one of the sides of the angle. Then, the angle can be measured and marked according to the degree markings on the protractor.

Triangles: Triangles are a drafting tool used to draw precise geometric shapes. Typically, triangles come in two variations: equilateral and right-angle. Equilateral triangles are used to draw 60-degree angles, while right-angle triangles are used to draw 90-degree angles. To use triangles, place the desired triangle over the area you wish to draw, then use a pencil or other drawing implement to trace along the edges of the triangle to construct your desired shape.
