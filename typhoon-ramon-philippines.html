6. Ano ang Talambuhay ni Ramon Magsaysay the seventh President of the Philippines ?​


Answer:

Si Ramon Magsaysay ay ang ika-7 Pangulo ng Pilipinas na kilala bilang "the Guy" o "Presidente ng Masang Pilipino". Siya ay ipinanganak sa Iba, Zambales noong Agosto 31, 1907 at pangalawang anak ni Exequiel Magsaysay at Perfecta Del Fierro. Bilang pangulo, binuksan niya ang pintuan sa mga mamamayang Pilipino kung saan sila ay binigyan ng pagkakataong lumapit sa kanya nang direkta at bigyan sila ng higit na kalayaan upang ipahayag ang kanilang sakit at pagdurusa. Magpahanggang ngayon, ang kanyang pamumuno at kabutihan ay ang mga katangiang hinahanap ng mga Pilipino para sa isang lider.

Nag-aral si Magsaysay sa Unibersidad ng Pilipinas at kumuha ng enhinyerang mekanikal bagamat sa Colegio de Jose Rizal niya natapos ang Antas sa Komersyo. Sa kanyang kapanahunan, karamihan sa mga pinuno sa pulitika ay kamag-anak ng Kastila. Si Magsaysay lamang ang may lahing Malay, katulad ng pangkaraniwang Pilipino.

Si Magsaysay ay nagsimula bilang isang mekaniko sa Try-Tran Bus Company at sa kalaunan ay naging bilang branch manager. Naging tanyag siya bilang isang mahusay na lider ng gerilya sa Ikalawang Digmaang Pandaigdig at pinangalanan bilang gobernador militar ng Iba, Zambales ni General Douglas McArthur nang palayain ng Estados Unidos ang Pilipinas.

12. what is the significance of ramon valera's creation of "terno" in philippines culture and identity?​


Answer:

Valera designed the inaugural gowns of all the first ladies from Aurora Quezon to Imelda Marcos, except for Evangelina Macapagal, although he did make the debutante gown of her daughter, President Gloria Arroyo. One of his most publicized designs was a gown worn by Imelda Marcos on a state visit to Iran.Oct 26, 2020

Explanation:

Valera's early innovation was modernizing the terno in 1939. He revived the traje de mestiza (what we know as the Maria Clara), traditionally a four-piece ensemble consisting of a blouse, skirt, overskirt, and scarf. He exaggerated the bell sleeves, which were copied by many. Then the terno became a one-piece silhouette, fastened with a zipper instead of hooks. He shocked the public when he removed the panuelo or scarf which covered the woman's bosom.

16. 1.what was the purpose of jose maceda and ramon santos for emphasizing Philippines music in their composition?2.how will their compositions affect our awareness?​


Answer:

Aki Onda (AO): Today I’m going to talk about José Maceda, who was born a hundred years ago in Manila in 1917. He was a highly skilled pianist turned Filipino ethnomusicologist turned composer. Maceda’s work has a very particular cultural and historical significance. As a composer, Maceda left many one-of-a-kind works combining both fieldworks on Filipino musicality and his knowledge of European avant-garde music. His music fused cutting-edge compositional techniques such as spatialization, attention to timbre, and musique concrète with traditional Asian instruments, rhythms, and structures. His fieldwork as an ethnomusicologist rigorously documented South East and East Asian musical practices and folkways. He was deeply fascinated by so-called indigenous music, or village music, from pre-historical times – music that had been performed in people’s lives as a ceremony or ritual for thousands of years. However, that music was disappearing during his lifetime because of global modernization and industrialization of society.

The collection you mentioned consists of several dance masterpieces created by Ramon Obusan, a renowned Filipino choreographer and folklorist. These dances showcase the diverse cultural traditions of the Philippines and other countries in the ASEAN region.

The first four series, "Unpublished Dances of the Philippines," Series I-IV, feature traditional dances from different regions of the Philippines that have not been previously published or performed. "Water, Fire and Life" and "Philippine Dances and Music-A Celebration of Life" are two more performances that showcase the rich cultural heritage of the Philippines through music and dance. "Saludo sa Sentenyal" is a dance piece that pays tribute to the 100th anniversary of the Philippine Revolution.

"Glimpses of ASEAN, Dances and Music of the ASEAN Member Countries" is a performance that highlights the cultural diversity of the Southeast Asian region by showcasing traditional dances and music from the different ASEAN member countries. Lastly, "Saplot (Ramon Obusan Folkloric Group): Philippines Costumes in Dance" is a performance that showcases the traditional clothing and costumes of the Philippines through dance.

Overall, Ramon Obusan's collection of dance masterpieces is a celebration of the rich cultural heritage of the Philippines and the ASEAN region as a whole, providing a glimpse into the traditions, music, and dances that have been passed down through generations.
