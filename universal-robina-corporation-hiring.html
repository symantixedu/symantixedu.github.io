23. The ration of teacher to student to students in a university is 1:40. If the university would hire 5 more teacher,the ratio would become 1:35.how many students are there​


Answer:

I'm working because I'm poor

Let's assume that there are initially 'x' number of students in the university, and 't' number of teachers are hired.

According to the problem, the ratio of teacher to student is 1:40. Therefore, initially, the university has:

t = x/40
teachers.

If the university would hire 5 more teachers, the ratio would become 1:35. Therefore, the university would then have:

t + 5 = x/35
teachers.

Equating both equations:

t = t + 5/40 - 5/35
t = x/40
x/40 = x/35 - 5/35
Simplifying the equation:

7x = 2000
Therefore, the total number of students in the university is:

x = 2000/7
x ≈ 285.71
Rounding off, there are approximately 286 students in the university
