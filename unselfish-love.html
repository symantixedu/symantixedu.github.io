Unselfish love is not about giving up what you want; it is about accepting and encouraging your partner's desires and dreams as much as they do yours. It is NOT “selfish” to pursue the things you want in life.

remember answering a question “Can selfishness be good for our society?” several times throughout past year and I’ve come to conclusion that selfishness is one thing that is keeping people sane. What is good about selfishness is that it makes every single one of us. Being selfless is something rarely done by us and it’s honestly understandable.

First of all, we all think that everything is “around” us, we are in the center and everyone else is somewhere there just for us. We can only see the world with our own eyes and therefore we can’t believe that other people are as important as we are.

Secondly, most of our actions are done for our own benefit. Not only that most of what we do we do for ourselves and for our own well-being but also we do things to other people to take something from it. For example, when we help a homeless person with a dollar. Everyone has done that at least once and it made us feel like we did somethomg good, like we were generous. That’s the whole point, we did it for ourselves. We might not get anythig back except for a simple “thank you” but we made ourselves feel like we did something good and it means for us that life is going to give it back, like a boomerang.

But if we did not care about ourselves what would we even care about? We wouldn’t think about other people because they wouldn’t need our help, they don’t care about themselves. It’s baely possible that we would always be helping each other, but it might as well happen. So there are two ways it can go: people will be selfless and won’t even try to do anything productive in this life because why would they want to do it OR everyone will be helping everyone.

I’ll start with the first one, chaotic. The world will end. It will end really fast because we wouldn’t need anything, wouldn’t care enough to do anything. Selfishness makes us care about our health, life, well-being, but it all will be gone and soon enough we will die. Most of us will not care about anything or anyone else. Maybe there will be some people left who just really genuinely want to save the world and do something but at the end they will give up too.

Now, the second one. It’s really not that likely to happen but if it happened, we would all be in danger. Right now it works like this: one person has their own back and keeps themselves going, so everyone is helping themselves and life goes on and everything is fine. But if we cared about other people more than about ourselves, we probably wouldn’t be able to choose who to care about more or who to help, so we would be trying to help everybody and it might turn into a chaos just as well as the first one. No one would go to doctor because they wouldn’t think about themselves, so none of us will know what to do. It’s possible though that we would mostly help old people, children and animals because they are considered as the ones that need the most help. A lot of people would die because they wouldn’t tell about they problems and nobody will help them. It may go well and people are going to build a new system of society which will be some sort of altruistic democracy and somehow it will keep us alive, but it may end bad and the process of our disappearing would take a little longer but it will end up the same.

So, in general, we need to be selfish to keep the society we already built progressing, but if we weren’t selfish there wouldn’t be a great chance that we will still be alive in a couple of centuries.
