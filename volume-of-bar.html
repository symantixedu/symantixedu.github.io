21. A 100.0-g bar of gold is heated from 25 °C to 50 °C during which it absorbs 322 J of heat. Assume the volume of the gold bar remains constant.


Answer:

We can use the heat equation,

Q = mcΔT

Where Q is the amount of energy transferred (J), m is the mass of the substance (kg), c is the specific heat (J g⁻¹ °C⁻¹) and ΔT is the temperature difference (°C).

Q = 322 J

m = 100.0 g

c = ?

ΔT = (50 - 25) °C = 25 °C

By applying the formula,

  322 J = 100.0 g x c x 25 °C

         c = 322 J / (100.0 g x 25 °C)

         c = 0.1288 J g⁻¹ °C⁻¹

Hence, the specific heat of gold is 0.1288 J g⁻¹ °C⁻¹.

24. the volume of rectangular bar is 640 cubic units. the width is 3 units more than the height and the length is 1 unit more than three times the height. Find the dimensions of the bar


These are the dimensions: 

H = 5 W = 8 L = 16

Solution

Let V be the volume of the rectangular bars and W, L, and H be each bar's width, length, and height, respectively.

Here:

V = 640 

W = H + 3 

L = 3 H + 1 

V = L x W x H

Define V:

640 = (3 H + 1) (H + 3)(H) 

or 640 = (3 H ^2 + 9 H + 3)(H) 

or 0 = 3 H^3 + 10 H ^2 + 3 H - 640

or 0 = (H-5)(3H^2 + 25 H + 128)

Consequently,

H-5 = 0 and 3 H2 + 25 H + 128 = 0

H = 5-0 or H = -25 + √-911/6

H = 5 or H = -25 - √-911/6

However, computing with -√911 lacks a viable answer.

Consequently, the actual solution is H = 5. Therefore, H = 5 W = 5 + 3 = 8 L = 3(5) + 1 = 16

The dimensions are H = 5 W = 8 L = 16

Cuboid significance = Length x Width x Height Cubic units.

The volume of a cuboid is the entire amount of space it occupies in three dimensions. A cuboid has six rectangular faces and is a three-dimensional structure. These six cuboid faces occur as two sets of three parallel faces. The volume is, therefore, a measurement based on the dimensions of these faces, such as length, width, and height. It is measured in units of volume. The surface area of a cube is the total area its rectangular faces cover.

Because the faces of a cuboid are entirely rectangular, the term "Solid Rectangle" is also known as a cuboid. This is because, in a rectangular cuboid, all angles are at right angles, and all faces are equal.
