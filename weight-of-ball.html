WHAT IS VOLLEYBALL?

A volleyball is a ball used to play indoor volleyball, beach volleyball, or other less common variations of the sport. Volleyballs are round and traditionally consist of eighteen nearly rectangular panels of synthetic or genuine leather, arranged in six identical sections of three panels each, wrapped around a bladder. A valve permits the internal air pressure to be adjusted. 28. Direct Linear VariationThe weight of the ball varies directly with the cube of its radius. A ball with the radius of 4 inches weighs 3.6 pounds. Find the weight of the ball with the radius of 6 inches.​


Step-by-step explanation:

If the weight of a ball varies directly with the cube of its radius, then we can write the relationship between them as w = kr^3, where w is the weight, r is the radius, and k is a constant of proportionality.

From the given information, we know that when r = 4 inches, w = 3.6 pounds. So, we can find k by plugging in these values:

3.6 = k * 4^3

Solving for k:

k = 3.6 / 4^3 = 3.6 / 64 = 9/16

Now that we know k, we can find the weight of the ball with the radius of 6 inches:

w = k * 6^3 = 9/16 * 6^3 = 9/16 * 216 = 189/16 pounds = 11.8125 pounds

Answer:

32.4 pounds

Step-by-step explanation:

Direct Linear Variation refers to a mathematical relationship between two variables, such that a change in one variable results in a proportional change in the other variable.

In this problem, the weight of a ball varies directly with the cube of its radius. This means that the ratio of the weight of the ball to its radius cubed is a constant. We can use this relationship to find the weight of a ball with a different radius.

Given that a ball with a radius of 4 inches weighs 3.6 pounds, we can set up an equation to solve for the constant of variation:

3.6 / (4^3) = k

where k is the constant of variation.

Next, we can use the equation to find the weight of the ball with a radius of 6 inches:

Weight = k * (6^3) = k * 216

Substituting the value of k from the first equation:

Weight = (3.6 / (4^3)) * (6^3) = 3.6 * (6^3) / (4^3) = 3.6 * 216 / 64 = 32.4 pounds.
