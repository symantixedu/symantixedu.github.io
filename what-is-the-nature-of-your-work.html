28. if you are a parent, is it natural that when you have a child, the child is taken directly from you and does not beg and is always with your in-laws even if you are not working. as a parent what would you say? ​


Answer:

As a parent, I would say that it is natural to want to have a close and loving relationship with your child, and to feel hurt and angry if someone tries to interfere with that. I would also say that it is not healthy or fair for anyone to take your child away from you without your consent or involvement, unless there is a serious risk of harm to the child.

According to some sources, parental alienation is when one parent discredits the other parent to a child or children they share, using strategies such as brainwashing, alienating, or programming. This can damage the parent-child relationship and cause the child to become estranged from the other parent. Some experts believe that parental alienation can have negative effects on the child's mental health and well-being.

If you are experiencing parental alienation, you may want to seek professional help from a therapist, a lawyer, or a mediator who can help you cope with your emotions, protect your rights, and restore your bond with your child. You may also want to communicate with your in-laws in a respectful and assertive way, setting clear boundaries and expectations about your role and involvement in your child's life.
