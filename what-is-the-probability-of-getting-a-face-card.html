15. Consider two decks of cards. What is the probability of getting a heart card, then a black face card? (answer in fraction pls)


Answer:

Let A = heart cards

Let B = black face cards

P(A) = 13/52 (since there are 13 heart cards out of the standard deck of cards.)

≈ ¼

≈ 25%

P(B) = 6/52 (since there are 6 black face cards out of the standard deck of cards.)

≈ 3/26

≈ 12%

If necessary:

P(A union B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A intersection B)

P(A union B) = ¼ + 3/26 - 0 (since you cannot find a heart card that is colored black and has a face)

P(A union B) = 19/52

≈ 37%
