23. what makes one drawing art and the other not art?​


Answer:

The classification of a drawing as "art" or "not art" is a subjective matter and can vary from person to person. Generally, a drawing is considered art if it involves creativity, imagination, and skill in its creation. Artistic drawings can also express emotions, ideas, or convey a message to the viewer. However, the level of creativity, skill, and message conveyed can vary widely depending on the individual artist and the style or genre of the drawing. Ultimately, the distinction between art and not art is often determined by the viewer's personal interpretation and perception of the drawing.
