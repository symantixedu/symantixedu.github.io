What is the distance between two parallel chords of length 12cm and 15cm in a circle of radius 24cm?

Radius of the circle, with centre at O = 24 cm.

Length of chord AB = 12 cm. Mid point is E.

Length of chord CD = 15 cm. Mid point is F.

Case 1. When AB and CD are on the same side of O.

OE = [24^2 - 6^2]^0.5 = [30*18}^0.5 = 23.24 cm.

OF = [24^2 - 7.5^2]^0.5 = [31.5*16.5}^0.5 = 22.80 cm.

The distance between the chords Ab and CD = 23.24–22.80 = 0.44 cm

Case 1. When AB and CD are on the opposite sides of O.

OE = [24^2 - 6^2]^0.5 = [30*18}^0.5 = 23.24 cm.

OF = [24^2 - 7.5^2]^0.5 = [31.5*16.5}^0.5 = 22.80 cm.

The distance between the chords AB and CD = 23.24+22.80 = 46.04 cm

27. THE DIAMETER OF A CIRCLE IS 10 CM LONG AND A CHORD PARALLEL TO IT 6CM LONG.FIND THE DISTANCE BETWEEN THE CHORD AND THE CENTER OF THE CIRCLE


✒️CIRCLES

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{PROBLEM}:} [/tex]

The diameter of a circle is 10 cm long and a chord parallel to it is 6 cm long. Find the distance between the chord and the center of the circle.

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \Large \: \rm{4 \: centimeters} [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

Illustration:Draw a circle then label the given: AB is the diameter and CD is a chord parallel to it.

Draw another diameter labeled EF which will bisect CD that intersects at H, making G the center of the circle.

Let x be the distance between the chord and the center of the circle. Measuring:CH and DH measures 3cm since chord CD measuring 6cm is bisected by diameter EF intersecting at H.

Since 10cm is the diameter, then the radius is 5cm. Therefore, GE and G.F measures 5cm.

The sum of HF and GH is the radius. Thus, HF is the difference of the radius and GH, which is (5 - x).Solve for x:

» Solve for x using the intersecting chords theorem. By the given illustration, the theorem suggests that:

[tex] EH \cdot HF = CH \cdot DH [/tex]

[tex] (GE + GH) \cdot HF = CH \cdot DH [/tex]

[tex] (5 + x)(5 - x) = 3(3) [/tex]

[tex] 25 - x^2 = 9 [/tex]

[tex] x^2 = 25 - 9 [/tex]

[tex] x^2 = 16 [/tex]

[tex] \sqrt{x^2} = \sqrt{16} [/tex]

[tex] x = 4 [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] The distance between the chord and the center of the circle is 4cm
